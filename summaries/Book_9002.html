BACKGROUND

Yayasan Jalin Kemitraan Nusantara (YJKN), also known as OUCRU ID, comprises three research groups and five supporting groups, with a staff of about 120 national staff, including 4 expatriate Oxford staff, located at the Jakarta office and study sites. In addition to our main host institutions above, we also collaborate closely with the broader Oxford Tropical Network, and numerous other international and local partners.

Yayasan Jalin Kemitraan Nusantara (YJKN) as a legal institution with yayasan (foundation) type,have a compliance with Indonesian regulations governing foundations and donor funding accountability, YJKN is required to conduct an independent annual financial audit. As a recipient of donor funding, YJKN seeks to ensure transparency, accountability, and adherence to applicable financial and tax reporting standards.

YJKN invites qualified public accounting firms (Kantor Akuntan Publik) to submit proposals for the provision of annual financial audit services. The objective of this engagement is to enable the appointed auditor to express an independent opinion on whether YJKN’s financial statements present fairly, in all material respects, the financial position, results of operations, and cash flows for the fiscal year, in accordance with the following applicable Indonesian Financial Accounting Standards for Non-Profit Entities:
- Financial Accounting Standards for Private Entities ( SAK-EP), which interprets PSAK 1 for nonprofit entities.
- Interpretation of financial Accounting Standards (ISAK-35)
- Tax Law /KUHP (e.g., article 4(2), PPh 21, PPh 23, VAT, annual SPT tahunan filling.
- Tax-related Minister of Finance Regulation (PMK), (e.q., PMK 168/2023, PMK 141/2025, PMK 37/2025)
- Government regulation PP No. 29/2020, PPh related to COVID when applicable

YJKN intends to select a Service Provider based on the lowest priced technically acceptable quotation received for the award of a Purchase Order. Lowest Price Technically Acceptable is defined as the lowest-priced offer that meets or exceeds the minimum requirements in the Statement of Work.

ELIGIBILITY

To be considered for an award, Offerors must include all of the following information in their quotations:

• Ability to meet or exceed the required annual audit of financials services (services outlined in Annex A, Statement of Work).
• Completed Proposal/Quotation Submission and Provider Qualification forms, in Annex B.

CONTENT AND FORMAT FOR QUOTATIONS

Offerors may submit and format their quotations using the format indicated (the offerors can create and propose their own cost structure). Offerors may request assistance to complete these forms from the person indicated at the beginning of this Request for Proposal (RFP).

• Cover letter (one page), signed by an authorized representative of your organization.
• Itemized price quotation using the Pricing Table attached to this solicitation.

EVALUATION

The award will be made based on the lowest evaluated price of proposals meeting or exceeding the acceptability standards for non-cost factors as stated in Annex A.

The provider has the liberty to prepare and propose their cost structure.

The evaluation criteria shall be as follows:

Evaluation Criteria Weights (point)
Proposed methodology and audit plan 35
Fee structure (including any out-of-pocket expenses) 35
Lead time of the service 20
Organisational Capacity & Key personnel qualification 10
Total Score 100

YJKN reserves the right to reject any or all offers, or to make multiple awards if such action is in the best interests of YJKN. YJKN may waive informalities and minor irregularities in offers received, should such actions be in the best interests of YJKN. YJKN reserves the right to purchase only a portion of the services offered by a vendor and to purchase services from multiple vendors.

Thank you for your participation. Should you have any questions, kindly contact the person specified at the beginning of the Request for Proposal.

ANNEX A

  • STATEMENT OF WORK
  • General Requirements

General Requirements
The service provider shall perform all required Financial Audit for YJKN, including – but not limited to – the following:

- Audit Standards Compliance: Conduct the audit in accordance with:
o Indonesian Standards on Auditing (Standar Audit Indonesia – SAI).
o Indonesian Public Accountant Professional Standards (Standar Profesional Akuntan Publik – SPAP).
o Indonesian Standards on Auditing (Standar Profesional Akuntan – SPA).
o Indonesian Foundation Law No. 28/2004 (Undang-Undang Yayasan).

- Financial Statement Examination:
o Review YJKN’s general ledger, trial balance, supporting schedules, and all material transactions.
o Assess internal controls over financial reporting, fundraising, and disbursement of donor funds.

- Compliance Testing:
o Verify compliance with the Foundation’s Deed (Akta Pendirian), Articles of Association (Anggaran Dasar), and amendments.
o Ensure proper use of donor funds and grants, in line with donor agreements and government reporting obligations.
o Assessment of internal controls over fundraising and fund disbursement.
o Test allowability, reasonableness, and allocability of costs, including program expenses, payroll, and overhead.

- Tax Review:
o Verify compliance with Indonesian tax laws (PPh 21, PPh 23, PPh Badan, VAT) and annual SPT submission.
o Check alignment with relevant Ministry of Finance regulations (e.g., PMK 168/2023, PMK 141/2025, PMK 37/2025) and PP No. 29/2020.
o Advising on compliance with relevant accounting and tax regulations.

  • STATEMENT OF WORK
  • Deliverables

Deliverables:
- Independent Auditor’s Report with opinion on the fairness of the financial statements.
- Management Letter with findings, recommendations for internal control improvements, and any non-compliance observations.
- Final Audited Financial Statements (Balance Sheet, Statement of Comprehensive Income, Statement of Changes in Net Assets, Cash Flow Statement, and Notes).

ANNEX A

  • STATEMENT OF WORK
  • Required Qualifications

Required Qualifications
- Auditor must be a registered and licensed Kantor Akuntan Publik (KAP) in Indonesia.
- Proven experience auditing Yayasan, NGOs, or other nonprofit entities with global donor-funded programs.
- Demonstrated familiarity with SAK-EP, ISAK 35, and Indonesian tax compliance for nonprofit organizations.
- Provide evidence of an independent quality control system and adherence to Indonesian Standards on Quality Management (ISQM
1).
- Assign a qualified audit team with at least one CPA (Akuntan Publik Bersertifikat).

ANNEX A

  • STATEMENT OF WORK
  • Confidentiality & Data Protection

Confidentiality & Data Protection
Both parties must comply with Indonesian data privacy laws (PDPA/UU PDP) and keep information confidential unless disclosure is mandated by regulators (e.g., OJK for public Foundations).
