DESIRE — The Starting Point
Desire is defined as the initiating impulse of achievement distinct from mere wishing or hoping; it must be definite, intense, and sustained to become the dominant motivating force in a person's life. Desire, when fixed and enforced by persistence, becomes the raw material through which plans and action produce tangible results.
- Desire must be definite and specific, not vague wishing.
- Intensity converts a wish into a consuming obsession that directs effort.
- Sustained desire supports long-term persistence and planning.
Edwin C. Barnes and Edison
The Barnes anecdote illustrates that arriving with a fixed, definite goal and identifying mentally with its attainment can reorder behavior and opportunity in favor of success. Barnes accepted menial work while mentally claiming the position of Edison’s partner and persisted relentlessly until circumstances aligned with his declared purpose.
- Mental identification with the goal preceded its physical attainment.
- Humble beginnings accepted for strategic access to the desired circle.
- Persistence over years without visible reward proved decisive.
Definite Purpose and Persistence
Success required Barnes to choose a definite purpose and to back that purpose with willpower, energy, and unremitting effort until the goal manifested; planning and patience were integrated with single-mindedness. His story emphasizes that defining the amount, the reward, and the exchange of value are prerequisites to sustained action that leads to fulfillment.
- Choose one clear objective and align all actions to it.
- Back plans with continuous effort and willingness to start small.
- Persistence must override short-term discouragement and lack of external validation.
Burn the Ships — Eliminate Retreat
The metaphor of burning ships conveys the psychological effect of removing retreat options so that necessity enforces commitment; when there is no permitted retreat the mind generates resourcefulness and determination required to win. Leaders and successful individuals often adopt this all-or-nothing stance mentally to create urgency and uncompromising resolve.
- Eliminate fallback plans that encourage half-hearted effort.
- Force full commitment to the chosen objective to mobilize creativity.
- Urgency produced by no-retreat situations intensifies effort and attention.
Chicago Fire and Marshal Field
After the Chicago Fire, Marshall Field’s decision to rebuild on the burned site exemplifies how resolute purpose converts catastrophe into opportunity; the choice to remain and recreate required vision and willingness to act against the crowd. - Rebuilding on the original site signaled confidence and refusal to be displaced.

AUTOSUGGESTION
AUTOSUGGESTION is defined as self-suggestion — the set of self-administered stimuli and repeated impressions that reach the mind through the five senses and serve as the communication channel between conscious thought and the seat of subconscious action.
Its functional role in the system is to carry emotionally charged, repeated instructions into the subconscious so that they become habitual drives that guide decisions, perception, and behavior.
Definition & Scope
Autosuggestion embraces every consciously repeated phrase, written statement, imagined scene, or sensory-act that a person voluntarily exposes to the mind and thereby permits to be recorded by the subconscious; it excludes rare spontaneous flashes of insight that bypass repetition.
Because autosuggestion operates through the senses and through repetition, control of sensory and repetitive input gives the practitioner practical mastery over which thought-impulses gain the power to shape action.
Five Senses & Gatekeeping
The five senses are emphasized as literal gateways by which autosuggestion transmits material to the subconscious, and the conscious faculty functions as the gatekeeper with the ability to admit or reject impressions; exercising this gatekeeping is the practical difference between intentional mental planting and passive absorption of social or cultural weeds.
Mastery of autosuggestion therefore begins with deliberate selection and repeat exposure to sight, sound, tactile acts, and vocalization that reinforce the chosen aim.
Conscious vs Subconscious Roles
The conscious mind acts as an outer guard that may accept or refuse sensory impressions and verbalized thoughts destined for the subconscious, and when the conscious faculty is neglected the subconscious will accept habitual impressions indiscriminately, producing behaviors that reflect the dominant, often unexamined, mental environment.
Thus deliberate use of conscious attention — choosing what to read, say aloud, and visualize — is the practical lever for programming the deeper mind.
Garden Metaphor — Planting & Weeding
The subconscious is likened to a fertile garden that, left unattended, will yield whatever seeds are sown; this metaphor prescribes purposeful sowing of constructive autosuggestions and systematic weeding of fear, envy, doubt, and other destructive thought-forms by active replacement with positive statements and visualized outcomes.
Persistent cultivation transforms the field of the mind from a place of random growth into an engineered plot that yields the desired habits and inclinations.
Emotion as the Essential Multiplier
The chapter makes the decisive point that mere verbal repetition is ineffective unless the repeated thought is thoroughly mixed with feeling, and that FAITH is the most powerful emotional multiplier for autosuggestion because emotion supplies the vitality and urgency that cause the subconscious to register and act upon the instruction.
Therefore each aloud repetition must be performed with inner conviction, sensory detail, and physiological engagement to be effective.
The Price of Ability — Persistence
The ability to influence the subconscious carries a non-negotiable price: long-term, sustained persistence in daily practice of focused repetition, vocalization, and concentrated visualization, because the subconscious typically requires repeated, emotionally charged impressions before replacing old patterns with new directives.
No shortcut or single clever act substitutes for the steady investment of time and feeling required to reconfigure deep mental habits.
Concentration & Visualization Practice
Actionable concentration practice is prescribed: fix on an exact monetary amount or specific objective, close the eyes, and visualize the physical details of possession until the image becomes vivid and accompanied by feeling, thereby compelling the subconscious to recognize the end-state as an expectancy rather than a speculative idea.
Combined with autosuggestion and faith, concentrated visualization functions as the engine that produces inspired, practical plans.
See, Feel, Demand
The practical instruction is to SEE yourself already in possession of the object of desire, FEEL the conviction and sensory reality of that possession, and mentally DEMAND that the subconscious produce the plan or steps needed to obtain it; this posture of expectancy conditions perception to notice opportunities and to accept inspiration as actionable guidance.
Adopt an "as-if" stance in thought and demeanor so the subconscious accepts the instruction as true and seeks means to fulfill it.
Begin Before the Plan Appears
Do not delay visualization or autosuggestion until a fully formulated, rational plan exists; instead begin the mental process and expect the subconscious and the sixth sense to produce practicable plans and impressions in time, because reason alone may be slow or limited while a primed subconscious often generates creative routes to attainment.
Act on small early steps while remaining prepared to implement larger inspired plans as they arrive.
Imagination & Sixth Sense
Imagination is described as the laboratory of the subconscious where new combinations and practical plans are synthesized, and when imagination is properly primed by emotion and repetition, inspiration often arrives as a sudden "sixth sense" flash which the practitioner should treat as a direct communication from Infinite Intelligence and act upon without hesitation.
Honor intuitive prompts, record them immediately, and evaluate for practical implementation.

Promptness is emphasized: when a plan or hunch arrives, act immediately because many inspirations are transient and vanish if not captured and tested; timely execution converts fleeting insight into material progress and signals the subconscious that its suggestions lead to effective outcomes, thereby reinforcing future inspired guidance.
Small, immediate actions validate the signal and prime larger sequences of progress.

Common Failures & Remedies
Frequent reasons for failure include unemotional repetition, sporadic practice, contradictory daily influences, and neglecting the conscious gatekeeper; remedies are precise: increase feeling during recitation, schedule strict daily repetition, eliminate negative inputs from the environment, and replace contrary statements immediately upon recognition.
Systematic repair of these failures restores the effective operation of autosuggestion.
Ethical Use & Neutrality of Law
Because the law of autosuggestion is neutral, it will manifest negative as readily as positive instructions; therefore ethical orientation matters — directing autosuggestion toward goals that do not harm others and that align with truth and justice both protects the practitioner and ensures durable, socially constructive outcomes.

The Sixth Sense — Door to the Temple of Wisdom
The Sixth Sense is presented as the culminating faculty of the Think and Grow Rich system and is characterized as the portion of the subconscious that functions as the receiving set for ideas, hunches, and inspirations.
It is described as the means by which Infinite Intelligence may communicate with an individual without deliberate effort from that individual.
The chapter frames the Sixth Sense as the apex of a sequential curriculum that requires prior mastery of the twelve preceding principles before reliable Sixth Sense functioning can be expected.
It is positioned not as a magical shortcut but as the distillation of practiced mental discipline, emotional balance, and persistent application that together produce a refined receptive capacity.
The faculty is thus both operational and moral, and its development is tied to habits of thought, ethical steadiness, and sustained interior work.
Nature and Ineffability of the Sixth Sense
The Sixth Sense is said to defy easy description because it is essentially experiential and not primarily conceptual, making verbal exposition inadequate for those who lack the internal reference points built by the prior practices.
Attempts to describe it to an unprepared mind will commonly fail because the receiver lacks the comparative experience needed to recognize the phenomenon.
Consequently the author emphasizes experience and practice over theorizing, urging the student to adopt the practical methods of the book and judge the results empirically.
The Sixth Sense therefore functions as a lived capacity rather than an article of belief, and its claims are set up to be tested in lived practice rather than accepted on authority.
Prerequisite Mastery: Stage-Gated Development
Development of the Sixth Sense is explicitly conditional upon having mastered the other principles such as desire, faith, autosuggestion, imagination, and persistence, because those earlier steps build the internal architecture that the Sixth Sense requires to function.
The book presents this as a stage-gated process where each earlier principle develops habits, feelings, and dispositions that progressively refine the subconscious and the imaginative faculties.
Without the grounding provided by the earlier steps the Sixth Sense is unlikely to produce reliable or useful impressions, and so the text sternly warns against attempting to shortcut the curriculum.
Students are therefore advised to view the Sixth Sense as the capstone in a long apprenticeship rather than as a first-line method for quick success.
Creative Imagination as Receiving Set
The Sixth Sense is closely identified with the Creative Imagination when that faculty is trained to receive impulses beyond normal conscious derivation, whether those impulses originate in Infinite Intelligence, the subconscious, or energized thoughts from other minds.
When trained, Creative Imagination supplies sudden ideas, strategies, and warnings that appear to arrive whole and ready for action, often with an accompanying sense of certainty.
This faculty is trained through repeated practice in visualization, controlled reverie, and the habit of quiet attentiveness, which increase signal-to-noise ratio in the mind's reception operations.
The disciplined imaginative practice thus functions as a tuning procedure for the Sixth Sense.
How the Sixth Sense Communicates
Communication via the Sixth Sense is often instantaneous and tends to present itself as a feeling of conviction, a vivid mental image, or a hunch that lacks a step-by-step logical derivation.
These communications commonly manifest as warnings of imminent danger or as timely notices of opportunity, and when acted upon they frequently produce practical advantages.
The faculty therefore functions as a sentinel and an early-warning system that supplements ordinary calculation and sensory input, providing additional data points for decision-making.
Because these impressions are typically non-rational in form, one must learn to test them through disciplined action and verification rather than accept them blindly.

Because its mechanisms are subtle and not yet precisely mapped, the outcomes of the Sixth Sense can seem miraculous to uninformed observers even though they are the product of natural processes.
This framing invites a methodological attitude: practice, observe, measure outcomes, and refine technique rather than rely on superstition or myth.

Guard the mind against negative influences, cultivate dominating constructive thoughts, and apply autosuggestion, imagination, faith, and Master Mind methods to accelerate development.
