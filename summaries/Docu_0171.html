Mapping D365 GL → SPT 1771
1. Purpose & audience
This manual converts Microsoft Dynamics 365 (D365) Main Accounts (GL) into a complete, auditable SPT 1771 submission package for Indonesia. Audience: junior accountants, tax preparers, tax managers, finance staff, and technical implementers responsible for mapping, reconciliation, validation, automation (PQ/VBA), submission, and audit response. Objective: produce DJP-ready CSVs, structured supporting schedules, validated reconciliation, and a defensible audit trail.

Narrative
A junior accountant, newly assigned to annual SPT 1771 preparation, logs into D365 with the CFO's instruction “We need a clean, repeatable process we can show auditors.” The accountant uses this manual as a practical map: extract the Trial Balance, apply the master mapping rules, document each judgment (why an item was split, why a deduction was claimed), and create CSVs DJP will accept. The manual is intentionally procedural: it explains not only what to do but why each step matters for audit defensibility. For a small manufacturing entity the manual is used to produce the first filing; the same structure scales to larger groups by adding entity-specific mapping rows and TP documentation.

Additional examples
- Example org A (small trading): The preparer uses a subset of the workbook: MasterMapping, TB_Imported, Reconciliation, AttachmentsIndex. The CFO signs the Reconciliation PDF and stores RUN zip on SharePoint.
- Example org B (multi-entity): Use the same template per legal entity and consolidate entity-level lampiran rows into the group-level reconciliation. Each entity maintains its own RunID and manifest.

A reproducible end-to-end process: extract → normalize → map → reconcile (Accounting Profit → Taxable Income) → prepare DJP CSV & attachments → automated validations & dry-run → finalize submission → archive & lessons learned. Included: master mapping workbook design, starter mapping rows, journal templates, validation rules, PQ & VBA conceptual architecture (no code), testing scenarios, troubleshooting templates, auditor response templates, and appendices (manifest, sample journals, ticket template, sign-off checklists).

3. Core definitions (short glossary)
MainAccount/GL = D365 account code; TB = Trial Balance extract; MappingID = unique identifier linking GL to a 1771 line; Lampiran = SPT attachment schedule (for example, 1771-I); Addback = book expense disallowed by tax; Deduction = tax-allowed reduction; Bukti Potong = withholding tax certificate; AttachmentID = unique ID for supporting file; Materiality = numeric threshold used for escalation and approvals.

Narrative & examples
These definitions power the entire process. For example, MappingID MAP_4001_1771I ties D365 account 4001 (Sales Domestic) to the 1771-I Pendapatan Usaha line. AttachmentID ATT_SALES_2025 refers to the SalesLedger_2025.xlsx file. Use consistent IDs; auditors look for them. Keep a small glossary sheet in the workbook so anyone new on the team reads exactly how terms are used in templates and validations.

Extra examples of identifiers
- MappingID pattern: MAP_ | MAP_6100_1771I.
- AdjustmentID pattern: ADJ | ADJ_ADD_0004.
- AttachmentID pattern: ATT | ATT_BP21_20260110_001.
4. One-line end-to-end workflow
1) Extract TB from D365 (raw).
2) Normalize currency, signs, and periods.
3) Apply master mapping and tag adjustments.
4) Prepare Accounting Profit → Taxable Income reconciliation.
5) Produce DJP e-Form CSV and attach AttachmentIDs.
6) Run automated validations and dry-run.
7) Submit, capture receipt, archive manifest and checksums.

Narrative & practical steps
Think of the workflow as an assembly line: the TB extract is the inbound raw material; normalization ensures every component is measured with the same units (IDR, correct sign); mapping is assembly—placing each GL into the right 1771 slot; reconciliation inspects the finished product; CSV creation packages it for DJP; validations act as QA; submission is shipping; archiving is record retention. For each step the manual supplies a checklist to ensure nothing is skipped (date stamps, sign-offs, attachments referenced).

Quick practical example timeline
- Day 1: TB extract saved as ACME_TB_20251231_v1.0.xlsx.
- Day 2–3: Mapping & adjustments by Tax team.
- Day 4: Validation, reviewer sign-off.
- Day 5: Final approval, CSV generation and submission.

6. Normalization & FX policy — rules to document
FX conversion: use DJP-accepted rate for tax-year (record source and date).
Sign rules: define debit/credit normality per account class and apply consistently.
Rounding: apply DJP rounding rules; capture rounding diffs.
Period cutoffs: use tax-year end for accruals; treat prepaid/deferrals per tax rules.
Multiple currencies: keep conversion ledger for material items or per-currency aggregated conversions. Record FXPolicyVersion, RateSource, RateDate, RoundingRules.

Narrative & example application
If the company has a USD invoice paid in December but booked in a different period, the tax cut-off and FX date matter. Document that you used Bank rate on 2025-12-31 (source: Central Bank or company Treasury) and produce an FX_Conv_Log (AttachmentID) showing each converted transaction. When auditors question a large FX variance, you can point to the FX_Conv_Log and show that the method was consistently applied. Note rounding: if DJP wants integer IDR amounts, document rounding rules and show the aggregate rounding difference as a small reconciliation line.

Concrete FX example
| Invoice: USD 50,000 above. Rate used: 15,000 IDR/USD (DJP accepted bank rate 2025-12-31). Converted amount: 50,000 × 15,000 = 750,000,000 IDR. Record ATT_FX_20251231_001 = bank statement showing rate. If multiple conversions produce rounding variance of 1,200 IDR, create "Rounding Difference" line in Reconciliation. |

8. Mapping decision logic — practical decision tree
1) Identify FS class (Revenue/Expense/Asset/Liability/Equity).
2) Does DJP have a specific Lampiran/line for this class? Yes → map to that line. No → map to "Other" with explanation.
3) Is any portion non-taxable or non-deductible? If yes → split into taxable/non-taxable rows with calculation and supporting docs.
4) Does the item require a supporting schedule? (Inventory, FA, Payroll, WHT) If yes → link to supporting tab and AttachmentIDs.
5) If ambiguous, create an Exception ticket, flag for Tax Lead, and do temporary suspense mapping only after documenting rationale. Always capture decisionID, rationale, and sign-off.

Narrative scenario
Example: GL 4700 "Sales returns" shows a large year-end credit. Decision: is this simply a reduction to revenue or a special rebate that DJP considers non-taxable? Use the decision tree: class = kontra-revenue → DJP line = Pengurang Pendapatan. If a portion is related to warranty refunds that are non-taxable under law, split and document the basis with the contract and customer refund evidence. If ambiguous, ticket TKT_YYYYMMDD is opened and Tax Lead signs the rationale.

Additional decision example
| GL 8300 Interest Expense: Determine whether interest is related-party or third-party. If related-party, check TP and possibly adjust for non-arm’s-length. Mapping: third-party interest → Beban Bunga; related-party interest → Beban Bunga + TP review → possible ADJ_TP. |

10. Common mapping categories & practical notes
Revenues: split domestic/export/VAT-exempt.
COGS: reconcile opening/closing inventory and manufacturing OH allocations.
Payroll: separate gross wages, employer BPJS, taxable allowances.
Depreciation: track book vs tax depreciation per asset.
Interest: separate related-party interest and exempt categories.
FX: realize vs unrealized, hedge accounting.
Grants: donors' conditions determine taxable treatment.
Intercompany: always check TP and document.

Narrative & examples
Mapping nuances matter. For example, a sale recorded as "other income" might actually be core operating revenue if recurring; misclassification inflates non-operating items and can lead to incorrect Lampiran placement. Payroll: employer contributions to BPJS are normally deductible, but specific voluntary benefits may be non-deductible—capture them as ADJ_ADD entries with attachment evidence. For intercompany items, require TP study and intercompany agreement to support any deviation from arm’s-length pricing.

Specific category examples
- Grants: Government subsidy 100,000,000; contract states non-taxable → map to Non-Taxable Grant line and attach ATT_GRANT_001.
- FX: Unrealized FX loss on intercompany loan 5,000,000 → map to Selisih Kurs with note; attach FX valuation schedule.
11. Tax adjustments — required metadata & templates
Each adjustment must include: AdjustmentID, Type (Addback/Deduction/Timing/TP/CapEx), Reason (tax law/article), GLRefs, Calculation steps, JournalID, SupportingFiles (AttachmentIDs), Owner, DatePosted, Status. Template fields allow audits to trace why an adjustment exists and who approved it.

Narrative example
Suppose you have a donation payment GL 6800 of 50,000,000. Tax law allows 5,000,000 deduction if certain conditions met. The adjustment entry ADJ_DED_0002 includes a calculation showing allowed deduction 5,000,000 and addback 45,000,000. Attach donation receipt (ATT_DON_001) and approval memo. Post JNL-TAX-ADD/DEDUCTION and reference ADJ_DED_0002 in the reconciliation. The clarity of this adjustment file reduces auditor queries.

More template examples
- ADJ_ADD_ENT_0001: Entertainment disallowed portion; Calculation: BookAmount × Disallow% = Disallowed.
- ADJ_TIMING_REV_0001: Accrual-to-cash revenue timing difference; Calculation shows invoices recognized in accounting but taxable when cash received. Attach invoice list and bank receipts.
12. Fixed Assets — end-to-end checklist
1) Export FA register with FAID, acquisition cost (local & foreign), acquisition date, accumulated depn (book), book method, tax method/rate, useful life, disposal history.
2) Compute tax depreciation per tax rules and find book-tax differences.
3) Generate per-asset adjustment lines with AdjustmentID and aggregate by 1771 line.
4) Post tax adjustment journals with clear narration including MappingID and FAIDs.
5) Attach FA invoices, capex approvals, disposal sale contracts, and asset destruction approvals with AttachmentIDs.

Narrative worked example
FA_100 purchased for 2,000,000,000 IDR with book depn straight-line 20 years, tax depn accelerated to 10 years due to incentive. Compute annual book depn = 100,000,000; tax depn = 200,000,000; difference 100,000,000 creates ADJ_DEPN_100 for each year. Post JNL-TAX-DEPN referencing FA_100 and ATT_FA_100_INVOICE, and attach depreciation schedules that auditors can follow line-by-line. If the company claims tax incentive, attach the incentive approval.

Additional FA examples
- Partial disposal: FA_200 sold part (component sale). Separate land vs building; compute gain for each component and map accordingly.
- Revaluation differences: If accounting revalued asset, document tax basis still uses cost; create reconciliation schedule showing revaluation not recognized for tax.
13. Inventory & COGS — handling & evidence
1) Obtain stock-take report and reconcile closing inventory.
2) Confirm valuation method (FIFO/Weighted).
3) Reclassify intercompany transfers and document transfer pricing.
4) For write-offs, provide approval memos, cause analysis, and attach supporting docs.
5) Prepare inventory movement schedule linking opening/receipts/consumption/closing.

Narrative example
During year-end: opening inventory 200,000,000; purchases 800,000,000; closing inventory 350,000,000 → COGS = 650,000,000. If a write-off 15,000,000 appears due to obsolescence, attach ATT_WO_015 with manager approval. If tax disallows part of the expense (e.g., related to non-business use), create ADJ_ADD and attach the relevant evidence. The inventory movement schedule should list receipts (GRN numbers), purchase invoices, and adjustments with cross-referenced AttachmentIDs.

Additional inventory scenarios
- Slow-moving stock: create valuation allowance and document methodology. If tax disallows certain allowances, add ADJ_ADD and attach stock movement proof.
- Intercompany transfer at cost: map COGS at transfer value and attach intercompany invoice and TP study if required.
14. Payroll & PPh21 — concrete process
1) Export payroll register and Bukti Potong master: EmployeeID, GrossPay, Allowances, NonTaxable, EmployerContrib (BPJS), PPh21 Withheld, NetPay, Period.
2) Reconcile monthly WHT payable GL to totals of Bukti Potong.
3) Flag non-deductible benefits and generate addback AdjustmentIDs.
4) Prepare payroll Lampiran with employee-level summary if required by DJP.
5) Index Bukti Potong scans under AttachmentIDs and cross-reference in WithholdingRegister.

Narrative example with calculation
Payroll total gross = 220,000,000. Employer BPJS paid = 20,000,000 (deductible). If a housing allowance 5,000,000 is non-deductible, create ADJ_ADD_PAY_001 = 5,000,000 and post JNL-TAX-ADD referencing GL 6001. Reconcile the PPh21 withheld (e.g., 4,500,000) to GL WHT payable and attach scanned Bukti Potong files ATT_BP21_001–025. Reconciliation must show totals by month and the PPh21 claimed as tax credit line in the SPT.

Other payroll examples
- Severance pay: check local tax treatment; often taxable but may have special rules—document and attach termination agreements.
- Employer stock plans: determine if contributions are deductible; if not, create ADJ_ADD and attach plan documentation.
15. Withholding tax register structure & reconciliation
Maintain WithholdingRegister with: Date, Payee, PaymentType, GrossAmount, WHTType, WHTRate, WithheldAmount, BuktiPotongNo, GLExpense, GLWHT_Payable, PaidToTaxOfficeDate, ProofFile(AttachmentID). Monthly reconcile sum(ByWHTType) to GL flows; investigate differences > materiality.

Narrative example
Contractor payment: gross 100,000,000; PPh23 @2% = 2,000,000 withheld. Booking: DR ProfessionalExpense 100,000,000 / CR Bank 98,000,000 / CR WHT23_Payable 2,000,000. On payment to tax office: DR WHT23_Payable 2,000,000 / CR Bank 2,000,000. Record BuktiPotongNo BP23-202509-001 and attach ATT_WHT_BP23_001. Monthly reconciliation ensures WHT payable GL equals sum of withheld amounts minus payments to tax office.

Additional WHT cases
- Customer WHT credits (company as recipient): ensure amounts withheld by customers are captured and attached; map credit to PrepaidTax and reconciliation schedules.
- Missing Bukti Potong chain: if supplier fails to issue Bukti Potong, document follow-up communication and create a suspense record; do not claim the tax credit until evidence is provided.
16. VAT & revenue recognition — practical cautions
Always map revenue net of output VAT. For contract-based revenue (construction, long-term), document tax recognition rules and attach contract extracts indicating milestones, completion percentages, or recognized amounts. Use VAT Masa returns as supporting documents when revenue timing aligns with VAT remittance.

Narrative example
Contract: 2-year construction contract with milestone billing. Accounting recognizes revenue on percentage-of-completion; tax may require recognition on invoice. Create an explanatory memo linking contract clauses to tax treatment (Attachment ATT_CONTRACT_001). Map accounting revenue to the 1771 line with adjustment lines addressing timing differences. Attach VAT Masa reports to prove output VAT recording where relevant.

Extra revenue examples
- Sales returns & allowances: produce a credit-note ledger and map adjustments to Pengurang Pendapatan with attachments.
- Consignment sales: revenue recognized when consignee sells; map only net revenue recognized per accounting and provide consignee sales records as attachments.
17. Foreign exchange (FX) methodology
Document FX policy header: FXPolicyVersion, RateSource (BI/Bank), RateDate, RoundingRules, TreatmentRealizedVsUnrealized, HedgeNotes. For realized FX gains/losses supply bank advices. For unrealized FX show FX valuation mechanics and attach schedule. Material FX items should show transaction-level conversion audit trail.

Narrative example & split
Company had exports invoiced in USD. Realized receipts converted on date of bank settlement → treated as realized FX gains. Outstanding receivables revalued at year-end rate producing unrealized FX which the company treats per tax rule. Provide an FX_Schedule with line-level mapping: TRAN_ID, USD amount, invoice date, settlement date, rate used, IDR converted amount, realized/unrealized flag, Attachment (bank advices). This avoids auditor disputes about FX timing.

Example FX entries
- Realized FX: Bank statement shows USD 50,000 settled at 15,200 IDR → recognized IDR = 760,000,000 → attach bank advices ATT_FX_BANK_001.
- Unrealized FX on receivable: USD 10,000 at rate 15,400 vs invoice at 15,000 → unrealized gain recorded; attach FX valuation schedule.
18. Transfer pricing (TP) minimal deliverables
Required TP deliverables: related-party counterparty list, intercompany agreements, TP policy, summary of comparables, TP worksheet, TP adjustment calculations, authoriser signature. Keep TP documentation linked to MappingID and AdjustmentID for audit.

Narrative example
An intercompany service charged 3,000,000,000 IDR. Benchmarking shows arm’s length is 2,700,000,000 → create ADJ_TP_0003 = 300,000,000 increase to taxable income. Attach TP study ATT_TP_2025_01 and the intercompany agreement ATT_ICA_01. Include a short memo in Reconciliation explaining rationale and who approved the TP adjustment.

Practical TP application
- Always include a one-line TP rationale in the Reconciliation for easy auditor reference.
- If the TP adjustment is material, require Tax Lead and CFO sign-off recorded in ChangeLog.
19. Capital gains & disposal procedure
Steps: identify disposal documents, compute sale proceeds and book carrying amount, compute tax basis and taxable gain/loss, map to Keuntungan Penjualan Aktiva, prepare disposal journal, attach sale contract, invoice, clearance, and transfer proof. Separate non-depreciable (land) and depreciable components for proper tax basis.

Narrative example
Asset disposal documented: FA_210 sold for 500,000,000. Book carrying = 350,000,000. Tax accumulated depn differs, so tax carrying = 320,000,000. Taxable gain = 500,000,000 - 320,000,000 = 180,000,000. Post disposal journal and create CSV entry mapped to Keuntungan Penjualan Aktiva. Attach ATT_SALE_FA_210 (sale contract) and proof of payment to support proceeds.

Additional disposal nuances
- Sale of part of asset: compute attributable carrying amount by component and report accordingly.
- Related-party sale at a discount: require TP study and possibly adjust to arm’s length proceeds for tax.
20. Non-deductible expenses — categories & journal approach
Fines & penalties: full addback.
Entertainment: compute allowable percentage and addback disallowed portion.
Donations: only deductible with approved receipt; otherwise addback.
Personal expenses: always non-deductible.
For each: create ADDBACK AdjustmentID, post tax addback journal referencing MappingID and source invoices, and attach memo citing tax article.

Narrative example
Parking fines paid GL 6710 amount 2,000,000 are non-deductible → ADJ_ADD_FINE_001 = 2,000,000; Journal JNL-TAX-ADD-0002 posted and memo cites relevant tax articles and the invoice scanned as ATT_FINE_001. Entertainment: invoices total 20,000,000, 60% allowed → disallowed portion 8,000,000 → ADJ_ADD_ENT_0001 = 8,000,000.

More examples & calculations
- Car lease for employee personal use: allocate portion business vs personal; disallow personal portion as ADJ_ADD_PERSONAL.
- Sponsorship: confirm deductibility criteria, if partial then calculate allowed portion and ADJ for remainder; attach sponsorship agreement and proof of payment.

Narrative & example

23. DJP e-Form CSV specification & metadata
DJP CSV rules: exact DJP headers and field order, IDR amounts (rounded per DJP), no separators or currency symbols, UTF-8 encoding, no BOM unless required. Always include MappingID and AttachmentID columns for traceability. Store CSV_Meta_RunID.txt with RunID, MappingVersion, TB_SourceFile, FXRateDate, MaterialityUsed, Preparer, Reviewer, Approver, Notes. Attach this metadata with submission archive.

Narrative & example
Think of the DJP CSV as an export with strict formatting rules. Mistakes like an extra header column or commas in amounts cause rejections. Before submitting, run a text inspection: verify headers match DJP’s template exactly, and open the CSV in a text editor (not Excel) to ensure encoding is UTF-8. Save a CSV_Meta file that explains which mapping version produced this CSV; it’s the single best audit artifact to show how the CSV was created.

26. Pseudocode and SQL checks (practical)
