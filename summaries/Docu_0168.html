Expert guidance — comprehensive, step-by-step:
• High-level goal: prepare FS that are complete, comparable and understandable. Treat IAS 1 as the rulebook for layout, aggregation, subtotals and minimum line items.
• Immediate checklist: statement titles, reporting currency, comparatives, rounding, materiality thresholds, current/non-current splits, required subtotals. Maintain a master template and GL→FS mapping.
• Practical actions: master templates for (1) statement of financial position, (2) profit or loss & OCI, (3) statement of changes in equity, (4) cash flows, (5) notes index. Keep one-page policy summaries for material standards.
• Interim (IAS 34): produce condensed statements + narrative on seasonality, reclassifications, significant events. Reconcile interim movements to ledgers.
• IAS 7: choose direct or indirect method; reconcile cash movements to bank. Document non-cash adjustments. Produce cash-flow working schedules.
• Deliverables / controls: GL→FS mapping, notes index, policy pack, reconciliations, presentation checklist, preparer/reviewer sign-offs.
Additional Explanation:

3) Build a notes index tab: each note should have (a) standard reference, (b) minimum columns/tables, (c) data source link, (d) preparer. Keep a ‘notes status’ column (Not started / Draft / Ready / Reviewed).
4) For comparatives, prepare a reconciliation tab showing restatements or reclassifications, and include a short memo (1 paragraph) explaining each material restatement.
5) Close checklist: preparer signs in the file, reviewer puts initials, technical accounting lead adds a line confirming policy adherence — keep as a review trail for auditors.

2) Recognition & measurement fundamentals
First-time adoption IFRS 1
Inventories IAS 2
Government grants IAS 20
Hyperinflation IAS 29
Provisions & contingencies IAS 37
Expert guidance — comprehensive, step-by-step:
• Principle: never recognise unless definition is met. For assets: future economic benefit controlled by entity. For liabilities: present obligation from past events.
• IFRS 1: build a reconciliation pack (previous GAAP → opening IFRS equity), document exemptions and retain source docs.
• Inventories: choose cost formula (FIFO/Wtd avg). NRV test each item: NRV = expected selling price − costs to complete − costs to sell. Example worksheet + write-down journal. Keep ageing and obsolescence reports.
• Provisions (IAS 37): build a provision matrix: event, probability, scenarios, expected cashflows, discounting. Keep legal opinions and board minutes. Record decision logs and journals.
• Government grants: log grant conditions, decide presentation (income vs capital reduction), and keep correspondence.
Additional Explanation:
1) Prepare an "IFRS decision memo" for any ambiguous item: state facts, reference the exact IFRS paragraphs, apply the definitions, then conclude (YES/NO) with a dated signature.
2) Inventory NRV process: export stock ageing → run NRV formula per SKU → flag SKUs where cost > NRV → prepare write-down journal with supporting market price evidence (e.g., price lists, sales records).
3) Provisions: for each potential obligation, create 3 scenarios (low/most likely/high) with probabilities; compute expected value; if probability >50% and outflow probable, recognise provision and cite legal opinion. Save scenario sheet as evidence.
Recognition & measurement (operational frameworks, decision tools, examples):
• IFRS 1 project plan items (detailed): create mapping of prior-GAAP chart of accounts to IFRS opening balance sheet lines; prepare reconciliation trial balances with scripted adjustments; list exemptions applied per IFRS 1 (e.g., business combinations, cumulative translation differences) with quantitative impact; produce disclosure schedule for elections with rationale and board sign-off.
• Inventory detailed procedures: SKU-level NRV workbook with columns: SKU, Location, Qty, Cost/unit, Cost total, Est sales price/unit, Costs to complete/unit, Costs to sell/unit, NRV/unit formula, NRV total, Write-down = max(0, Cost total − NRV total), Evidence (market price link), Preparer, Reviewer. Automate flags for slow movers (no sales in 12 months) and high obsolescence risk. Provide sample GL journal and link to stock ledger snapshot.
• Provision matrix build-out: columns: CaseID, Issue summary, Legal opinion date, Probable/possible, Scenario1 (amount, prob), Scenario2, Scenario3, Expected value, PV discount rate, PV amount, Proposed accounting treatment, Evidence link, Prepared by, Reviewer, Board sign-off date. Maintain a rolling litigation tracker and calendar for key deadlines.
• Policy decision flowcharts: for recognition decisions create one-page flows for (a) asset recognition (control test + probable inflows), (b) liability recognition (present obligation + outflow probable), (c) government grant recognition (matching with conditions), (d) capitalisation vs expense (research vs development). Include sample fact patterns and mapped IFRS paragraphs.
• Sample "IFRS decision memo" template (fillable): Facts | Paragraphs cited | Definitions applied | Comparable precedent (past TAC decisions) | Quantitative impact (journal entries + balance movement) | Sensitivity analysis | Evidence attachments | Recommendation | Preparer | Reviewer | TAC sign-off date.
• Practical journal examples: Inventory write-down: Dr Inventory write-down expense / Cr Inventory allowance; Provision for warranty: Dr Warranty expense / Cr Provision for warranty; Government grant received in advance: Dr Cash / Cr Contract liability (or deferred grant) with memo on condition monitoring.

4) Leases (IFRS 16)
Leases (lessee ROU & liability) IFRS 16
Expert guidance — checklist & numeric example:
• Discovery: central lease register (contract ID, lessor/lessee, start/end, options, payments, CPI links).
• Identification: is there an identified asset? Does customer control its use? Watch embedded leases.
• Measurement: determine lease term (exercise options if reasonably certain), calculate discount rate (implicit or incremental borrowing rate). Example: payments 12,000 pa × 5 years @5% → PV ≈ 51,954 → initial ROU & liability = 51,954 (+/- direct costs/incentives).
• Post-initial: depreciate ROU, recognise interest on liability (effective interest), remeasure on modifications. Disclose maturities, variable payments, practical expedients used. Maintain system triggers for remeasurement.
Additional Explanation:
1) Run a contracts search (AP, procurement, HR) for keywords: "lease", "rent", "term", "option", "leaseback". Flag all candidate contracts for lease assessment.
2) For each candidate: answer the identification questions and store a short conclusion in the register (Yes lease / No lease + rationale). Attach the page of the contract showing the asset identification clause.
3) Discount rate: if implicit not available, compute incremental borrowing rate using company's average borrowing spread + government yield for comparable term; document inputs and source (bank quotes, treasury rates). Save calc sheet in register.
Leases (full operational playbook & templates):
• Lease register comprehensive fields: LeaseID | Entity | Lessor | Asset type | Asset description | Location | Contract start | Contract end | Renewal options (dates & terms) | Term assessment (reasonably certain Y/N) | Payment schedule (dates, amounts, currency) | CPI/index link | Variable payments type | Initial direct costs | Incentives | Lease classification (finance/operating pre-IFRS16 note) | Incremental borrowing rate memo link | PV lease payments | ROU asset opening | Lease liability opening | Subsequent remeasurement events log | Evidence attachments (contract pages).
• Discount rate derivation template: If implicit rate unavailable: build incremental borrowing rate (IBR) model using company credit spread + risk-free curve for lease currency and term, adjust for lease-specific factors (collateral, lessor recourse). Provide screenshotable inputs (bank quotes, sovereign yield curves) and memo with sensitivity (±50bps effect on PV).
• Initial measurement calculation cells (spreadsheet formula): PV of fixed payments + PV of certain variable payments linked to index = Lease liability; ROU = Lease liability + initial direct costs − lease incentives received. Include examples for single payment, monthly, annual and irregular payments.
• Subsequent accounting worked example: Show Year 1 table with opening liability, interest (EIR), cash payment, principal reduction, closing liability; ROU depreciation schedule with componentization if useful life different from lease term. Provide journal entries for interest, depreciation, cash payment and reassessment.
• Transition options & disclosure mapping: IFRS16 transitional practical expedients (e.g., use of hindsight, low-value asset exemption, short-term lease exemption) and required disclosure mapping (sum of ROU assets, lease liabilities, depreciation, interest) with example wording for notes.
