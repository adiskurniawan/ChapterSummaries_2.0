1. Bank reconciliation automation
Purpose: automatically reconcile bank statements to the general ledger to reduce manual matching and identify exceptions.
Detailed functionality: ingest multiple bank file formats, normalize dates/amounts, perform exact and fuzzy matching using amount tolerance and date windows, suggest matches ranked by confidence, support auto-mark-off for high-confidence matches and queue low-confidence matches for review, produce reconciliation certificate and audit trail.
Inputs: raw bank statements (CSV/XLSX), GL cash/clearing ledger, optional supplier/customer mapping and opening uncleared items.
Outputs: matched pairs, suggested matches (with confidence scores and match rules used), unmatched bank items, unmatched GL items, reconciliation report (PDF/Sheet), audit log of actions and overrides.
Implementation approach: Power Query to import and normalize multiple bank formats + mapping tables; VBA routines to compute fuzzy matching (amount tolerance, date window, normalized payee names), interactive review form for operator to accept/override suggestions; logging and export to PDF or ledger import file.
Controls & validations: format/spec validation on imports, checksum on totals, prevention of duplicate matching, configurable tolerance parameters, segregation of auto-markoff vs manual review.
Edge cases: test floated/cleared timing differences, split payments, rounding differences, foreign currency transactions, duplicate bank lines, stale unmatched items.
Deliverables: reconciliation workbook with PQ queries, VBA matching module and review form, documentation of matching logic, sample config table.
Complexity: Medium–High (data normalization and fuzzy matching are non-trivial).
