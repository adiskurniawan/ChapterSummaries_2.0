Chapter 1 — Deferred Tax Liabilities Table
Chapter & Summary
1 – WHAT’S NEW? — Regulatory change, effective-rate approach, transition

Regulation references & effective date: Peraturan Pemerintah No.58/2023 and Peraturan Menteri Keuangan No.168/2023. Effective date: 1 Jan 2024. Repeals and replaces selected PMK/PMK lampau noted in source.

Core change: Replace multiple legacy PPh 21/26 withholding schemes with a unified Effective Rate (Tarif Efektif, TER) framework. TER is applied to categories of monthly and one-off payments to simplify computation and reduce administrative variance. Explicitly states no new tax and no additional tax burden.

Transitional rules:
1) Identify period crossing 1 Jan 2024
2) Apply legacy rules to payments due before effective date
3) Apply TER for payments due on/after effective date
4) Reconcile withholding credits in next SPT Masa or annual reconciliation

Implementation aids: Online calculator on [www.pajak.go.id](http://www.pajak.go.id), sample FAQs, numeric examples, template journal entries.

Payroll systems note: Update TER tables, add daily prorate logic, tag payments by effective date, include audit trail fields (rule version, TER category, rounding rule).
2 – LATAR BELAKANG — Why the reform and expected outcomes

Problem statement: Old PPh 21 rules used multiple overlapping calculation methods causing inconsistent outcomes and high administrative cost.

Objectives:
1) Simplify withholding for employers and payers
2) Improve voluntary compliance by reducing errors
3) Enable better automated validation in withholding systems
4) Provide consistent outputs across payroll software

Design choices: Use deterministic TER tables instead of ad hoc aggregation. Apply monthly aggregation; daily prorating for partial months or one-offs. Compute taxable base → apply TER → subtract PTKP → produce withholding.

Expected benefits: Reduced audit disputes, fewer correction submissions, lower operational cost, faster taxpayer service. KPIs: fewer employer queries, fewer correction SPT Masa, higher match rate with annual SPT.

Operational implication: Payroll vendors release patches; employers test full-month, partial-month, FX, bonus, severance, and pension scenarios.
3 – OVERVIEW: PERUBAHAN DAN PENYESUAIAN PENGHITUNGAN — Scope, TER mechanics, categories

Scope changes: Include natura/kenikmatan, certain pension distributions for active employees, consolidated monthly reporting for fixed earnings.

TER mechanics:
- TER Monthly Category A/B/C for wage bands
- TER Daily for prorated periods
- TER One-off for bonuses/termination

Calculation flow:
1) Determine gross taxable components
2) Map recipient to TER category
3) Apply TER (monthly/daily/one-off factor)
4) Subtract monthly PTKP portion
5) Apply rounding rule
6) Withhold

Category assignment rules: Employment status, payment type, frequency. Examples provided in source.

Examples (source):
1) Monthly salary IDR 15,000,000 → TER Category A → apply table % → withholding
2) Mid-year bonus → annualize → One-off TER → prorate to month
3) Natura → monetary equivalent → include in gross

Edge rules: FX payments use bank reference rate on payout date; nonresident employers follow Pasal 26.
4 – DASAR PENGENAAN DAN PEMOTONGAN — Tax base, PTKP, inclusion/exclusion rules

Tax base definition: Wages, allowances, natura/benefits in kind, bonuses, commissions, employer pension contributions (taxable portion). Exclusions enumerated in source.

PTKP handling: PTKP allocated monthly; mid-year changes (marriage, dependents) → update records, recalc monthly PTKP.

Natura valuation: Convert to taxable monetary equivalent using cost-to-employer or market value; worksheets in lampiran.

Timing rule: Withhold on payment or vesting. Periodic wages → monthly; accrued/unpaid items → follow entitlement vs payment rules with retroactive adjustments if necessary.

Developer notes: Separate gross components, tag income codes, ensure PTKP mapping; use formula sheets and mind maps.
5 – PEMOTONG PPh PASAL 21/26 — Withholding agent roles and obligations

Who must withhold: Employers, government agencies, other designated payers. Exceptions listed in source.

Obligations: Calculate correctly, remit by statutory deadline, issue bukti potong, file SPT Masa, retain audit records.

Rights of pemotong: Request NPWP/docs; apply higher rate if NPWP missing; document exemptions.

Entities not required to withhold: Certain government payments/transfers. Employer must document exceptions.

Administrative checklist:
1) Verify recipient identity and NPWP
2) Classify payment type and TER category
3) Compute gross taxable base and apply TER/PTKP
4) Generate bukti potong
5) Remit and file SPT Masa
6) Archive supporting documents

Sample SPT Masa subtotals/reconciliation included.
6 – PENGHITUNGAN UNTUK PEGAWAI TETAP — Detailed stepwise calculation and examples

Taxable components: Base salary, fixed/variable allowances, natura, employer pension contributions (if taxable).

Step algorithm:
1) Aggregate monthly gross components
2) Full month → use Monthly TER
3) Apply monthly PTKP portion
4) Separate one-offs → One-off TER / daily prorate
5) Apply rounding (nearest Rp1 or Rp100)
6) Issue bukti potong, record journal entries

PTKP example: Married, 1 dependent → annual PTKP X → monthly PTKP = X/12

One-off payments / bonuses: Bonus IDR 50,000,000 → annualize → One-off TER → compute withholding

Severance / termination: Identify component → check exemption or table → compute withholding (flowchart in source)

Mid-year joiner/leaver: Daily TER prorate, e.g., join day 10/30 → apply factor 21/30

FX & non-resident: Convert FX using official rate; foreign employers → Pasal 26 / treaty rules.

Implementation checklist: Unit tests for standard month, partial month, bonus, severance, natura valuation, FX conversion.
7 – PENGHITUNGAN UNTUK PEGAWAI TIDAK TETAP — Rates, prorata, and reporting

Definition: Daily labor, contract workers, temporary staff, honoraria recipients who are not permanent employees.

Withholding base: Per payment; multiple payments same month → aggregate per rules.

Daily TER process:
1) Calculate gross for payment period
2) Convert to daily equivalent if needed
3) Apply daily TER
4) Subtract prorated PTKP if eligible
5) Round and remit

Example: Daily worker 10 days at IDR X/day → compute monthly equivalent → apply non-permanent TER category.

Honorarium / short contracts: Aggregate multiple honoraria in month for correct withholding; source has worked example.

Reporting & bukti potong: Issue per payment or period; lampiran provides format and required fields.

Admin tips:
- Flag non-permanent payees
- Store contract period dates
- Implement aggregation window logic
8 – PENGHITUNGAN UNTUK BUKAN PEGAWAI — Service providers and special tariffs

Scope: Consultants, freelancers, vendors, rent recipients, other non-employee recipients.

Tariff types: Some final, others non-final (creditable in annual SPT); source lists tariff matrix by income type and references.

Document requirements: NPWP required; non-NPWP → higher rate. Retain contract/invoice as support.

Calculation steps:
1) Identify income category / final vs non-final
2) Compute gross payment (exclude VAT if separate)
3) Apply tariff per table
4) Non-final → record withholding credit

Foreign recipients: Apply Pasal 26; if DTT applies → request certificate of tax residency → apply reduced treaty rate after verification.

Examples: Consultant fee IDR 100,000,000 → 2% final → withhold IDR 2,000,000. Rent examples and NPWP/no-NPWP scenarios included.

Operational note: Integrate with AP system; produce separate ledger entries for net pay and tax withheld.
9 – PEMOTONGAN PPh 21 LAINNYA — Special participants, pensions, and former employees

Coverage: Event participants, trainees receiving honoraria, pension participants still employed, payments to former employees (post-employment benefits).

Event participants: Treat per payment; multiple payments same month → aggregate per aggregation rule.
Example: conference honorarium calculation.

Pension participants still employed: Determine if benefit taxable under PPh 21 / PPh 26; apply deduction rules; map to TER category.
Example: employer pension payout partially exempt; remainder taxed via TER.

Former employees: Severance / retirement payments follow distinct rules.

Decision tree:
1) Identify payment type
2) Check exemption thresholds
3) If taxable → TER or legacy treatment per transitional rules
4) Compute withholding

Appendix cross-refs: Each case → lampiran examples with journal entries, bukti potong, reconciliation steps.
10 – PEMOTONGAN PPh PASAL 26 — Nonresident withholding, treaties, and computation

Definition & scope: Withholding on Indonesian-sourced payments to nonresidents (individuals/entities) for services, royalties, interest, specified payments.

Tariffs & treaty application: Domestic Pasal 26 rates unless DTT provides lower rate.

Required evidence: Certificate of Tax Residency, contracts, invoices.

Calculation formula:
1) Determine gross Indonesian-sourced payment
2) Determine gross vs net basis per income type
3) Apply statutory Pasal 26 or treaty rate
4) Retain documentation if using treaty rate

Example: Royalty gross IDR X → 10% domestic or 5% treaty → withhold accordingly.

Procedural requirements: Issue bukti potong Pasal 26, withhold/remit using designated code, file SPT Masa Pasal 26, keep treaty docs for audit.

Edge cases: Permanent establishment risk; if PE exists → tax treatment may change, withholding may convert to reporting obligations.

Comprehensive worked examples: Monthly salaried employee, part-timer, daily worker, consultant fee, bonus, severance, natura valuation, pension drawdown, Pasal 26 royalty. Inputs, steps, TER lookup, PTKP, rounding, withholding, journal entries, bukti potong included.

Step-by-step tables: Base salary, allowances, natura, employer contributions, PTKP, TER factor, withholding amount, reconciliation (withheld vs remitted vs reported).

Flowcharts & decision trees: Classify recipient → choose TER → apply PTKP → compute withholding → issue bukti potong → remit. Printable flowcharts in source.
