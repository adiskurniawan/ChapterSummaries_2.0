1.0 BACKGROUND AND DEFINITIONS Intra-Programme transactions are those entries, expenditure items that are settled by an OUCRU unit/ entity but actual expenditure incurred is recognised by another OUCRU unit/entity. Within OUCRU Group, there will be 4 Legal entities:
+ OUCRU Vietnam: Includes of Ho Chi Minh and Hanoi Units (“OUCRU VN”)
+ EOCRU (OUCRU Indonesia) (“OUCRU IND”)
+ OUCRU Nepal (“OUCRU NP”)
+ PAHS
In which, OUCRU Nepal and PAHS are excluded from OUCRU Intra-Programme transaction procedures due to regulatory requirements and tax compliance related complexities involved in recording and reporting of transactions not strictly undertaken wholly and exclusively for OUCRU Nepal and PAHS entities.

Therefore, OUCRU Vietnam (HCM and HN) and OUCRU Indonesia are the main entities that will be subjected to OUCRU Intra-Programme transaction procedures.
2.0 PURPOSE OUCRU financial dimensions setup, and in particular, the Approval Code (AC) is unit/entity specific, therefore, cross-geographical use of AC is not permitted by the system. For this reason, when a PI requires to undertake transactions in more than one unit, the PI is encouraged to setup separate ACs (therefore budgets and other setups) to ensure expenditure and the related settlement occur within the same unit/entity.
However, there might be instances where setting up separate ACs are not practical or the best option. An example of is a one-off or unanticipated and low-value transaction needs to be expensed/paid that require a workaround.
3.0 RESPONSIBILITY Intra-Programme transactions involving OUCRU Vietnam (OUCRU VN) and OUCRU Indonesia (OUCRU IND) must follow the prescribed procedures in ensuring transactions are recorded correctly, settled swiftly on monthly basis and the related accounts are reconciled periodically, ideally: monthly basis. Head of Finance (“HoF”) of VN and IND are primarily responsible for completing the required transactions and performing the periodic reconciliations.

Designation Unit Responsibility
1st Unit – The unit/entity that makes payment on behalf of the other unit(s). (Payment on behalf unit)
2nd Unit – The unit/entity that owns the expenditure (Expense incurring unit). The expenditure will impact the budget - Approval code(s) managed by this Unit.
* Depending on the nature of the transaction, a unit can be 1st Unit in some cases and can be 2nd Unit in other cases within a month.

Purpose: Recording the “expenditure” as balance sheet account to prevent this from impacting the actual expenditure figures since it is not belong to 1st Unit monthly expenditure. The 1st Unit only makes payment on behalf of 2nd Unit.

#3 Providing/ communicating the posted details
Once the accounting/GL entry is completed, HOF of the 1st Unit share with the 2nd Unit - where the AC of the concerned transaction is managed:
A. All supporting documents:
• Original document can be kept in 1st Unit
• Scanned version will be sent to 2nd Unit (for bookkeeping and audit purposes).
B. Completed details of the transaction including, but not limited to GL Extraction information:
• Invoiced amount & payment amount
• Item date
• GBP, USD, EUR Equivalent value.
• Relevant monthly exchange rates used
 Sharing of the details must happen immediately after the payment and relevant balance sheet entries are made by the 1st party unit.
Purpose: This step will enhance the information communication between the units/parties and the accuracy of the posting therefore enhance periodic reconciliation between the two.
Due date: ???
Who Performs: Head of Finance

#4 Perform periodic reconciliation
Ideally, the Intra-programme reconciliation would be performed on a monthly basis. The 1st Unit HoF will send the list of all transactions that had been paid on behalf to check and reconcile with the 2nd Unit’ transactions expenditures. Any discrepancy must be discussed and finalized with approval from CFO.
Purpose: To make sure that information are communicated, checked and reconciled between related parties.
Who Performs: Head of Finance and CFO.
