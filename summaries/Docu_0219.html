Q: Why should one family manage BPJS Kesehatan through one main Mobile JKN account instead of spreading the record across several people?
A: Show answer
One main account makes the membership record easier to control because the family sees the same data, the same service history, and the same payment position in one place. A shared family account is also easier to review when the household changes. If a dependent is added, removed, or corrected, the main manager can update the information in one place and keep the rest of the record aligned. That continuity matters because BPJS Kesehatan administration is not just about logging in; it is about keeping a long-running membership file accurate enough to support service access, payment review, and contact updates without confusion.
