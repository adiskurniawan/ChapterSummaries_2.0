Topic
Details
Short definition Fiscal: treatment, classification, valuation, depreciation and reporting rules set by tax law for computing taxable income.
Accounting: recognition, measurement, depreciation and disclosure rules used for financial reporting under accounting standards (IFRS/GAAP/local GAAP).
Primary purpose Fiscal: compute taxable profit and comply with tax rules.
Accounting: present economic reality to investors, creditors, management.
Recognition & initial measurement Fiscal: may follow tax-code criteria and prescribed tax basis.
Accounting: recognized when control + probable future benefits + cost reliably measured; measured at cost (or revalued amount if allowed).
Depreciation method & useful life Fiscal: methods, rates and useful lives often prescribed by tax authorities (fixed schedules, accelerated options).
Accounting: management selects method and useful life to reflect consumption (straight-line, declining balance, units of production).
Capitalization threshold & revaluation Fiscal: thresholds and revaluation typically restricted by tax law.
Accounting: entity policy driven by materiality; revaluation may be allowed and impairment must be tested.
Disposal & gain/loss Fiscal: taxable gain based on tax proceeds minus tax basis, subject to tax rules.
Accounting: gain/loss recognized per accounting standards; timing/amount may differ from tax.
Typical timing differences & tax effect Differences in basis, life, method or threshold create temporary differences → deferred tax assets/liabilities under accounting rules.
Practical examples Fiscal: 1) Machinery cost IDR 1,000,000: tax life 5 years (20% SL) → larger tax depreciation early years.
Accounting: 2) Same machinery: accounting life 10 years (10% SL) → slower depreciation; results in temporary difference and deferred tax.
Fiscal: Capitalization threshold: tax capitalizes >IDR 1,000,000.
Accounting: Accounting capitalizes >IDR 5,000,000.
Implications for finance & tax teams Maintain parallel asset registers; reconcile regularly; record deferred tax for temporary differences; ensure documentation for capitalization, useful-life estimates and tax elections; configure ERP to track dual bases.
Controls & compliance risks Poor reconciliation or documentation increases risk of tax adjustments and audit findings; ensure strong vendor/asset tagging, approvals, and retention of supporting evidence.
Practical checklist (operational) 1. Maintain fields: accounting cost, tax cost, accounting accumulated depreciation, tax accumulated depreciation, tax code.
2. Document capitalization thresholds, depreciation policies and rationale.
3. Reconcile accounting vs tax asset registers monthly/quarterly.
4. Compute and post deferred tax for timing differences each reporting period.
5. Obtain written tax rulings for ambiguous items where possible.
6. Train AP/asset custodians on asset tagging and required documentation.
One-line summary Fiscal: fixed-asset rules serve tax calculation and compliance.
Accounting: fixed-asset rules serve economic reporting — differences must be tracked with reconciliations and deferred-tax treatment when temporary.
