Fixed Assets (Capital Assets)
Purpose & objective

Fixed assets represent a significant investment, carry heightened compliance, audit and donor risk, and require robust lifecycle controls from acquisition through disposal. This chapter establishes a comprehensive framework for identifying, approving, recording, safeguarding, depreciating, monitoring and disposing of fixed assets and non-capital assets managed by Save the Children International (SCI).

The objectives of this policy are to ensure that:
• assets are correctly classified as capital or non-capital in line with SCI policy, accounting standards and donor requirements;
• capital assets are capitalised, depreciated and reported accurately in the financial system;
• donor-funded assets are managed strictly in accordance with donor and member regulations;
• asset registers provide a complete, accurate and auditable record of all assets owned or managed by SCI;
• assets are safeguarded against loss, misuse, theft or unauthorised disposal;
• asset acquisitions, transfers and disposals are properly authorised and documented; and
• financial statements, donor reports and audits are supported by a full, transparent audit trail.

Given SCI’s limited availability of unrestricted funds, the volume of capital assets at country office level is expected to be low. However, the control standards described in this chapter apply regardless of volume and must be followed consistently.
Scope

This chapter applies to:
• all tangible fixed assets (capital assets) owned by SCI;
• all non-capital assets recorded in asset registers for control and logistics purposes;
• assets purchased with unrestricted (general) funds;
• assets purchased with restricted (award/donor) funds;
• assets managed by SCI but legally owned by donors, members or third parties;
• asset acquisition, capitalisation, depreciation, revaluation, transfer and disposal; and
• all country offices, sub-offices and field locations.

This chapter does not cover:
• inventory or consumables;
• intangible assets other than computer software;
• assets purchased and owned directly by partners under sub-awards (unless temporarily held by SCI).
Key definitions & asset classification principles

Capital asset (fixed asset) — SCI defines a capital asset as tangible property or equipment that meets all of the following criteria:
• purchased using SCI unrestricted funds;
• has a total unit cost of USD 5,000 or more, inclusive of taxes, shipping, duties, installation and other costs necessary to bring the asset into use; and
• has an estimated useful life of more than one year.

Capital assets are:
• recorded on the balance sheet;
• capitalised at cost; and
• depreciated over their estimated useful economic life.

Assets purchased using award (restricted) funds are not capitalised, regardless of value, because legal ownership rests with the donor rather than SCI.
Non-capital assets

SCI recognises non-capital assets, which are items of strategic or operational importance that do not meet the full definition of a capital asset. Non-capital assets:
• are recorded in the asset register for accountability, logistics and safeguarding;
• are expensed in full in the year of acquisition;
• are not capitalised or depreciated; and
• remain subject to donor, member and asset management rules.

An item is classified as a non-capital asset if any of the following apply:
Value-based criteria
• unit cost between USD 1,000 and USD 5,000 (e.g., HF radios, generators, computer equipment);

Strategic importance criteria (even if value is below USD 5,000)
• expected useful life of more than one year and powered by electricity or fuel and has a serial number (e.g., laptops, desktops, VHF radios);
• incurs ongoing running costs that require monitoring and control (e.g., mobile phones, satellite phones);
• value exceeds USD 5,000 but purchased with restricted (donor) funds (e.g., donor-funded vehicles);
• defined as an asset by the donor, regardless of value (e.g., digital cameras funded by a donor).
Core recognition principle

• Capital assets: recognised on the balance sheet and depreciated over their useful life.
• Non-capital assets: expensed immediately but recorded and tracked in the asset register.
• Donor-funded assets: tracked for control and reporting but not capitalised.
Governance & responsibilities (RACI)

Country Director (CD):
• ensures asset acquisitions and disposals comply with the Schedule of Financial Authorities (SoFA);
• approves capital asset budgets and unplanned asset purchases;
• approves asset disposal and transfer proposals;
• delegates day-to-day asset management and ensures compliance with this policy.

Country Finance Director (CFD):
• ensures correct classification, capitalisation and depreciation of assets;
• ensures asset costs and depreciation are accurately recorded in Agresso;
• ensures the asset register is complete, accurate and regularly reviewed;
• ensures compliance with donor and member asset regulations;
• signs off monthly asset register reviews and depreciation journals.

Logistics / Supply Chain:
• manages physical custody, tagging and tracking of assets;
• records acquisitions, disposals, transfers and location changes;
• supports stock counts and condition assessments.

Finance Team:
• maintains the accounting records for assets and depreciation;
• prepares and posts depreciation journals;
• supports reconciliations, audits and donor reporting.

Regional Office / Centre:
• approves capital budgets and revisions;
• reviews quarterly asset registers;
• ensures consistency, donor compliance and audit readiness.
1. Capital and non-capital assets

To determine whether an item is a capital or non-capital asset, country offices must apply the Asset Capitalisation Decision Tree, which considers:
• funding source (restricted vs unrestricted);
• unit cost (inclusive of all directly attributable costs);
• useful life;
• strategic importance; and
• donor or member definitions.

This decision must be documented and retained for audit.
2. Asset acquisition — Planning and approval

SCI reviews asset purchases and depreciation during:
• preparation of the annual capital budget; and
• the year, when budget revisions are requested.

Timing differences affect depreciation:
• delayed purchases reduce current-year depreciation;
• early purchases increase current-year depreciation.

Country offices are accountable for managing variances against approved depreciation budgets.
Capital asset approval requirements

• Capital assets must be included in the approved annual capital budget.
• If not budgeted:
– a budget revision request is required;
– additional approval is required;
– a business case is mandatory for assets over USD 15,000;
– assets over USD 150,000 require Centre approval per SoFA.
Total cost of an asset (capitalised cost)

The capitalised cost includes:
• purchase price;
• taxes and duties;
• shipping and freight;
• installation and commissioning;
• set-up and configuration costs; and
• all costs necessary to bring the asset into use.

Excluded from asset cost:
• vehicle registration fees;
• licences;
• insurance;
• spare parts;
• operating and maintenance costs — these are expensed.
Acquisition process (steps)

1. Confirm approval under capital budget (capital assets only).
2. Obtain additional approvals where required.
3. Follow procurement policy and donor source/origin rules.
4. Retain all supporting invoices and documentation.
5. Assign a unique asset number and tag the asset.
6. Record full details in the asset register (description, serial, funding source, cost, date, custodian, location).
7. Forward asset registration to finance for Agresso setup.
8. Review asset register monthly to confirm completeness.
Accounting entry on acquisition

Dr Capital Asset (1500 / 1510 / 1540 / 1550 / 1600) — or Expense (non-capital assets)
Cr Bank / Supplier (1000 / 2000)
Capitalisation of partner-owned assets

• Assets purchased by partners via sub-awards: Not capitalised or recorded by SCI (partners record and manage per sub-award).
• Assets purchased by SCI and donated to a partner: Capitalise on acquisition → record in register → subsequently remove to reflect disposal/donation.
3. Asset register — Overview & expectations

Country offices must maintain a comprehensive asset register covering all capital and non-capital assets. The register must record:
• asset number;
• description and serial number;
• funding source (donor/general);
• acquisition date and cost;
• location and custodian;
• depreciation details (capital assets);
• disposal or transfer details; and
• supporting documentation links/references.
Monthly process (asset register & depreciation)

• Finance reviews the asset register monthly.
• Updates include additions, disposals, location/custodian changes, and value adjustments.
• Review must be signed off by the CFD.
• Monthly depreciation is calculated and posted.

Depreciation journal
Dr Depreciation expense (7710 / 7740 / 7750)
Cr Accumulated depreciation (1511 / 1541 / 1551)
Quarterly process

• At quarter-end the country office submits the asset register with supporting documentation (purchase invoices, shipping, installation invoices etc.) to the Regional Office.
• The Regional Office reviews completeness, sufficiency of audit trail and donor compliance.
• Regional Office conducts periodic spot-checks during visits.
Year-end process

• Annual physical stock check against the register to confirm existence, location and working condition.
• Record and resolve discrepancies and document results.
• Submit annual confirmations and supporting evidence to Centre as part of year-end close.
4. Depreciation — Principles

Depreciation spreads the cost of a capital asset over its useful life and must be:
• compliant with SCI policy, donor and local regulations;
• recorded monthly by the country office; and
• applied on a straight-line basis unless otherwise approved.
Depreciation calculation & budgeting guidance

For assets purchased in the current financial year:
• Estimate cost of assets to be purchased (including taxes, shipping & installation).
• Calculate monthly depreciation per asset: cost × monthly charge (from table).
• Multiply by remaining months in financial year (remember full month in acquisition month; no charge in disposal month).

For assets purchased in prior years:
• Use actual cost and remaining useful life to calculate monthly charge; multiply by 12 months or the number of months remaining if life less than 12 months.

Accounting treatment
Dr Depreciation expense – donor / undesignated SoF(s)
Cr Accumulated depreciation
4.1. Direct award charging vs. depreciation

• Where donor rules explicitly permit, asset costs may be charged directly to the award rather than capitalised and depreciated.
• Direct charging must be documented, comply with donor agreements, and be approved by finance and donor focal points.
• Ensure donor-approved treatment is applied consistently and recorded in the asset register and financial notes.
5. Asset disposal / transfer — Overview

All disposals, transfers and sales must be planned, authorised and documented. Special attention is required where assets are financed from awards or where legal title remains with members/donors. Procedures vary by funding source (award-funded vs overhead-funded).
5.1. Assets funded from awards (donor-funded)

• Between project end date and final report, prepare a disposal/transfer plan in consultation with the relevant member(s) and donor, ensuring compliance with donor requirements.
• On transition to SCI, members may transfer either legal title or only the right-to-use; clarify ownership and implement disposal/transfer plan accordingly.

Process (summary):
1. Country Director proposes disposal/transfer plan (may delegate preparation to CFD/logistics) and reviews/approves the proposal.
2. Proposal sent to Regional Finance Director by end of month immediately before project end date.
3. Regional Finance Director reviews and approves (or requests amendments) by project end date.
4. Country office sends the proposal to the Member via express lane immediately following project end date.
5. Country office works with Member to resolve queries, keeping Regional Finance Director updated.
6. Final proposal approved by Regional Finance Director prior to final agreement with Member.
5.2. Assets funded from overheads

• Disposal requests must be approved by the Country Director and Regional Finance Director using the asset disposal form.
• Submit all relevant documentation for review (condition, valuation, approvals).
5.3. Sale of assets

• Country offices should seek to maximise proceeds when selling assets (except when asset is donated free of charge).
• Net proceeds of sales must be credited to the Asset Replacement Reserve (99900013) held at Centre.
• Country Offices do not have automatic access to those funds; separate application required to access for replacement purchases.

Transition specifics:
• If vehicle transferred to SCI as a non-capital asset: proceeds go to Asset Replacement Reserve and CO may apply to Centre to use funds for replacement.
• If only right-to-use was transferred (member retains legal title): CO must liaise with Member to settle proceeds as per member guidance.
5.4. Documentation (disposals & transfers)

• Retain all supporting documentation for audit: proof of sale, disposal invoices, donation documentation, member/donor agreements, approvals, correspondence and valuation evidence.
5.5. Asset register (on disposal)

• Immediately update the asset register to reflect disposal/transfer date, method, proceeds, approvals and any reclassification.
• Ensure audit trail links register entries to disposal documentation and accounting entries.
5.6. Accounting treatment (detailed)

Capital assets (on disposal/sale):
• Remove cost and accumulated depreciation; recognise proceeds and gain/loss:
Dr Accumulated depreciation (1511 / 1541 / 1551)
Cr Asset cost (1500 / 1510 / 1540 / 1550 / 1600)
Dr/Cr Bank (sale/scrap proceeds / costs, if any) (1000)
Cr/Dr Gains / losses on disposal of assets (7600 / 7610)

Non-capital assets:
• If agreed with member, proceeds from sale of non-capital assets are to be credited to the income & expenditure account or to the replacement reserve as appropriate:
Dr/Cr Bank (1000)
Dr/Cr Gains / losses on disposal of assets (7600 / 7610) / Replacement reserve (see section on Asset Replacement Reserve).
Audit, compliance & donor considerations

• Asset records must support statutory accounts, donor audits and internal audits.
• Donor and member rules supersede SCI policy where stricter or specifically agreed.
• Non-compliance risks include cost disallowance, reputational damage and weakened internal control ratings.
Record retention

• Retain asset documentation per statutory, donor and internal retention requirements (in practice retain for audit periods commonly 6–10 years or longer where local law requires).
• Asset registers and supporting documentation must be archived with clear indexing and access controls.
Key risks & controls

Risks:
• misclassification of assets;
• unauthorised disposal or transfer;
• donor non-compliance;
• incomplete or inaccurate asset registers;
• theft or loss of assets.

Controls:
• approval thresholds and capital budget controls;
• unique asset tagging and custody assignments;
• monthly register review and CFD sign-off;
• annual stock checks and spot checks;
• segregation of duties for acquisition, custody and accounting;
• donor compliance checks and documentation;
• secure storage of asset documentation.

Implementation reminders — immediate actions for country offices

1. Ensure asset approvals are obtained under SoFA and capital budgets.

4. Post depreciation monthly and retain supporting calculations.
5. Prepare quarterly submissions to Regional Office with supporting documentation.
6. Plan and execute an annual physical stock count with reconciliation and variance explanations.
