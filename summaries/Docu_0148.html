Common practice to allocate monthly bank charges in a multi-funder NGO (single-column)
Policy overview
Define a written bank-charge allocation policy approved by finance leadership. State objectives (fairness, auditability, compliance with donor rules), responsible owners, frequency (monthly), and rounding/immateriality rules.
Primary allocation principle — benefit/cost causation
Charge funds that generated the banking activity where identifiable (e.g., donor receipts, fund payments). When direct causation not possible, use a consistent, documented proxy (see methods below).
Method A — Pro-rata by eligible expenditure (common default)
Allocate total monthly bank charges across active funds in proportion to each fund’s share of total eligible program and admin expenditures during the month. Exclude restricted items that shouldn’t bear admin costs if policy requires.
Method B — By transaction count / volume (when transactions drive fees)
If fees are driven by number of transfers/collections, allocate by count or weighted count (e.g., domestic vs international). Use bank/treasury system reports to extract transaction counts per fund.
Method C — By cash flow value (use for value-based fees)
Allocate by the absolute value of inflows/outflows per fund if fees scale with amounts processed (e.g., percentage fees on receipts). Use monthly cash movement summaries per fund.
Method D — Direct charge to fund/account
If an individual bank account is dedicated to a single fund, book the charge directly to that fund. For earmarked donor accounts with separate banks, charges normally hit that fund.
Hybrid approach
Combine methods where appropriate — e.g., base fee pro-rata by expenditure + variable fee by transaction count. Document weighting and formula and apply consistently.
Foreign currency & correspondent bank charges
Allocate FX conversion spreads and intermediary bank charges to the fund that initiated or benefited from the FX transaction. If unclear, treat as program/admin overhead and allocate pro-rata. Record gross charge plus any recoveries separately.
Journal entry examples (monthly automated OR manual)
1) To record bank fee accrual: DR Bank Charges (shared) / CR Bank (bank ledger).
2) To allocate to funds: DR Fund A — Bank Charges / DR Fund B — Bank Charges / CR Bank Charges (shared).
Show calculation table in working paper supporting amounts.
Accrual & timing treatment
Accrue charges in the month they relate to (not when bank posts). Use cut-off controls for month-end. Reverse accruals only when necessary and document.
GL structure & mapping
Maintain a single central GL control account for bank fees and mapped cost-centre / fund subaccounts. Use consistent chart of accounts codes for ease of reporting.
Controls & supporting documentation
Retain bank statements, fee schedules, transaction reports, allocation workpapers and formula snapshots. Require sign-off by Finance Manager and attach to monthly financial package.
Donor restrictions & allowable costs
Check donor agreements: some donors explicitly allow or disallow banking fees. If disallowed, charge to unrestricted administrative budget or absorbed by local organization unless donor agreement permits reclassification. Document exceptions and obtain donor approval if needed.
Reporting & disclosure
Report allocation basis in donor financial reports and annual financial statements (policy note). For significant reallocations, disclose methodology and rationale.
Immateriality threshold & rounding
Set a threshold under which charges are expensed to admin centrally (e.g., amounts < USD 50 per fund per month). Apply consistent rounding rules and disclose threshold in policy.
Reconciliation & review checklist (month-end)
1) Extract total bank charges from statement.
2) Reconcile to bank GL control.
3) Run allocation calculations and produce supporting schedule.
4) Post allocations (or reversing accruals).
5) Finance Manager review & sign-off.
6) File supporting docs.
Systems & automation tips
Where possible, configure the ERP or accounting tool to capture transaction counts and cash flows by fund and automate allocation postings to reduce manual error. Store the allocation script/workbook as part of controls.
Audit readiness & sample working paper contents
Include: bank statement, bank fee schedule, extraction showing total fees, detailed allocation schedule (method, formula, inputs), journal entries, approval sign-off, and donor notes on restrictions.
Example calculation (compact)
Total bank fees = 1,200. Eligible expenditures: Fund A = 30,000; Fund B = 70,000 → Fund A share = 30% → allocation = 360; Fund B = 840. Show formula: Allocation_FundX = TotalFees × (EligibleExp_FundX / SumEligibleExp).
When to seek donor clarification
If a donor’s terms are ambiguous about bank charges, request written clarification or include bank charges as part of indirect/administrative cost recovery discussion. Keep copies of donor responses.
Governance — review frequency
Review the allocation policy annually or whenever banking arrangements change (new account, fee schedule changes, major funding mixes). Document any policy changes and effective dates.

Common practice to allocate monthly staff cost in a multi-funder NGO (single-column)
Policy overview
Define a written staff-cost allocation policy approved by senior finance and program leadership. State objectives (equitable, auditable, compliant), scope (salaries, benefits, payroll taxes, allowances, bonuses), responsible owners, frequency (monthly), and rounding/immateriality rules.
Primary allocation principle — cost causation & benefit
Allocate staff costs to the fund(s) that employed or benefited from the staff member’s time. When direct causation is not possible, apply a documented, consistent proxy method (time-based, budget-based, FTE share).
Method A — Time-based (timesheets) — preferred where feasible
Collect monthly detailed timesheet records showing hours by project/fund/activity. Allocate salary and associated benefits in proportion to hours worked for each fund. Use approved timesheet system and require supervisor validation.
Method B — Pro-rata by effort / FTE split
For staff with stable, recurring splits, use an authorized FTE percentage allocation (e.g., 0.6 program A / 0.4 admin). Review and re-authorize splits at least quarterly or when duties change. Maintain signed authorizations.
Method C — Pro-rata by eligible payroll cost or budgeted labour
Allocate using each fund’s share of total eligible payroll costs for the month if time-data is not practical. Apply only where donors permit and document limitations.
Method D — Direct charge to fund/account
If a staff member is 100% funded by a single donor/fund (contract, secondment), charge the full salary and associated costs directly to that fund. Maintain the funding agreement on file.
Method E — Hybrid approach
Combine time-based for program-facing roles and budget/FTE-based for shared or administrative roles. Document weighting and calculations.
Fringe benefits & payroll taxes
Allocate benefits (pension, insurance, leave accruals) and statutory payroll taxes using the same basis as the related salary (i.e., if salary is split 60/40, allocate benefits 60/40). For Central HR or corporate benefits, allocate using agreed overhead or FTE basis.
Leave accruals and overtime treatment
Accrue annual leave, sick leave, and long-service leave in the month they are earned and allocate using the same distribution as the salary accrual. Overtime should be allocated to the fund where the extra hours were recorded.
Secondments & shared positions
Record secondments with formal agreements stating cost-sharing. Allocate salary and benefits per agreement. For shared positions without formal split, require documented manager approval for the allocation basis.
Recruitment, severance & restructuring costs
Classify recruitment costs and severance according to donor rules; typically charged to the fund that bears the staff role’s primary responsibility. If organizational-wide, treat as administrative expense and allocate pro-rata.
Accrual & timing controls
Accrue salaries and benefits monthly for the period earned (not paid). Use payroll cut-off controls for month-end. Post accruals and reverse in the subsequent month if necessary.
GL structure & mapping
Use a central payroll control account with sub-ledger detail by employee and mapping to fund/cost-centre combinations. Maintain consistent COA codes for salaries, benefits, taxes, and allowances.
Timesheet standards & approval workflow
Define minimum granularity (daily/weekly), required activity codes, sign-off rules (employee + supervisor), and submission deadlines. Retain electronic or signed paper timesheets as supporting evidence.
Controls & supporting documentation
Retain employment contracts, funded job descriptions, payroll registers, timesheets, FTE authorizations, payroll tax filings, and allocation workpapers. Require Payroll Officer and Finance Manager review.
Donor restrictions & allowable costs
Verify donor agreements for staff-cost eligibility (direct vs indirect). Where donors restrict charging salaries, absorb costs in unrestricted funds or cross-charge to indirect cost recovery if permitted. Document and, if required, get donor approval.
Indirect (overhead) vs direct charging
Define which roles are direct program costs vs organizational overhead. Administrative staff typically allocated as indirect costs using a transparent basis (FTEs, eligible expenditure, or direct-benefit driver). Ensure classification aligns with donor policies.
Cost recovery & salary recharge policy
If recharging staff between funds, use inter-fund recharge journals with identical debits and credits and supporting timesheets. Apply recharge rates that include salary plus proportional benefits and taxes.
Immateriality threshold & rounding
Set a threshold under which minimal allocations are expensed centrally (e.g., < USD 50 per fund per month). Apply consistent rounding rules and disclose thresholds in policy.
Journal entry examples (monthly)
1) To record gross payroll: DR Gross Salaries Expense / DR Employer Benefits / CR Payroll Taxes Payable / CR Bank/Cash (net pay).
2) To allocate: DR Fund A — Salaries / DR Fund B — Salaries / CR Central Payroll Control. Include calculation schedule.
Reconciliation & month-end checklist
1) Reconcile payroll register to bank payments and payroll control GL.
2) Produce allocation schedule showing per-employee split and totals by fund.
3) Post allocation journals.
4) Management review & sign-off. 5) Archive supporting docs.
Systems & automation tips
Use payroll/ERP systems that support employee fund tagging, timesheet integration, and allocation posting automation. Exportable reports should show hours, FTE, gross pay, and benefit lines by fund.
Audit readiness & working paper contents
Include payroll register, bank pay-file, employment contracts, timesheets, allocation schedule, journal entries, approvals, and donor agreements. Provide trail from employee record to final funded cost.
Example calculation (compact)
Staff gross monthly pay = 4,000. Timesheet shows 120 hours (60%) on Fund A and 80 hours (40%) on Fund B. Allocation: Fund A = 2,400; Fund B = 1,600. Apply same split to related benefits and taxes.
When to seek donor clarification
If donor terms are ambiguous on staff charging, request written clarification or propose a treatment in the next narrative/financial report and obtain written acceptance. Keep donor responses on file.
Governance — review frequency
Review staff-cost allocation policy at least annually or when staffing, funding mix, or payroll legislation changes. Record revisions, effective dates, and approval minutes.

Additional charges commonly allocated in a multi-funder NGO (single-column)
Office rent / lease
Allocate by floor-space occupied by programs/cost-centres, headcount, or FTE share. Direct-charge where a room/office is dedicated to a single program.
Utilities (electricity, water, gas)
Allocate by sqm (space-based), headcount, or program operating hours. For program-specific facilities, charge directly.
Internet, data & hosting
Allocate by headcount, users per fund, or direct-usage metrics (GB consumed) if available. For dedicated project servers, charge direct.
Telephony / mobile / VOIP
Allocate by direct phone lines to programs, call minutes by program, or headcount. Use call logs for accuracy where possible.
IT equipment depreciation (PCs, servers)
Allocate depreciation by user assignment or program ownership. For shared equipment, allocate by FTE or usage hours.
Software licenses & subscriptions
Charge directly to the fund using the software if single-use; otherwise allocate by user counts, seats per fund, or usage metrics.
Office supplies & stationery
Allocate by headcount, transaction count, or direct issue records. Small immaterial items can be expensed centrally below threshold.
Printing, photocopying & repro
Allocate by print-job counts per program, estimated pages, or headcount. Use printer usage logs if available.
Vehicle purchase depreciation
Allocate depreciation by mileage logged per fund, or by agreed FTE driving share. Maintain mileage logs.
Vehicle running costs (fuel, maintenance, insurance)
Allocate by mileage or trip logs per program; for pooled fleets use FTE or trip-weighted allocation.
Travel & per diems (field visits, conferences)
Charge directly to the fund that authorised the travel. If travel supports multiple funds, allocate by travel purpose hours or days.
Workshop & training costs
Charge to the beneficiary program if training is program-specific; otherwise allocate across funds that benefited (attendance-based) or to admin if organisation-wide.
Recruitment & onboarding costs
Charge to the fund that funded the position or allocate across funds benefiting from the hire (FTE split).
Consultants & professional fees
Charge directly to the project commissioning the consultant; if multi-benefit, split by time record or contract statement of work.
Audit, accounting & compliance fees
Allocate by budget share, eligible expenditure, or headcount; external audit often allocated pro-rata on total expenditures or per donor reporting complexity.
Legal fees & contracts management
Direct-charge when related to a fund-specific contract; otherwise allocate as admin overhead (FTE or expenditure basis).
Insurance (policies: liability, asset, health)
Allocate by insured asset ownership (asset-related insurance), by number of insured staff per fund (health), or by eligible expenditure for general liability.
Monitoring & evaluation (M&E)
Direct-charge to programs when M&E is program-specific; shared M&E functions allocated by program size, visits, or number of indicators.
Security & safety costs
Charge to programs operating in high-risk locations directly; general site security allocated by site occupancy or program presence.
Warehouse, storage & logistics
Allocate by volume/weight of goods stored per program, number of shipments, or direct booking records.
Customs, duties & import handling
Charge to the importing program or split when supplies serve multiple programs; keep import docs as support.
Communications, outreach & publicity
Allocate by campaign beneficiaries or direct-charge to the program sponsoring the communication; shared brand costs allocated by reach or budget share.
Fundraising & donor engagement costs
Allocate to fundraising pool or assign directly to specific fundraising campaigns; apportion customer relationship management costs by donor engagement level.
Depreciation (office furniture, general assets)
Allocate by asset assignment to cost-centres or by square-metre if furniture is location-based.
Bank & finance charges (other than monthly bank fees)
Allocate transactional finance fees, merchant fees, and currency conversion costs to the initiating fund or treat as finance overhead and allocate pro-rata if unclear.
Headquarters / shared services recharge (HR, legal, IT)
Allocate via a formal recharge model — FTE, eligible expenditure, or activity drivers — with documented service level and rates.
Safeguarding, compliance & investigations
Direct-charge to the program concerned if incident-specific; otherwise allocate centrally or by risk-exposure metric.
Medical, staff welfare & occupational health
Allocate staff medical costs to the employing fund or to central staff welfare budget; use employee fund splits for shared staff.
Conference & meeting costs
Charge to the organising program; if multi-program, split by attendee count or relevant agenda time per program.
Translation & interpretation
Charge to the program requiring language services; for multi-program documents, split by pages or beneficiary counts.
Project supplies & small equipment
Direct-charge to the consuming program; use stock issue records for allocation.
Contingency & reserves
Typically not allocated monthly; held centrally and applied per approved emergency or donor guidance. If allocated, do so by risk-weighted exposure.
Environmental & disposal costs
Charge to the program generating waste or to HQ environmental budget for shared facilities; allocate by volume or asset life.
Security deposits & guarantees
Record on balance sheet and amortise costs to the fund that provided the guarantee or benefited from the arrangement.
Sub-grants & partner payments
Charge direct to the program funding the sub-grant; if shared, split according to underlying fund contributions to the sub-grant.

Table 4
Common practice to allocate each category cost in a multi-funder NGO (single-column)
Office rent / lease — Allocation practice
Allocate rent to funds using a space-based metric (square metres) where program teams occupy discrete rooms or floors; use headcount or FTE where space is shared and staff density varies; for field offices allocate direct to the program that operates the office. Maintain a site occupancy register and floorplan and attach a monthly space allocation schedule as supporting workpaper. If a donor funds a dedicated facility, charge directly.
Utilities (electricity, water, gas) — Allocation practice
When metered by site, allocate to the site’s primary fund. For shared sites, allocate by square metres, headcount, or estimated operating hours per program. For high-usage equipment (cold storage, labs), directly charge measured consumption to the program. Reconcile estimated allocations to total bills each month and adjust via a clearing account.
Internet, data & hosting — Allocation practice
Allocate fixed connectivity fees pro-rata by headcount or number of active user accounts per fund. Allocate variable usage charges (bandwidth/GB) by measurable consumption when available. Charge hosting costs directly to the program owning the content/service. Maintain user-seat lists and monthly usage reports.
Telephony / mobile / VOIP — Allocation practice
Charge direct to the fund where a mobile line or desk phone is provisioned. For shared corporate plans, allocate by number of assigned devices or by call-minute logs per program. For staff mobiles with mixed use, require monthly call logs or a fixed percentage allocation agreed with supervisors.
IT equipment depreciation (PCs, servers) — Allocation practice
Allocate depreciation to the fund assigned as the primary user of the equipment. For shared equipment, allocate by number of users, FTE share, or usage hours. Document asset assignment in the fixed asset register and update allocations when assets are reallocated.
Software licenses & subscriptions — Allocation practice
Direct-charge single-use licences to the consuming program. For multi-use subscriptions, allocate by number of seats assigned to each fund, or by usage metrics where available. For organisation-wide licences, allocate as shared overhead using headcount or eligible expenditure.
Office supplies & stationery — Allocation practice
Charge small consumables under an immateriality threshold to central admin. For larger or identifiable purchases issued to a project, charge directly using stock issue records. For recurring supplies, allocate by headcount or transaction count per fund. Maintain periodic stock count supporting allocations.
Printing, photocopying & repro — Allocation practice
Allocate using printer job logs (pages per project) where available. Otherwise allocate by headcount or proportion of departmental budget. For large program-specific printing jobs, charge directly and attach job orders/invoices.
Vehicle purchase depreciation — Allocation practice
Allocate depreciation to the fund that holds title or primary use. For pooled vehicles, allocate depreciation by recorded mileage per fund (logbook). If mileage logs are unavailable, use approved FTE driving shares or trip counts. Reconcile odometer readings at month end.
Vehicle running costs (fuel, maintenance, insurance) — Allocation practice
Allocate fuel and maintenance by documented mileage or trip logs per program; insurance by ownership (assign by funded program) or, if pooled, allocate pro-rata by mileage. For ad hoc program trips, charge trip costs directly to the commissioning fund with trip authorisation attached.
Travel & per diems — Allocation practice
Charge travel costs directly to the fund which authorised or funded the travel. When travel supports multiple programs within one trip, allocate by days or activities per program as shown on travel claim forms and travel authorisations. For participant lists with mixed funding, allocate per attendee funding source.
Workshop & training costs — Allocation practice
Charge directly to the program for program-specific training. For cross-cutting or organisational training, allocate by number of participants per fund, by headcount, or treat as capacity-building overhead and allocate via an agreed indirect cost method. Retain participant lists showing fund affiliations.
Recruitment & onboarding costs — Allocation practice
Charge recruitment costs to the fund that will employ the new staff. For positions supporting multiple programs, allocate by the agreed FTE split, with supporting HR authorisation. For organisational-wide recruitment (e.g., CEO), allocate centrally as overhead.
Consultants & professional fees — Allocation practice
Charge consultant fees directly to the commissioning program per contract/SOW. If outputs benefit multiple funds, allocate by time-recorded inputs or agreed percentage shares. For retainer arrangements providing shared services, distribute fees using usage logs or FTE-equivalent estimates.
Audit, accounting & compliance fees — Allocation practice
Allocate statutory audit fees pro-rata by eligible expenditures or number of audit reports required per fund. For fund-specific audit scopes, charge directly to that fund. Internal audit and compliance expenses may be allocated as overhead using headcount or eligible expenditure basis.
Legal fees & contracts management — Allocation practice
Direct-charge legal fees for fund-specific contractual work to the relevant program. Allocate general corporate legal costs as overhead using budgeted expenditure, headcount, or other equitable driver. Document approvals for legal engagements and cost allocation rationale.
Insurance (liability, asset, health) — Allocation practice
Allocate asset insurance to the fund owning the insured asset. Allocate staff health insurance by the employing fund or distribute across funds by FTE if staff work across programs. General liability insurance may be allocated pro-rata by eligible expenditure or headcount. Keep policy schedules mapped to GL codes.
Monitoring & evaluation (M&E) — Allocation practice
Charge M&E costs directly to the program if they relate to program indicators and activities. Shared M&E units should be allocated across beneficiary programs by number of site visits, indicators per program, or program budget share. Maintain an M&E activity log to support allocations.
Security & safety costs — Allocation practice
Charge security costs in high-risk projects directly to the program that requires the service. For site-level base security benefiting multiple programs, allocate by headcount on site or by number of program days on-site. Document security risk assessments and authorisations for cost allocation.
Warehouse, storage & logistics — Allocation practice
Allocate warehouse costs by volume (m3) or weight of goods stored per program, or by number of shipments handled. For shared logistics contracts, use shipping manifests or inventory issue records to allocate monthly charges. Keep stock movement reports as supporting evidence.
Customs, duties & import handling — Allocation practice
Charge customs and import-related fees to the importing program that will use the goods. For consolidated shipments serving multiple programs, split costs in proportion to value or quantity received by each program, supported by customs documents and receiving reports.
Communications, outreach & publicity — Allocation practice
Charge campaign-specific communications directly to the sponsoring program. For organisational brand or multi-program campaigns, allocate by programme reach, beneficiary numbers, or by budget share. Use campaign media logs and invoices to back up allocations.
Fundraising & donor engagement costs — Allocation practice
Directly charge costs of specific fundraising campaigns to those campaigns. General fundraising (donor relations, CRM systems) may be allocated across restricted funds treated as fundraising support or absorbed by unrestricted funds, per donor rules. Maintain donor cost centre reporting.
Depreciation (office furniture, general assets) — Allocation practice
Allocate depreciation for furniture and shared assets by space (sq m) or by cost-centre assignment in the fixed asset register. For furniture assigned to a program or site, charge depreciation directly to that program’s fund. Update asset locations whenever moved.
Bank & finance charges (transactional & FX) — Allocation practice
Allocate merchant fees, transaction fees, and FX costs to the initiating fund. Where intermediary or correspondent bank charges are unclear, treat small amounts as finance overhead and allocate pro-rata by eligible expenditure; for significant charges trace back to initiating transactions and allocate accordingly.
Headquarters / shared services recharge (HR, legal, IT) — Allocation practice
Operate a formal recharge model with published rates and service descriptions. Common allocation drivers are FTE, eligible expenditure, or direct-use metrics (IT seats, HR hires). Issue monthly recharge invoices with supporting service logs and require program acceptance.
Safeguarding, compliance & investigations — Allocation practice
Charge incident-specific safeguarding investigation costs to the program or budget that incurred the risk; otherwise allocate central safeguarding program costs as overhead or by risk exposure metric. Retain investigation approvals and donor notifications where required.
Medical, staff welfare & occupational health — Allocation practice
Charge employee medical costs to the employing fund for program-hired staff. For organisation-wide staff welfare programs, allocate by headcount or treat as a central HR cost. For shared staff, split by FTE proportion. Keep claims and approvals attached.
Conference & meeting costs — Allocation practice
Charge meeting costs to the organising program. If participants from multiple funds attend, allocate by attendee count per fund or by time allocated to fund-related sessions. Maintain attendee lists and agendas as support.
Translation & interpretation — Allocation practice
Charge language services directly to the program requesting translation. For documents serving multiple programs, split by pages or beneficiary count per program. Retain translation orders and version control to show allocation driver.
Project supplies & small equipment — Allocation practice
Direct-charge consumables and small equipment to the program that consumes them via stock issue or purchase order. For central stores, use stock issuance records to allocate monthly. Avoid allocating immaterial consumables centrally where practical.
Contingency & reserves — Allocation practice
Maintain contingency centrally; drawdowns are approved per policy and charged to the fund requiring the spend. Do not routinely allocate contingency monthly; where a reserve is pre-funded by a donor, treat as designated and charge per donor guidance. Document approvals for transfers from reserves.
Environmental & disposal costs — Allocation practice
Charge clean-up and disposal costs to the program that generated the waste; for general facility environmental compliance allocate to the site cost-centre (shared) by floor area or eligible expenditure. Maintain environmental compliance logs and disposal receipts.
Security deposits & guarantees — Allocation practice
Capitalised deposits remain on the balance sheet and should be attributed to the fund that provided them; amortised fees or guarantee costs charged to the benefiting program. Reconcile deposit balances to fund source.
Sub-grants & partner payments — Allocation practice
Charge sub-grants and partner disbursements directly to the program funding them. If a sub-grant is co-funded, split according to each fund's contribution share and retain the partner contract and fund source allocation schedule.
Merchant & online payment fees (donations, ecommerce)
For donor-received payment processing fees, charge to the fund that received the donation. For pooled fundraising platforms, allocate processing fees across restricted campaigns according to donation receipts net of fees, or treat as fundraising cost depending on policy. Reconcile payment processor reports monthly.
Data protection & privacy compliance costs
Allocate direct compliance costs (e.g., project-specific DPIA) to the project fund. Central privacy program costs may be treated as overhead and allocated by headcount or eligible expenditure. Keep DPIA reports and policy approvals attached.
Office cleaning & janitorial — Allocation practice
Charge cleaning costs by site. For multi-program sites, allocate by square metres or by headcount. For program-specific rented premises, charge direct to the occupying fund. Maintain cleaning schedules and vendor invoices.
Catering & hospitality
Charge catering directly to the organising program or meeting cost centre. For general office pantry supplies, allocate by headcount or include within office supplies overhead. Require expense approvals for hospitality and attach attendee lists.
Fuelcards & transport allowances
Allocate fuelcard costs and transport allowances to the employing program or to the program authorising trips. For pooled allowances, pro-rata by FTE or usage logs. Reconcile fuelcard statements to trip logs monthly.
Staff training & professional development
Charge program-specific training to the program; corporate training allocated as overhead by headcount or by benefiting departments. Keep training approvals and attendance records tied to fund allocations.
Safety equipment & PPE
Direct-charge PPE to the program requiring it (e.g., field project). If PPE is general site equipment, allocate by headcount or site occupancy. Attach equipment issue records or safety audit recommendations to support costs.
IT helpdesk & support — Allocation practice
Allocate recurring support contracts by number of supported users per fund or by ticket counts. For major system implementations benefiting specific programs, capitalise and allocate per asset policy. Provide monthly support usage reports as backing.
Teleconference / webinar platforms
Charge platforms directly to programmes running events; share platform subscriptions allocated by event usage or number of webinars per fund. Track event bookings and invoices.
Quality assurance & technical support
Direct-charge technical QA to the program commissioning the support. For shared QA teams, allocate by number of reviews per program or sample size; maintain QA logs to substantiate allocations.
Local community contributions / in-kind support
Record local contributions in project accounts where donor rules permit; allocate contributed goods or services to the program that received them. Provide valuation methodology, receipts, and partner confirmations.
Monitoring software & analytics platforms
Charge monitoring tools directly to programs using dashboards; for shared platforms allocate by user seats, queries run, or indicators tracked. Provide usage exports for monthly allocation.
Capital project costs (site build, upgrades)
Capitalize major site build costs to the balance sheet and depreciate by asset class; allocate depreciation to the fund that funded construction or to the fund that benefits from the asset according to usage. For multi-funder builds, apportion capital cost by agreed funding contributions and reflect in asset register.
Exchange rate gains & losses — Allocation practice
Allocate realised FX gains/losses to the fund where the underlying transaction originated. For unrealised FX movements on central balances, allocate according to the fund exposure (pro-rata by month-end cash balances) or central finance policy. Disclose treatment in financial notes.
Immateriality threshold & rounding — Allocation practice
Define a consistent materiality threshold (e.g., USD 25–50) below which allocation is charged centrally to reduce administrative burden. Apply rounding rules consistently and disclose threshold in the financial policy. Routinely review threshold for appropriateness.
General allocation governance — Key controls
Document allocation policy, approved drivers (FTE, sqm, transaction count, eligible expenditure), and responsibility matrix. Maintain a standard allocation workbook or ERP routine, require manager sign-off monthly, and retain supporting evidence (timesheets, invoices, logs) for audit. Reconcile allocated totals back to central GL control accounts each month.
Journal entry & working paper standards — Allocation practice
Prepare an allocation schedule that shows: total pool, allocation driver, inputs (staff hours, sqm, transactions), formula, per-fund amounts, and GL posting lines. Post one allocation journal per pool with cross references to supporting workpapers. Use clearing accounts to capture timing differences and document any manual adjustments with approvals.
Donor-specific restrictions & exceptional treatments
Before allocating, confirm donor terms: some donors prohibit charging certain overheads or require specific allocation bases. Where donor rules differ from policy, treat donor requirements as precedence and document the alternative treatment. Seek written donor approval for exceptions and retain correspondence in working papers.
Systems & automation recommendations
Use payroll, ERP, and expense systems that capture fund tags at transaction entry and can produce driver reports (timesheets, seat lists, transaction counts). Automate allocation postings where possible and retain the allocation logic/version in system change control. Ensure monthly exports of allocation inputs are archived with the GL posting.
Audit & transparency — Evidence pack
For each allocation provide an evidence pack: pool supporting invoice/bill, driver exports (timesheet extract, sqm schedule, transaction log), allocation workbook, journal entry, reviewer sign-off, and donor correspondence if relevant. Keep the pack accessible for internal and external audit for at least the organisation’s retention period.

Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) — Cost Allocation (Single-column)
Purpose & scope
Define the objective: to allocate shared and direct costs across funds and cost-centres fairly, consistently, and in compliance with donor rules. Scope includes salaries, benefits, bank charges, rent, utilities, IT, travel, M&E, depreciation, consultant fees, and other recurring/shared expenditure. Applies to all departments, field offices, and partner-funded projects.
Policy statement
All cost allocations must follow the principle of cost causation (benefit received). Direct costs charged to the fund that incurred them. Shared costs allocated using approved drivers (timesheets, FTE, sqm, transaction count, eligible expenditure, mileage) documented in this SOP. Donor-specific rules take precedence where conflicts arise.
Definitions & key terms
— Pool: aggregated cost category to be allocated (e.g., Bank Charges Pool).
— Driver: measurable basis for allocation (e.g., hours, sqm, transactions).
— Allocation schedule: working paper that documents inputs, formula, and outputs.
— Eligible expenditure: donor-allowable costs used as a driver.
Roles & responsibilities
— Finance Director: approves allocation policy, review & sign-off on material methodological changes.
— Finance Manager: oversees monthly allocation process, reviews exceptions, maintains templates.
—Payroll Officer / HR: provides timesheets, FTE splits, payroll registers.
—Cost Centre / Program Managers: validate time allocations and program-specific charges.
—Accounts Payable: posts initial pool invoices to central control accounts.
—Internal Audit: periodic review of allocation compliance.
Allocation hierarchy & precedence
1. Direct charge (100% charged to fund where cost occurred).
2. Time-based allocations (timesheets) for staff-related pools.
3. Agreed fixed FTE splits for stable shared staff.
4. Activity drivers (sqm, transaction count) where direct measures exist.
5. Pro-rata by eligible expenditure when other drivers unavailable.
6. Material donor restrictions override the above — document all exceptions.
Approved allocation drivers (examples)
— Staff salaries & benefits: timesheets / FTE splits.
—Bank charges: transaction count or eligible expenditure.
—Rent & utilities: square metres or headcount.
—Vehicle costs: mileage logs.
—IT licences: seats assigned or usage metrics.
—M&E: site visits/indicators per program.
Materiality & immateriality thresholds
Set threshold for allocating immaterial amounts to reduce admin burden (example: USD 25–50 per fund/month). Amounts below threshold booked to central admin expense. Threshold to be reviewed annually. Document rounding rules.
Monthly allocation calendar & deadlines
Day 1–3: extract central invoices and bank statements.
Day 4–6: collect driver inputs (timesheets, seat lists, transaction logs).
Day 7–9: prepare allocation schedules and draft journals.
Day 10–11: Program Manager validations.
Day 12–14: Finance Manager review and posting.
Day 15: Monthly close reconciliations. (Adjust dates to local month length and payroll dates.)
Data sources & supporting evidence
List required inputs per pool: vendor invoice, bank statement, payroll register, approved timesheets, fixed asset register, mileage logs, printer/IT usage logs, workshop participant lists, travel authorisations. Each allocation must reference the exact supporting documents and file path.
Calculation & documentation standards
Use the standard allocation workbook template. Each allocation schedule must show: pool total, driver type, raw inputs (with export timestamps), calculation formula, per-fund amounts, GL lines, and preparer initials and date. Include a one-line rationale for choice of driver. Save workbook in central repository with month and pool naming convention.
Journal entry conventions
— Use central GL control accounts for initial pool posting.
— Post one allocation journal per pool with contra to central control.
— Journal narration must reference supporting allocation schedule ID.
— For reversals, post a reversing journal in the subsequent month and document reason.
Timesheet & payroll allocation rules
— Timesheets must record daily hours by activity and fund/project code.
— For recurring multi-fund staff, maintain an authorised FTE split form signed by the line manager and Finance.
— Apply the same split to salary-related benefits, taxes, and leave accruals.
— Where timesheets are late, use prior-month split as interim and flag for adjustment once timesheet received.
Treatment of donor-restricted items
— Review donor agreements before allocating. If a donor disallows a cost, do not allocate to that fund — charge to unrestricted/admin or seek donor approval.
— Maintain a donor exceptions register with correspondence and effective dates.
Reconciliations & control checks
— Reconcile sum of allocations to the pool total and to the central control GL monthly.
— Run reasonableness checks (e.g., allocations by fund do not exceed fund balances; per-employee allocations match payroll register totals).
— Flag and investigate variances > 2% or above defined tolerance.
Approval & sign-off matrix
— Preparer: creates allocation schedule (Finance Assistant).
— Reviewer: Finance Manager signs off on formula and inputs.
— Approver: Finance Director signs off on material reallocations or methodology changes (>USD X threshold).
— Program Manager: validates program-specific allocations for staff time and direct charges.
System & template requirements
— Use approved allocation workbook template stored in shared drive.
— ERP tagging: transactions must capture fund and cost-centre at point of entry where possible.
— Maintain version control for allocation logic and scripts.
Exception handling & adjustments
— Late supporting docs: book allocations using best available proxy and post an adjustment in the next month with full explanation.
— Donor dispute or retrospective donor restriction: set up an exceptions journal and engage with donor for resolution; maintain audit trail.
Audit evidence & retention
— Keep allocation working papers, supporting exports, journals, and approvals for the organisation’s retention period (minimum 7 years or as required by donor/local law).
— Structure folders by year/month/pool and index files by allocation schedule ID.
Training & competence
— Provide annual training for finance staff and program managers on allocation policy, timesheet completion, and use of the allocation workbook.
— Maintain an SOP change log with training dates and attendees.
Quality assurance & internal review
— Internal Audit to perform periodic sample testing of allocation schedules and supporting evidence.
— Finance Manager to run quarterly analytical reviews comparing allocation drivers over time to detect anomalies.
Donor reporting & disclosure
— Reveal allocation methods in donor financial reports where required.
— For material allocations affecting donor budgets, include explanation in narrative report and obtain donor acknowledgement if required.
Version control & policy review
— Review SOP annually or when there is a change in funding mix, significant process change, new ERP, or material donor policy change.
— Maintain a version history table with effective date, summary of changes, and approver signature.
Sample allocation workflow (step-by-step)
1. Extract pool invoice and post to central control GL.
2. Export driver data (timesheets, sqm list, transaction logs).
3. Populate allocation workbook with drivers and validate raw inputs.
4. Calculate allocations with embedded formulas; run automated totals check.
5. Obtain Program Manager validation for program-related items.
6. Finance Manager reviews and authorises the schedule.
7. Post allocation journal referencing schedule ID.
8. Reconcile and file evidence.

Escalation & dispute resolution
— Program managers raise allocation disputes to Finance Manager within 5 working days of posting.
— Finance Manager investigates; unresolved issues escalated to Finance Director within 10 working days. Document outcomes and corrective actions.
Change management & communication
