ISAK 35 / ISAK 335: Presentation of Financial Statements of Non-Profit-Oriented Entities
Purpose & objective
This chapter sets out the accounting, presentation and disclosure principles for non-profit-oriented entities (foundations, NGOs/CSOs, religious institutions, non-profit educational institutions, child welfare institutions, etc.) in accordance with ISAK 35 (renumbered ISAK 335 since 1 Jan 2024). Objective: to ensure the financial statements reliably and auditablely present the statement of financial position, operating results, changes in net assets and cash flows; to meet the reporting obligations to donors, regulators and stakeholders; and to facilitate inter-period comparability while adjusting terminology/presentation to reflect the non-profit character.
Scope
Governs the preparation and presentation of the five minimum financial statement components for non-profit entities that prepare financial statements under Indonesian Financial Accounting Standards (SAK — PSAK/ISAK). Applicable to all entities whose primary purpose is not commercial profit, regardless of legal form or funding source. Not intended to replace recognition/measurement rules in other specific PSAKs (e.g., property, plant & equipment, investments, employee benefit liabilities) — entities shall follow the relevant PSAK(s) for measurement and recognition.
Key definitions & core recognition principle
• Non-profit-oriented entity: an entity whose resources are provided without the expectation of proportionate economic returns to the providers.
• Net assets: the residual of assets less liabilities (replacement for the term “equity”).
• Net assets with donor restrictions: resources subject to donor-imposed restrictions (purpose, time or other conditions).
• Net assets without donor restrictions: resources available for general use by the organisation.
• Control of resources: the entity recognises donations/grants when it controls the resources and when donor conditions (if any) do not create a liability or remain unmet.
Core principle: statements are prepared on an accrual basis; classification, line titles and descriptions may be adapted to reflect the non-profit character without reducing the quality of reporting.
Core principles / Key principles
1. Comply with PSAK for measurement; use ISAK 35/335 for non-profit presentation adjustments.
2. Use the term net assets (amounts with/without restrictions) instead of equity.
3. Present the minimum financial statement components in accordance with ISAK 35/335 (Statement of Financial Position, Statement of Comprehensive Income, Statement of Changes in Net Assets, Statement of Cash Flows, Notes).
4. Separate and clearly disclose donor-restricted resources; reclassify only when restrictions are satisfied.
5. Maintain consistency of presentation between periods; disclose and explain any reclassifications and their effects.
6. Enhance transparency: disclose accounting policies for recognition of donations, measurement of non-cash benefits, allocation of shared costs and support services.
Governance & responsibilities (RACI)
• Board / Trustees: approve accounting policies, the annual report and policies for use of restricted funds.
• Director / Executive Director: ultimate responsibility for compliance and stewardship of donor funds.
• Finance Director / CFO: owner of policy implementation for ISAK 35/335, oversight of statement preparation, disclosures and auditor relationship.
• Accounting team: prepare journals, ledger, schedules for net asset classification and notes preparation.
• Programme managers / Budget holders: verify restricted fund use in accordance with donor conditions and sign allocation evidence.
• Internal Audit / Compliance: review compliance with classification and disclosure; sample donor→GL reconciliations.
• External Auditor: express an audit opinion on fair presentation and compliance with ISAK/PSAK.
Components of financial statements (mandatory & illustrative layout)
Minimum presentation:
• Statement of Financial Position — show current/non-current assets, current/non-current liabilities, and total net assets presented separately (with/without restrictions).
• Statement of Comprehensive Income — revenue (unrestricted/restricted donations), program expenses, administrative expenses, period surplus/deficit.
• Statement of Changes in Net Assets — movement of restricted vs unrestricted net assets; opening-to-closing reconciliation.
• Statement of Cash Flows — direct or indirect method; classification of operating cash flows should reflect non-profit character (e.g., receipts of restricted donor funding shown separately).
• Notes to the Financial Statements — accounting policies, details of donor restrictions, summary of major programmes, commitments/contingencies, and resource management information.
Presentation adaptations & acceptable terminology
• Titles may be adapted (e.g., “Statement of Changes in Net Assets” instead of “Statement of Changes in Equity”).
• Line items may be labelled to reflect sources (e.g., “Cash donations”, “In-kind contributions”, “Grants with restrictions”).
• Explanations must state that terminological adjustments do not change the underlying accounting principles. If management reports are added (e.g., budget vs actual or activity reports), state the basis of preparation in accounting policy.
Classification of net assets — robust guidance
• Net assets without donor restrictions: available for general use. Disclose policies for use of reserves/retained funds, restructuring funds and liquidity reserves.
• Net assets with donor restrictions: segregate by restriction type:
(a) restricted-purpose (program/project),
(b) restricted-time (multi-year grants),
(c) endowments (if applicable — perpetual/temporary).
• Transfers & satisfaction of restrictions: document the event(s) that satisfy restrictions (e.g., programme completion; passage of time) and reclassify in the Statement of Changes in Net Assets with narrative and supporting evidence.
Income recognition — detailed rules & examples
General rule: control + no substantial unmet conditions → recognise revenue. Specifics:
• Unconditional grants & donations (cash): recognise on receipt (or when control transfers).
• Conditional grants: recognise when conditions are substantially met (i.e., obligation/liability no longer exists). If conditions create a present obligation to return funds, initially recognise as a liability (deferred income).
• In-kind contributions: recognise when measurable and controlled (e.g., donated services only when recognition criteria are met — professional services that would otherwise be purchased). Provide measurement basis (fair value) and disclose valuation method.
• Exchange transactions (fees for service): apply relevant PSAK revenue recognition (measure and present as exchange revenue).
• Government grants / capital grants: present separately; capital grants are often deferred and amortised to match depreciation when restricted to asset acquisition.
Expense recognition & allocation (program vs support)
• Recognise expenses on an accrual basis by function and nature. Define and document allocation keys for shared costs (FTE, direct spend, timesheets).
• Program expenses: costs directly attributable to programme delivery.
• Support/administrative expenses: governance, finance, HR, fundraising — disclose allocation policy and thresholds for direct/indirect classification.
• Volunteer contributed services: recognise only when recognition criteria and reliable measurement are met; otherwise disclose nature and estimated extent.
Accounting treatment & sample journal entries (narrative + GL examples)
Journal narrative guidance: always include GrantID/DonorCode, ProjectCode, Period (YYYYMM), and whether the donation is restricted.
Typical entries:
1. Unrestricted donation received (cash): Dr 1010 Bank / Cr 4100 Donation income — Unrestricted. (Narrative: “Donation — Donor ABC — Unrestricted — GrantID G123 — 202512”).
2. Restricted grant received (cash) with conditions not yet met: Dr 1010 Bank / Cr 2100 Deferred grant liability (or Cr 4300 Net assets with restrictions if control is not conditional).
3. Recognise restricted grant on satisfaction of conditions: Dr 2100 Deferred grant liability / Cr 4300 Release of restricted net assets (or Cr 4100 Income — restricted).
4. Program expense paid from restricted funds: Dr 6000 Program expense (Project P001) / Cr 1010 Bank; (Narrative: “Program expenditure — Project P001 — Donor ABC — GrantID G123”).
5. In-kind asset donation recognised: Dr 12xx Inventory / Dr 14xx Fixed asset (if asset) / Cr 4100 Non-cash donation income (fair value); include valuation memorandum.
Notes & mandatory disclosures — suggested templates
Include: basis of preparation; policy on classification of net assets; policy for revenue recognition (conditional vs unconditional); disclosure of significant donors (amounts/% of revenue) if concentration risk exists; details of donor-restricted balances and purposes; movements in net assets (opening, receipts, releases, reclassifications, closing); related-party transactions; commitments, contingencies and subsequent events; operating leases/IFRS-16 implications if applicable; key judgements and estimates (valuation of in-kind, allocation keys).
Donor reporting & reconciliation controls
• Maintain a donor ledger mapping GrantID → Project codes → GL codes. Reconcile monthly donor reports to the GL and bank receipts. Disclose reconciling items and timing differences.
• For restricted grants provide a schedule: Opening balance; Receipts; Recognised income; Expenditure; Closing balance — supported by ledger and bank evidence. Ensure donor schedules do not omit SAK-required disclosures; donor schedules are supplemental and do not replace audited financial statements.
Audit expectations & audit pack (detailed)
Auditor focus areas: classification of net assets; timing of recognition for conditional grants; valuation and disclosure of in-kind contributions; donor→GL→bank reconciliation; completeness of commitments/contingencies; related-party and key management compensation disclosures; and adequacy of notes. Audit pack: trial balance with Grant→Project mapping; grant agreements; bank receipts & advices; movement schedule for net assets; sample donor reports; valuation memoranda for non-cash items; Board minutes for significant policy decisions; management representation on restricted use.
Common errors & mitigations (expanded)
Common errors: misclassifying restricted vs unrestricted; premature recognition of conditional grants; failure to recognise deferred grant liabilities; inadequate valuation/disclosure of in-kind donations; insufficient donor→GL reconciliation; omission of key judgements in notes.
Mitigations: standard chart of accounts with GrantIDs; automated donor ledger; mandatory deferred income account for conditional grants; policy templates for valuation of non-cash donations; monthly donor reconciliations with sign-off; staff training and periodic internal/external health checks.
Transition guidance & effective dates
• ISAK 35 was originally effective for periods beginning 1 January 2020; the numbering was updated to ISAK 335 per the SAK renumbering effective 1 January 2024 — the renumbering does not change substantive requirements. Entities should present comparative figures consistently and disclose the nature of any reclassifications made on transition.
Implementation checklist (immediate, short & medium term)
Immediate (0–3 months):
☐ Map current Chart of Accounts to Net Assets with/without restrictions.
☐ Create GrantID structure and donor sub-ledgers.
☐ Update financial statement templates (titles, line items) and accounting policy note.
☐ Run a retrospective sample for prior year restricted receipts — identify misclassifications.
Short (3–6 months):
☐ Implement deferred income workflows for conditional grants.
☐ Train programme & finance staff on recognition triggers and required documentation to release restrictions.
☐ Draft standard note templates (net assets movement schedule, donor concentration).
Medium (6–12 months):
☐ Automate donor→GL reconciliation with periodic dashboard reporting.
☐ Engage an external reviewer for ISAK 335 readiness and disclosure adequacy.
☐ Update SOPs and embed GrantID sign-off in the closing checklist.
Templates & repository (suggested standard files)
Maintain central, version-controlled templates:
• Chart of Accounts mapping to Net Assets categories
• Grant register (GrantID, donor, conditions, period, ProjectCode, reporting frequency)
• Net Asset Movement schedule (opening, receipts, releases, closing)
• Donor reconciliation worksheet (bank receipts ↔ donor reports ↔ GL)
• Valuation memorandum template for in-kind contributions
• Disclosure checklist for Notes (ISAK 335 compliance)
Sample note wording — Net assets with donor restrictions (example)
“Net assets with donor restrictions consist of grants and donations restricted by donors for specified programmes and/or future periods. Movements during the year are summarised as follows: Opening balance [IDR x]; Receipts [IDR y]; Amounts released to income on satisfaction of conditions [IDR z]; Exchange differences and other adjustments [IDR a]; Closing balance [IDR b]. The entity recognises restricted income as revenue when the related conditions are satisfied; where conditions create an obligation to return funds, such receipts are initially recorded as deferred income (liability).”
Mapping to GL & journal narrative standards
Require a standard narrative format for each journal: GRANTID | DONORCODE | PROJECTCODE | PERIOD YYYYMM | DESC | AMOUNT | NETASSET-W/WO-REST. Ensure GL postings include GrantID to allow drilldown. Establish mandatory fields in the ERP/GL upload for automated reporting.
Controls & segregation (finance / programme interplay)
• Maker/checker for recognition of restricted releases: preparer (finance) / approver (programme manager + CFO).
• Dual sign-off for write-backs and refunds to donors.
• Evidence required to release a restriction: programme completion report, approved statement of expenditures, or explicit donor waiver.
• Periodic third-party confirmation for large multi-year grants.
Special cases & guidance
• Endowments / Permanently restricted funds: treat according to donor terms; disclose investment policy and spending rules.
• Donor refunds & clawbacks: recognise as a liability when an outflow is probable; disclose contingencies and management estimates.
• Government grants with stipulations: apply conditional grant guidance; where stipulations relate to asset acquisition, defer recognition and amortise in line with depreciation of the asset.
• Domestic currency controls: disclose foreign exchange policies and presentation currency; recognise FX on restricted balances per PSAK guidance and disclose exposure.
Reporting to donors vs audited financial statements
Donor schedules and management reports are supplementary. Timing or classification differences must be reconciled and explained. Donor-specified formats must be provided but do not replace ISAK-required disclosures in the notes. Maintain an archive of donor agreements linked to GL evidence for audit.
KPIs, MI & dashboards (suggested)
• % of donor receipts reconciled to GL within X days.
• Number/value of restricted funds released outside approved criteria.
• Donor concentration metric (top 3 donors % of total revenue).
• Ageing of deferred income (conditional grants).
• Timeliness of annual audited statements vs statutory due dates.
Common audit queries & pre-emptive responses
Q: How was restricted income recognised? → Provide the grant agreement, donor conditions matrix, release evidence (scope of work, completion certificate).
Q: Why did classification change from prior year? → Provide the reclassification memo and impact on comparatives.
Q: What is the valuation basis for an in-kind donation? → Provide external/market valuations, internal calculations, and reviewer sign-off.
Training, capacity building & continuous improvement
Provide role-based training for finance, programme and executive teams on: recognition triggers, GrantID use, donor reconciliation, note drafting, and management of deferred income. Run tabletop exercises for complex grants and hold annual refreshers after major standard updates.
Common pitfalls, automated checks & mitigations (technical)
Automated checks to implement in ERP/GL: mandatory GrantID on revenue postings; block posting if the deferred income account is not used for flagged conditional receipts; variance alerts when restricted spending exceeds authorised budget; periodic integrity checks between donor ledger and GL totals; validation rule to require valuation memorandum attachment for non-cash postings.
Record retention & archive (audit readiness)
Retain grant agreements, bank receipts, donor reports, sample invoices/receipts, valuation memoranda, and Board approvals for the statutory minimum (commonly 6–10 years, or longer if donor or local law requires). Maintain indexed audit packs with a cross-reference table to facilitate sample selection.
Immediate recommended actions for country offices & small NGOs
1. Map all open grants → assign GrantIDs and classify as conditional/unconditional.
2. Update financial statement templates to show the net assets split and movement schedule.
3. Implement a deferred income account for conditional receipts and post opening balances where relevant.
4. Reconcile donor reports to the GL and produce an opening balance adjustment memo if necessary.
5. Prepare a sample audit pack for the most significant donor to test readiness.
References & further reading (authoritative)
Key sources and guidance: DSAK-IAI discussion paper / post-implementation review (PIR) on ISAK 335; PwC practical guide to the updated PSAK numbering and ISAK 335 illustrative examples; KPMG overview of SAK Indonesia (sections on ISAK 335). Review these documents for official wording, illustrative financial statements, and transition examples.

ISAK 35 / ISAK 335, Fund Accounting, Internal Controls and Related Guidance (Non-Profit Entities)
Purpose & objective
This consolidated chapter integrates ISAK 35 / ISAK 335 presentation rules with practical fund accounting, internal control, recognition and disclosure guidance for non-profit entities. Objective: ensure financial statements prepared under SAK present a reliable, auditable view of position, performance, cash flows and movements in net assets; ensure donor-restricted and unrestricted resources are tracked and controlled operationally; and provide a single, de-duplicated operational → statutory bridge for finance, programme and audit teams.
Scope
Applies to all non-profit-oriented entities preparing financial statements under Indonesian SAK and applying ISAK 35/335, and to country offices or programme units that receive restricted grants, donations, in-kind support or multi-donor funding. Covers presentation, classification, income/expense recognition, fund accounting design, COA/dimensions, internal controls, reconciliations, audit pack requirements, transition steps, and optional comparisons with IPSAS.
Key definitions & core recognition principle
• Net assets: assets less liabilities (replacement for “equity”), presented as with donor restrictions and without donor restrictions.
• Restricted vs unrestricted resources: donor-imposed purpose/time restrictions vs resources available for general use.
• Fund / GrantID / FundID: self-balancing account set or unique identifier linking donor agreements, budgets, GL postings and reports.
• Donor ledger: subsidiary ledger tracking receipts, recognition, expenditure and balances per GrantID.
Core principle: prepare statements on an accrual basis; operational fund accounting must produce audit-ready evidence to support presentation choices under ISAK 35/335.
High-level presentation requirements (ISAK 35/335 + PSAK 1 interaction)
Minimum statements: Statement of Financial Position; Statement of Comprehensive Income; Statement of Changes in Net Assets; Statement of Cash Flows; Notes. Use “Net assets” terminology. Adapt titles/line items to reflect non-profit activity (donations, grants, in-kind) while preserving PSAK measurement. Disclose accounting policies, key judgements and movement schedules for net assets.
Classification of net assets
Separate net assets into at least: (a) Net assets with donor restrictions (further split by purpose/time/endowment if material), and (b) Net assets without donor restrictions. Document policy for transfers, releases and internal designations; disclose movements in the Notes.
Income recognition — consolidated rules
1. Unconditional (non-exchange) receipts: recognise as income when entity obtains control and no substantive conditions remain.
2. Conditional grants: recognise as a liability (deferred income) when donor conditions create an obligation to return/refund; reclassify to income only when conditions are met — require documentary triggers (milestone acceptance, certified SOE, time elapsed).
3. In-kind contributions: recognise at fair value when control exists and measurement is reliable; for donated services, recognise only when they would otherwise be purchased and measurement is reliable.
4. Exchange transactions / fees for service: apply relevant PSAK revenue recognition rules.
5. Capital / government grants tied to assets: initially deferred and amortised in line with asset depreciation where appropriate.
Expense recognition & allocation
Record expenses on an accrual basis. Present by function (programme, support/administration, fundraising) and by nature where required. Define and document allocation keys for shared costs (FTE, timesheets, direct spend). Disclose allocation methodology in notes and reconcile to donor reporting where donors require effort reporting.
Fund accounting framework (operational design)
• Hierarchy: Entity → Fund/Grant (GrantID) → Programme/Project (ProjectCode) → Activity/Cost centre.
• COA design: separate revenue accounts for unrestricted, restricted and conditional receipts; dedicated deferred income liabilities; expense accounts by function; net asset control accounts split by restriction.
• Dimensions: enforce mandatory GrantID/ProjectCode fields (not free text).
• Donor ledger: single source of truth per GrantID mapping receipts → recognition → expenditures → bank activity.
Controls & segregation
• Segregation of duties: receipt, recording, approval and reconciliation must be distinct roles.
• Maker/checker on restricted releases: preparer (finance) + programme confirmation + approver (CFO or delegated signatory).
• Bank controls: track restricted funds via sub-ledgers even when pooled bank accounts are used (virtual sub-balances or tags).
• Documentation: every journal must reference GrantID, donor, restriction status and supporting documents (agreement, SOW, milestone evidence).
Reconciliation & reporting
Mandatory monthly reconciliations: Bank ↔ GL cash; Donor ledger ↔ GL restricted net assets / deferred income; Donor reports ↔ accounting records (explain reconciling items). Produce fund movement schedules for Notes: Opening balance → Receipts → Releases/Recognition → Expenditure → Closing balance. Maintain an indexed, audit-ready reconciliation pack per major grant.
Accounting treatment & concise sample journals
Standard narrative format: GrantID | DonorCode | ProjectCode | Period YYYYMM | Desc.
1. Receipt — unrestricted donation (cash): Dr Bank / Cr Income — Unrestricted. Narration: Donation — Donor X — Unrestricted — G001 — 202512.
2. Receipt — conditional grant (conditions unmet): Dr Bank / Cr Deferred income (liability).
3. Release on condition satisfied: Dr Deferred income / Cr Income — Restricted (or Dr Deferred income / Cr Net assets with restrictions, depending on policy).
4. Program expenditure (paid from restricted funds): Dr Program expense (Project P001) / Cr Bank; include GrantID in narration.
5. In-kind asset received: Dr Fixed asset / Cr Non-cash donation income (fair value) — attach valuation memo.
Notes and mandatory disclosures (consolidated checklist)
Basis of preparation; policy on classification of net assets; treatment of conditional grants and deferred income; in-kind valuation policy; movement schedule for net assets; major donor concentration (top donors %); related-party transactions; commitments, contingencies, subsequent events; key judgements and estimates; reconciliation of donor schedules to audited amounts where material.
Audit pack & evidence standards
Pack should include Trial Balance by GrantID, original grant agreement, bank receipts, donor reports, movement schedule, valuation memoranda for non-cash items, sign-off evidence for restricted releases, Board minutes for significant policy decisions, and management representation on restricted use. Ensure cross-references index for auditor sampling.
Transition guidance (PSAK 45 → ISAK 35 → ISAK 335 renumbering)
Identify legacy classifications, map COA to Net Asset categories, prepare reclassification journal entries with memos, restate comparatives where required and disclose nature of change. Note: renumbering to ISAK 335 (effective 1 Jan 2024) did not change substantive requirements — disclose accordingly.
Linkage to key PSAK measurement rules
ISAK 35/335 governs presentation/disclosure only; measurement follows PSAK standards: PSAK 16 (PPE), PSAK 24 (Employee benefits), PSAK 71 (Financial instruments), PSAK 73 (Leases), etc. Ensure fund accounting entries align with measurement and impairment rules under these PSAKs.
Donor reporting vs statutory reporting — governance
Donor schedules are supplemental — reconcile and explain timing/classification differences. Donor formats must not override accounting recognition; maintain archive linking donor reports to GL evidence. Establish pre-agreed reconciling templates for major donors.
Controls automation & system rules (recommended)
ERP/GL controls: mandatory GrantID on revenue postings; block reclassification of flagged conditional receipts until evidence uploaded; deferred income aging dashboard; validation rule requiring valuation memo uploads for non-cash entries; alerts for restricted spending > budget.
KPIs, MI & dashboards
Suggested KPIs: % donor receipts reconciled to GL within X days; ageing of deferred income; donor concentration metric (top 3 donors %); number/value of restricted releases outside approved criteria; timeliness of audited statements. Use dashboards for monitoring and escalations.
Common errors & mitigations (single list)
Errors: premature income recognition; misclassification restricted vs unrestricted; missing deferred income accounts; pool-mixing of funds; inadequate documentary evidence for releases; donor→GL reconciliation gaps.
Mitigations: enforce GrantID, donor condition matrix, deferred income workflow, standard narrative templates, mandatory reconciliation sign-offs, targeted training and periodic internal/external health checks.
Record retention & audit readiness
Retain grant agreements, bank receipts, donor reports, valuation memos, sample invoices, release evidence and Board minutes per statutory/donor retention requirements (commonly 6–10 years or longer if required). Maintain indexed electronic audit packs with immutable version control.
Implementation roadmap (prioritised & non-duplicative)
Immediate (0–3 months): map donors/grants and assign GrantIDs; update COA and FS templates; implement deferred income account; start monthly donor→GL→bank reconciliations for material grants.
Short (3–6 months): enforce mandatory GrantID in ERP, deploy deferred income aging dashboard, train programme/finance on release triggers and evidence requirements.
Medium (6–12 months): automate donor ledger → GL integrations, deploy dashboards/KPIs, conduct mock audit readiness review and adjust SOPs.
Special cases & contingency guidance
Endowments: disclose investment and spending policies. Clawbacks/refunds: recognise liability when outflow probable and disclose contingencies. Pooled bank accounts: use sub-ledger tagging to preserve fund traceability. Foreign currency controls: disclose FX policies and treat FX on restricted balances per PSAK rules.
Conclusion
A consolidated, non-duplicated operational and statutory framework linking fund accounting, controls and ISAK 35/335 presentation reduces audit risk, improves donor confidence, and ensures financial statements are accurate and supportable. Implement the phased roadmap, enforce system controls, and maintain audit-ready documentation to operationalise this framework.
