Answers
Does the change of exchange rate increase or decrease your spending power locally? Which types of costs benefit most? — Year-2 FX (4 Flurin/USD) increases USD purchasing power locally versus a lower rate (e.g., 3.5). Biggest beneficiaries: locally-priced items and services (local salaries/wages, field allowances, venue hire, catering, local transport, consumables, local maintenance). Little or no benefit for USD-priced imports, foreign supplier invoices, or any costs contractually denominated in USD.
What risk does the exchange rate create when reporting back in USD? — FX creates reporting volatility and apparent variances: local spending converted at a different rate can show a USD underspend or overspend despite correct local execution; realized/unrealized FX gains or losses when converting balances; mismatch between budgeted and reporting rates; potential audit or compliance queries if conversion method differs from funder rules. Mitigations: agree a conversion rule with the funder (e.g., monthly average or official funder rate), disclose the method and FX impact in the report, include an FX sensitivity table, and hold a small contingency for conversion differences.
Why inflation matters more later in the grant? — Later-year costs reflect cumulative/compounded price increases and statutory changes (wages, fringe, supplier price rises). Because Year-1 activity was delayed, more purchasing and hiring shifts into Years-2/3, concentrating exposure to higher future inflation; renewals and contracts executed later will be priced at higher nominal rates, so real purchasing power erodes and budget shortfalls are more likely without adjustment.

Options to propose (clear, selectable): A — Carryforward approval. Permit unspent Year-1 funds and committed POs to be carried into Year-2 to complete delayed activities (preferred if funder will not add cash). B — PO accrual recognition. Treat issued POs as committed/accrued for Year-1 reporting where invoices are pending (provide POs, shipping/ETA). C — Restricted virements. Allow reallocation of cancelled workshop / travel savings into other non-Personnel-Research categories (explicit exclusion: Personnel-Research). D — No-cost extension. Extend end date to complete delayed activities without adding funds. E — Targeted top-up. One-off incremental funding to cover demonstrable cost increases (incremental fringe + net inflation on forecasted spend). F — Fix FX conversion method. Agree a conversion rule for reporting (monthly average or funder’s published rate) and accept an FX sensitivity annex.
Preferred package to request (practical & low friction): Ask for A + B + C + F together (carryforward + PO accrual + limited virements + agreed FX rule). Rationale: preserves project delivery without immediate extra cash, resolves Year-1 reporting and reduces audit risk. Offer D or E as secondary options only if funder cannot approve carryforward/accruals.
What to include with the request: Year-1 actuals, Column-F justifications, list of committed POs with dates/ETAs, recruitment timelines, revised Year-2 forecast showing impact of fringe (20%), local inflation assumptions, FX sensitivity table (USD impact at 3.5 vs 4.0), and a one-page summary of the requested package and alternatives.

Personnel-Research), and (F) confirm your preferred FX conversion method; if not, please advise whether you prefer option D (no-cost extension) or E (targeted top-up).”
Decision rule / internal fallback: If funder approves package A+B+C+F → implement reforecast and proceed. If funder rejects carryforward/accruals but offers D (extension) → accept and rephase activities. Additional examples of challenges (Year-1 → Year-2 grant context)
1) Cashflow shortfall from delayed advance — disrupts recruitment, supplier payments; mitigation: short-term bridging loan or rephasing of activities.
2) Bank transfer delays and high bank charges — slower vendor payments and higher USD costs; mitigation: consolidate payments, negotiate fees, use local payment rails.
3) Exchange control / repatriation restrictions — inability to convert Flurin to USD when required; mitigation: hold FX buffer, agree reporting currency treatment with funder.
4) Import/customs clearance delays for equipment — extended delivery timelines and demurrage costs; mitigation: use experienced freight forwarder & include incoterm clarity in POs.
5) Price escalation for imported equipment (USD-denominated supplier increases) — budget shortfall for capital items; mitigation: seek supplier price locks or request donor top-up/alternative procurement.
6) VAT / tax treatment changes or unexpected local taxes — increased project cost or blocked recoverability; mitigation: obtain tax advice and include tax provisions or request exemption letters.
7) Fringe/benefit law changes mid-grant (beyond expected) — sudden personnel cost jump; mitigation: quantify incremental cost and propose funder top-up or internal reallocation.
8) Contractual or procurement non-compliance (wrong tendering) — audit finding and repayment risk; mitigation: remedial procurement actions and retrospective approvals.
9) Virement restrictions (e.g., Personnel-Research locked) — inability to move funds to cover overruns elsewhere; mitigation: request specific approvals or use allowed categories.
10) Staff turnover after late hires — productivity loss, training costs, recruitment delays; mitigation: overlap contracts, offer retention incentives, document handover.
11) Ethical approvals / regulatory delays for fieldwork — postponement of data collection; mitigation: fast-track approvals, partial remote data collection, timeline reforecast.
12) Data loss or equipment damage in transit — cost to replace and data gap; mitigation: insurance, backup procedures, POs with supplier liability clauses.
13) Local supplier insolvency — supply chain disruption and procurement restart; mitigation: identify alternative suppliers and pre-qualify backups.
14) Currency volatility between budget & report periods — apparent USD variances and audit queries; mitigation: agreed FX policy, sensitivity annex, small contingency.
15) Unplanned statutory payments (social security increases, severance) — unbudgeted liabilities; mitigation: model scenarios and request partial funding or reprioritise non-critical spend.
16) Reduced participant engagement (low workshop attendance) — wasted budget and weaker outcomes; mitigation: hybrid delivery, adjust participant incentives, reallocate PSD.
17) Foreign travel restrictions or visa delays — cancelled field missions and delayed outputs; mitigation: remote alternatives, local consultants, schedule adjustment.
18) Bank account / signatory changes causing payment hold-ups — stalled vendor/invoice settlement; mitigation: maintain backup signatories and immediate governance updates.
19) Audit timing mismatch (funder audit due while invoices pending) — disputed costs or questioned accruals; mitigation: prepare supporting docs, request audit timeline alignment.
20) Project scope creep driven by partner requests — budget and timeline pressure; mitigation: formal change control with funder approval and prioritisation.
21) Inadequate financial systems or weak bookkeeping — reporting delays and errors; mitigation: temporary accounting support, tighten controls, provide training.
22) Misalignment between technical and finance teams — mismatched forecasts vs operational reality; mitigation: joint monthly forecasts and reconciliation meetings.
23) Security or political disruption in field locations — suspension of field activities and staff safety risks; mitigation: contingency planning, relocate activities, insurance.
24) Donor policy changes (reporting frequency, eligible costs) — need for rapid reforecast and compliance work; mitigation: request clarification and submit revised budgets.
25) Cumulative inflation & delayed spend concentration — real funding shortfall in later years; mitigation: model inflation scenarios, propose top-up or no-cost extension.

Programme budget build, review, upload and verification
Executive summary (one line): Produce an auditable, correctly coded and monthly-phased programme budget, upload to D365, verify uploaded figures against approved budget, fix any mismatches, archive working papers, and hand the budget into monthly BvA and quarterly forecasting processes.
Purpose / Objective (expanded): Ensure each programme budget: 1) reflects programme objectives and deliverables; 2) contains complete resource estimates (staff, goods, services, assets, partner costs); 3) is coded to OUCRU account structure and project IDs; 4) is phased monthly for implementation and procurement timing; 5) is uploaded to D365 using the approved post-back template; 6) passes verification reports that reconcile uploaded figures to approved budget totals; 7) has a complete audit pack archived.
Scope (detailed):
In scope: programme-level budget build, line-level cost estimation, monthly phasing, OUCRU coding, donor currency conversion decision, review cycles, sign-off, D365 post-back upload, verification and re-upload cycles, archiving.
Out of scope: actual payment processing, supplier invoicing, vendor master setup, GL posting rules implementation beyond upload, routine monthly BvA adjustments once budget is live (those are downstream processes).
Trigger (start event / frequency):
- Annual budget cycle kick-off, or
- New programme setup request, or
- Major reforecast trigger (material change).
Record trigger date and responsible owner in the cover sheet.
Primary outcome / success criteria (measurable):
- D365 uploaded budget equals approved budget within tolerance (0% tolerance on totals recommended).
- Verification report: all cost-centre/project totals match.
- Zero critical coding errors.
- Archive contains signed approval, calculation sheets, exchange-rate log, and verification evidence.
Primary roles (RACI-lite, responsibilities):
- Programme Lead / Budget Owner: provides objectives, review/approve draft, sign final. (R)
- Budget Preparer / Costing Owner: build lines, phasing, assumptions docs, produce upload template. (A)
- Finance (Consolidation & Validation): assign coding, consolidate, run verification, escalate errors. (C)
- Support functions (Procurement/HR/IT): provide input on lead times, salaries, licensing costs. (C)
- Management Approver(s): required escalated approvals per DAO. (A)
- D365 Operator / System Admin: execute uploads, provide upload logs, support re-uploads. (R)
- Archive / Audit Owner: store working papers & sign-off records. (I)
Inputs (expanded):
- Programme objectives & outputs (document).
- Activities & deliverables list (workplan).
- Resource list: staff FTEs, goods, services, assets, partners & unit definitions.
- Procurement schedule & lead times.
- Quantity & unit-cost schedules; calculation sheets.
- OUCRU chart of accounts, cost-centre list, project list, DEA codes.
- Exchange rates (approval date rate / method).
- Donor currency instructions (if donor-specific).
- D365 post-back template and upload spec.
- Prior-year budgets / reference templates (if available).
Outputs (expanded):
- Approved programme budget (line-level) with OUCRU coding and monthly phasing.
- Final D365 upload file (.csv/.xlsx) and upload log.
- Verification report(s): pre- and post-upload.
- Approval records: signed draft, management approvals.
- Audit pack: calculation sheets, assumptions, exchange-rate log, reviewer comments, re-upload history.
- Handover note to Reporting for Monthly BvA and Forecast cycles.
Preconditions (what must be true before start):
- Programme exists and has a project ID.
- OUCRU chart of accounts and cost centre master accessible.
- Budget templates & upload spec accessible.
- Required inputs from programme (workplan, procurement timing) available.
- Exchange rates defined for budget period or agreed conversion rule.
Postconditions (state after finish):
- Budget lines present in D365 and reconciled to approved totals.
- Working papers archived and retrievable.
- Budget visible in reporting feeds and forecasting models.
High-level steps — detailed with sub-steps, roles & SLAs:
1. Define programme objectives & outputs — Programme Lead: provide written objectives and measurable outputs. SLA: within 3 business days of request.
2. List activities & deliverables — Programme Lead / Preparer: map activity → deliverable → cost-driver. Deliverable IDs required.
3. Identify inputs — Preparer: list staff FTEs, consultants, goods, assets, partner costs. Attach unit definitions.
4. Estimate quantities & unit costs — Preparer: use historical rates or supplier quotes; show calculation lines and assumptions. Mark any estimates as provisional.
5. Assign OUCRU IDs & codes — Finance: map each line to Account / Cost centre / Project / DEA. Use lookup table. SLA: 2 business days for mapping.
6. Decide transaction & donor currency — Preparer + Finance: determine transaction currency and donor conversion approach (spot at approval, monthly average, donor-specified). Document method and date used for exchange rates.
7. Monthly phasing — Preparer: phase by expected delivery dates and procurement lead-times (include procurement lag factor column).
8. Document assumptions & risks — Preparer: produce assumptions sheet (rates, inflation, contingency, exchange), risk register with mitigation and monetary impact.
9. Joint review (Finance + Support) — Finance convenes review, capture changes in review log. SLA: 5 business days to resolve routine comments.
10. If adjustments required — Preparer revises; return to review until settled. Keep version control.
11. Budget holder signs draft — Budget Holder: sign-off with date and version.
12. Finance consolidation & validation — Finance consolidates programmes, runs validation scripts (sum checks, coding checks, currency checks).
13. Management approvals — route higher-level approvals per delegation of authority. Record approver and date.
14. Prepare post-back template & Upload to D365 — Finance/D365 Operator: populate template, validate locally (pre-upload validation), upload. Keep upload log and system response file.
15. Run verification reports — Finance/D365: run verification reports that compare D365 balances to approved budget by project/cost centre/account/month.
16. If mismatch → fix & re-upload — diagnose cause (mapping, rounding, currency), correct template, re-upload. Limit re-upload cycles; escalate if >3.
17. Budget holder confirms upload — Budget Holder signs confirmation that upload matches.
18. Archive working papers & audit pack — include signed approvals, calc sheets, exchange-rate log, verification reports.
19. Handover to Monthly BvA & Forecasting — include notes on assumption, conversion method, and known limitations.
Decision points — scripts & acceptance criteria:
- Transaction currency & conversion method: choose method that aligns with donor contracts or internal policy. Acceptance: documented method and rate date.
- Adjustments required?: Accept if all reviewers sign off or if deviations are minor (- Management approvals: escalate per DAO; approvals recorded in audit pack.
- Uploaded figures match approved budget?: Pass if totals by project/cost centre/account/month match exactly (or within pre-agreed rounding tolerance). If not, perform root cause and correct.
Pre-upload validation checklist (copy-and-use):
1. All mandatory fields populated (Project ID, Account, Cost Centre, DEA, Transaction Currency).
2. Monthly phasing columns sum to line total.
3. Line totals match quantity × unit cost.
4. No negative amounts unless authorised.
5. Exchange-rate column applied where donor currency differs.
6. No blank or duplicate line IDs.
7. Totals by project / cost centre reconcile to summary.
8. Filename & template version noted in cover sheet.
Verification report checks (post-upload):
- Totals by Project, Cost Centre, Account and Month match approved workbook.
- Reconcile number of uploaded lines to prepared lines.
- Sample 10 lines across high-value codes: verify description, qty, unit cost, currency, and coding.
- Currency conversion applied correctly (formula check).
- No unexpected rounding differences; if present, document cause.
Common upload errors & fixes (practical):
- Error: Missing Project ID → Fix: populate project field and re-upload.
- Error: Currency mismatch → Fix: re-check transaction vs donor currency and exchange-rate cell.
- Error: Duplicate lines → Fix: remove duplicates in source template and re-upload.
- Error: Post-back template version mismatch → Fix: use current upload spec; coordinate with D365 Admin.
- Error: Rounding causing total variance → Fix: identify rounding column, apply cent-level adjustment in a control line and annotate.
Exceptions, remediation & escalation matrix:
- Validation error (template): D365 Operator fixes template; if recurring → escalate to System Admin within 1 working day.
- Master data missing (project/cost-centre/account): raise master-data ticket; hold upload for that project or upload other projects; escalate if SLA breached (3 business days).
- Unresolved mismatch after 3 re-uploads: escalate to Head of Finance and Budget Holder for decision within 24 hours (options: manual journal, accept small discrepancy with justification, postpone).
- Late approval threatens reporting deadline: notify Reporting and Management; record risk and decision.
