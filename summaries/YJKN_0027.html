Accounting for Bonus Accrual

Bonus Accrual – recognition and subsequent settlement
A significant portion of staff remuneration at OUCRU includes contractual bonuses that are earned over a period but paid at a later date. In order to ensure that payroll expenses are recognised in the correct accounting period, OUCRU units are required to accrue bonus costs on a monthly basis. These accruals reflect the obligation arising from services already rendered by employees.
At each month-end, Heads of Finance at OUCRU units will prepare accounting journals to recognise the bonus expense incurred during the period and to record the corresponding bonus accrual liability. Upon payment, the accrual will be reversed accordingly.

Important points to note by Head of Finance
• Bonus expenses must be recognised monthly under the Approval Code in which the related employee services are rendered, regardless of the Approval Code to which the employee belongs at the payment date.
• Any differences between accrued bonus amounts and actual payments upon settlement must be adjusted to the expense of the payment Approval Code.
• The bonus accrual account is classified as a balance sheet liability and should be reconciled regularly. Any residual balance arising from exchange differences should be cleared to the relevant realised exchange gain or loss account.
• Monthly bonus accrual amounts are calculated on a proportionate basis, based on the applicable bonus type of each unit and in line with employee contracts and OUCRU policies.
• The Head of Finance may determine the currency used for bonus accrual journals.
• The Head of Finance may choose either option in Section B: Payment Journal, provided it is applied consistently across periods.

Payroll sheet modifications
The payroll calculation sheet will be updated to support bonus accrual calculations by adding two new fields:
• Bonus Accrual – Monthly: Captures the bonus expense accrued for the current period
• Bonus Accrual – Cumulative Balance: Tracks the total accrued bonus liability to date.
The monthly bonus accrual amount should be calculated based on the relevant month’s salary. Refer to Appendix 1 for the assessment of factors considered in determining the monthly accrual calculation

Consolidated comments

1. The policy is sound in concept and the full manual is much stronger operationally, but both documents still need clearer terminology, defined ownership, and a simpler distinction between policy, procedure, and system design.

2. The accounting treatment is broadly correct: monthly accrual, settlement at payment, and no leave accrual. The main policy gap is whether Option 1 and Option 2 are both allowed by default or one must be fixed as the standard treatment by unit.

3. The strongest improvement area is clarity in the calculation logic and examples. Appendix 1 and Appendix 2 should be rewritten so the assumptions, calculation method, and journal outcomes are easier to follow and consistent in format.

4. The full manual is well designed around RequestID and AccrualRegister, but it needs a clearer minimum mandatory pack: which fields, documents, and approvals are compulsory for every case, and which are optional or conditional.

5. The validation framework is useful, but the hard blockers, override conditions, and recommended checks should be separated more clearly so users do not treat every control as equally mandatory.

6. Journal mapping, FX, and payment variance treatment are practical, but the policy should state cleaner precedence rules for functional currency, donor reporting currency, payment currency, and exchange-rate source hierarchy.

7. The tax, payroll, aggregation, dispute, and restatement sections are sensible, but they need a more usable decision tree or escalation matrix for local teams, especially where jurisdictional treatment differs.

8. The automation design is strong and implementation-ready, but operational ownership is still not fully explicit. The policy should say who owns failure response, sequence service recovery, exception handling, and evidence pack completeness.

9. The controls, sampling, retention, and security sections are audit-friendly, but they would be stronger if each control linked directly to a test, evidence requirement, retention rule, and responsible owner.

10. Overall, the documents are strong as a master operating model. The main next step is simplification and normalization: reduce ambiguity, define the minimum mandatory standard, and make the policy easier for Finance teams to apply consistently without interpretation differences.

1. Purpose
This policy establishes the minimum mandatory accounting treatment for contractual employee bonus accruals across OUCRU units. The objective is to ensure bonus-related payroll expense is recognised in the correct accounting period, the related liability is recorded consistently across units, and supporting evidence is sufficient for management review, internal control, external audit, and donor scrutiny.

2. Scope
This policy applies to:
• Contractual bonuses earned over a service period and paid at a later date.
• All OUCRU units processing payroll and related accrual journals.
• Bonus accruals charged to grants, approval codes, overhead, or intercompany arrangements where applicable.

This policy does not apply to discretionary bonuses unless specifically approved by Finance management in writing.

3. Policy Requirements

3.1 Monthly Recognition of Bonus Expense
OUCRU units must accrue bonus expense monthly based on employee services rendered during the reporting period.

At each month-end:
• Bonus expense must be recognised in the relevant payroll expense account.
• A corresponding bonus accrual liability must be recorded.
• The accrual must be posted to the Approval Code, Grant Code, or other approved cost object where the employee worked during that month.
• Any allocation method used must be supportable, consistent, and documented in the payroll calculation sheet.

3.2 Settlement of Bonus Accruals
When bonuses are paid:
• The accrued liability must be reversed or cleared using an approved settlement method.
• Any variance between accrued and actual payment amounts must be recognised in the payment period.
• Units must apply the selected settlement method consistently across accounting periods unless Finance approves a change in writing.
• Payment clearing must be traceable from accrual journal to settlement journal and bank or payroll evidence.

3.3 Currency and Exchange Treatment
• Units may determine the transaction currency used for accrual journals, provided the treatment is applied consistently.
• Exchange differences arising after settlement must be cleared to the relevant realised exchange gain or loss account, or another approved account where applicable.
• If donor reporting is required in a different currency, the reporting currency equivalent must be calculated using the approved exchange rate source for the reporting period.

3.4 Reconciliation Requirements
The bonus accrual account is classified as a balance sheet liability and must be reconciled regularly by the responsible Finance team.

Reconciling items must be:
• investigated promptly,
• documented with a clear root cause,
• assigned to an owner and due date, and
• cleared or escalated in a timely manner.

3.5 Evidence and Approval Requirements
Bonus accruals must be supported by appropriate evidence for the relevant employee, period, and allocation basis. Minimum evidence should include the calculation worksheet, payroll extract or contract reference, approval evidence where required, and supporting allocation or timesheet documentation where donor charging applies.

Monthly accrual calculations must:
• Be based on the relevant month’s salary.
• Follow employee contractual terms and OUCRU policy requirements.
• Apply the approved accrual methodology consistently.
• Be reviewed for reasonableness against prior months, expected payout, and known contract terms.

6. Accrual Methodology Guidance

6.1 Included Bonus Types
This policy applies to contractual annual bonuses, including:
• Vietnam 13th and 14th month salary bonuses
• Indonesia religious bonus and end-of-contract bonus
• Nepal religious bonus
• Other country-specific contractual bonuses approved by Finance

6.2 Excluded Items
No accrual should be made for untaken leave obligations because settlement patterns and future utilisation assumptions are too uncertain for bonus accrual treatment.

6.3 Factors Included in Monthly Accruals
Monthly accruals should generally be based only on the salary applicable to the relevant month.

The following items are normally excluded from monthly accrual calculations and recognised when incurred:
• Future salary increases
• Probation-related adjustments
• Exchange rate fluctuations
• Other immaterial or uncertain adjustments
• Discretionary amounts not contractually earned at the reporting date

6.4 Estimation and Override Rules
Where the contractual amount is known but final payment timing or allocation is uncertain, Finance may accrue based on best estimate using documented assumptions. Any override to the normal calculation basis must be approved and documented.

7. Approval Code Treatment
Bonus accruals must follow the employee’s Approval Code assignment during the service period.

If an employee changes Approval Code during the year:
• Monthly accruals continue under the new Approval Code from the effective transfer date.
• Payment-period variances are recognised under the current Approval Code and Grant combination.
• Where donor restrictions or funding rules differ, Finance must confirm the correct allocation before posting.

All supporting calculations, payroll schedules, approval evidence, allocation support, and journal documentation relating to bonus accruals must be retained in accordance with OUCRU document retention requirements.

Records must be retained in a way that allows the transaction to be traced from calculation to journal entry to payment and reconciliation, including any revisions or exceptions.

Comprehensive Bonus Accrual
Executive summary
This is an end-to-end master manual for monthly bonus accruals in complex funding environments (multi-donor NGOs, mixed legal-entity groups). It combines accounting principles (short-term employee benefits), donor compliance, measurement and allocation rules, operational procedures, automation architecture (Power Query + orchestrator + optional SQL), orchestration runbooks (non-code and code patterns), controls and test scripts, reconciliation procedures, audit-pack standards, KPIs, training/competency frameworks, security hardening, implementation roadmaps and continuous improvement practices. The single audit key is the RequestID (ACC-BONUS-YYYYMM-CC-####). The AccrualRegister is the canonical single table of record for month-end reporting, donor schedules and audit evidence. This document is implementation-ready for Finance, Grants, HR/Payroll, Internal Audit and IT/Automation teams. How to use: copy the AccrualRegister CSV header into ETL; adapt RequestID naming; run the operational runbook; apply validations; capture evidence; upload JE to ERP; archive the audit pack.
Purpose & outcomes
(1) Provide a consistent, auditable process to measure and record monthly bonus accruals for service rendered.
(2) Deterministically allocate costs across donors/projects/overheads.
(3) Produce donor-ready schedules and disclosures.
(4) Provide automation and tooling to enforce validations and evidence capture.
(5) Deliver audit packs meeting external audit and donor audit standards.
(6) Minimise financial statement restatements and donor disputes.
(7) Provide clear ownerable controls and remediation paths for variances.
(8) Build a continuous improvement loop that reduces manual exceptions and improves forecast accuracy.
(9) Ensure deterministic traceability from AccrualRequest → supporting evidence → posted JE → clearance.
Scope & exclusions
Scope: annual pro-rata, quarterly, spot awards, retention/contractual bonuses, sign-on/exit payments, commissioned/incentive arrangements (contract-defined), consultant contractual bonuses where contract stipulates payment; applies to HQ, country offices, sub-offices, branch offices, pooled funds, shared service centres and intercompany secondments.
Exclusions: equity-based compensation (RSUs, options), actuarial long-term employee benefits (defined benefit pension liabilities), litigated settlements until legally settled, payroll taxes requiring actuarial models.
Boundary rule: any arrangement with vesting or settlement beyond 12 months must be escalated to payroll tax and actuarial specialists for separate treatment.
Local law note: always consult local payroll/tax teams for statutory exceptions; document legal opinion in RequestID folder.
Accounting framework & principles
Recognition principle: recognise undiscounted liabilities and matching expense for bonuses when service is rendered and a present obligation exists (short-term employee benefit).
Measurement principle: measure at the best estimate of the amount payable; document assumptions, drivers and evidence.
Standards references: IAS 19 / local GAAP short-term employee benefits; where local practice diverges, document deviations and approvals.
Presentation & disclosure: record accruals as current liabilities (Accrued Bonuses) and present expense under staff costs; disclose measurement policies, donor-specific treatments, and material restatements.
Judgement policy: any management judgement that materially affects accrual must be described in the calculation worksheet and supported by a rationale signed by Country FD.

Calculation: MonthlyAccrual = EstimatedAnnualBonus × (ServiceFractionForMonth / 12) × EligibilityFactor − AdvancesAllocatedThisMonth.
ConfidenceLevel: High (contract + timesheet), Medium (HR estimate + manager cert), Low (management estimate) — route to approval tiers based on confidence.
Audit rule: save calculation workbook and M-code snapshot in RequestID folder.
Rounding: use country-specific rounding rules and record residual distribution policy (usually overhead).
