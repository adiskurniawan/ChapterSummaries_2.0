Accounting for Bonus Accrual

Bonus Accrual – recognition and subsequent settlement
A significant portion of staff remuneration at OUCRU includes contractual bonuses that are earned over a period but paid at a later date. In order to ensure that payroll expenses are recognised in the correct accounting period, OUCRU units are required to accrue bonus costs on a monthly basis. These accruals reflect the obligation arising from services already rendered by employees.
At each month-end, Heads of Finance at OUCRU units will prepare accounting journals to recognise the bonus expense incurred during the period and to record the corresponding bonus accrual liability. Upon payment, the accrual will be reversed accordingly.

Important points to note by Head of Finance
• Bonus expenses must be recognised monthly under the Approval Code in which the related employee services are rendered, regardless of the Approval Code to which the employee belongs at the payment date.
• Any differences between accrued bonus amounts and actual payments upon settlement must be adjusted to the expense of the payment Approval Code.
• The bonus accrual account is classified as a balance sheet liability and should be reconciled regularly. Any residual balance arising from exchange differences should be cleared to the relevant realised exchange gain or loss account.
• Monthly bonus accrual amounts are calculated on a proportionate basis, based on the applicable bonus type of each unit and in line with employee contracts and OUCRU policies.
• The Head of Finance may determine the currency used for bonus accrual journals.
• The Head of Finance may choose either option in Section B: Payment Journal, provided it is applied consistently across periods.

Payroll sheet modifications
The payroll calculation sheet will be updated to support bonus accrual calculations by adding two new fields:
• Bonus Accrual – Monthly: Captures the bonus expense accrued for the current period
• Bonus Accrual – Cumulative Balance: Tracks the total accrued bonus liability to date.
The monthly bonus accrual amount should be calculated based on the relevant month’s salary. Refer to Appendix 1 for the assessment of factors considered in determining the monthly accrual calculation

Consolidated comments
1. The policy is sound in concept and the full manual is much stronger operationally, but both documents still need clearer terminology, defined ownership, and a simpler distinction between policy, procedure, and system design.
2. The accounting treatment is broadly correct: monthly accrual, settlement at payment, and no leave accrual. The main policy gap is whether Option 1 and Option 2 are both allowed by default or one must be fixed as the standard treatment by unit.
3. The strongest improvement area is clarity in the calculation logic and examples. Appendix 1 and Appendix 2 should be rewritten so the assumptions, calculation method, and journal outcomes are easier to follow and consistent in format.
4. The full manual is well designed around RequestID and AccrualRegister, but it needs a clearer minimum mandatory pack: which fields, documents, and approvals are compulsory for every case, and which are optional or conditional.
5. The validation framework is useful, but the hard blockers, override conditions, and recommended checks should be separated more clearly so users do not treat every control as equally mandatory.
6. Journal mapping, FX, and payment variance treatment are practical, but the policy should state cleaner precedence rules for functional currency, donor reporting currency, payment currency, and exchange-rate source hierarchy.
7. The tax, payroll, aggregation, dispute, and restatement sections are sensible, but they need a more usable decision tree or escalation matrix for local teams, especially where jurisdictional treatment differs.
8. The automation design is strong and implementation-ready, but operational ownership is still not fully explicit. The policy should say who owns failure response, sequence service recovery, exception handling, and evidence pack completeness.
9. The controls, sampling, retention, and security sections are audit-friendly, but they would be stronger if each control linked directly to a test, evidence requirement, retention rule, and responsible owner.
10. Overall, the documents are strong as a master operating model. The main next step is simplification and normalization: reduce ambiguity, define the minimum mandatory standard, and make the policy easier for Finance teams to apply consistently without interpretation differences.

[REVISED] 1. Purpose
This note sets the minimum mandatory accounting treatment for monthly bonus accruals at OUCRU units. It applies to contractual bonuses earned over a period and paid at a later date.

[REVISED] 2. Recognition and subsequent settlement
A significant portion of staff remuneration at OUCRU includes contractual bonuses that are earned over a period but paid later. To ensure that payroll expenses are recognised in the correct accounting period, OUCRU units must accrue bonus costs monthly. • Bonus expenses must be recognised monthly under the Approval Code in which the related employee services are rendered, regardless of the Approval Code to which the employee belongs at the payment date.

• The Head of Finance may determine the currency used for bonus accrual journals, provided it is applied consistently and supported by the unit’s approved accounting approach.
