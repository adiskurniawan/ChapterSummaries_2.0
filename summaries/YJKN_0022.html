1. Purpose & Scope
This SOP defines mandatory procedures, controls and standards for preparing, reviewing, approving, posting and archiving journal entries across all ledgers and systems used by the organisation (D365, Agresso, GLACOS and approved offline tools). It covers: manual journals, recurring journals, accruals and reversals, prepayments, fixed asset capitalisations, inter-office recharges (2030/2031), FX revaluations, payroll journals and corrections including prior-period restatements. The SOP must be read alongside the Chart of Accounts, Data Maintenance Manual, Accounts Payable and Payments SOPs, and the Delegation of Authority (DoA). It applies to all staff, contractors and authorised agents who prepare, review, authorise or post journals. Failure to follow this SOP may result in transaction rejection, reversal, audit finding or disciplinary action.
2. Principles & Control Objectives
• Segregation of duties: Where possible, different individuals prepare, review and post journals. Posting must never be performed by the same person who drafted or authorised the voucher unless a documented compensating control exists and is approved by the Controller.
• Complete, auditable evidence: Every journal must have a voucher pack enabling a reviewer or external auditor to trace amounts to source records and reconstruct judgment steps.
• Mandatory coding: All lines must include the account, cost centre, project, SOF and DEA where required by the account rule. If the account requires an analysis (vehicle, staff, supplier), that analysis value must be provided.
• Clear narrative: A standard narrative format is required (see section 8) to ensure consistent interpretation and automated searchability.
• Timeliness & cut-offs: Routine journals should be posted in the month the economic event occurs. Accruals are to be posted at period end and reversed when final invoices are available per the reversal schedule.
• Audit readiness & retention: Retain vouchers per statutory and donor requirements; maintain an indexed archive with search capability and redundant backups.
3. Definitions & Roles
Definitions: Journal entry, voucher reference, preparer (initiator), reviewer, authoriser, poster, recurring journal owner, DoA, SOF (source of funds), DEA (donor expenditure analysis), GLACOS, DV (data validation).
Roles and accountabilities:
• Preparer (Initiator): Complete journal, include full attributes, working papers, narrative and required attachments. Run validation checks and submit to Reviewer.
• Reviewer: Confirm arithmetic, attribute completeness, donor rules and evidence sufficiency. Document review results and return or escalate as needed.
• Budget holder (BH): Approve business rationale and confirm budget availability. For reallocations BH approvals from both sides required.
• Authoriser: Confirms DoA compliance and signs/approves. Must not be Preparer. For thresholds above DoA limits, obtain further approvals per matrix.
• Poster: Perform posting/upload to ledger and capture posting reference. Validate posting results and update voucher pack.
• Controller/Finance Director: Maintain SOP and controls, run exception reporting, approve changes to recurring journals and carry out audit sample reviews.
4. Voucher Reference & Archive Standards
Voucher Reference (mandatory): Format YYYYMMDD_JNL_<OFFCODE>_<NNNN> where YYYYMMDD = creation or period-end date, OFFCODE = 5-digit cost centre or office code, <NNNN> = sequential zero-padded number. Example: 20260331_JNL_03100_0045.
Placement: Voucher reference must be the first string in the ledger transaction description field to enable ledger→file traceability and system searches.
Voucher pack contents: Final PDF voucher; calculations (spreadsheet); original supporting documents where available (invoices, contracts, GRN/DSN, delivery evidence, payroll register, bank advice, tax certificates); budget holder approval(s); reviewer notes; authoriser sign-off; posting confirmation (posting ID/batch or GLACOS upload log with checksum). If the voucher uses a newly created attribute or code, attach the Data Maintenance request and approval to the voucher file.
File naming: journals/YYYYMM/<voucherref>_<shortdesc>.pdf (shortdesc 3–6 words).
Index & metadata: Maintain CSV or ledger register columns: voucher ref, posting date, preparer, reviewer, approver, amount, journal type, account(s) affected, posting reference, storage path, retention expiry date, tags (e.g., accrual, capex, intercompany).
Backups: Primary repository (central finance drive) + offsite/cloud copy. Test restores quarterly. Retention meets minimum statutory/donor requirements (example minimum 7 years unless donor states otherwise).
5. Coding & Attribute Rules (detailed)
Mandatory attributes: Account (4 digits), Cost Centre (5 digits), Project (7 digits where required), SOF (8 digits where applicable), DEA (6 digits for expenditure), Analysis (account-specific). Country auto-populates from Cost Centre.
Account rules: Each account code has a rule defining mandatory attributes. Example: Bank (1000) requires bank account analysis; Payroll accounts require staff analysis. The system enforces these where possible; however the preparer must verify attribute completeness. If a required attribute is missing, stop and request Data Maintenance.
Project & SOF mapping: Projects and SOFs must map to donor award structure. DEAs are linked to SOF to prevent mis-posting. Use the Award Management mapping tool when assigning SOF/DEA.
Analysis field conventions: Examples — Staff: CCC000000000 format; Supplier: CCC00000 domestic or 99800000 for international; Vehicle: CCCV0000 etc. Follow Data Maintenance for naming conventions. Attach Data Maintenance approvals when codes were recently created.
6. Pre-Posting Checklist (expanded)
Mandatory for preparer and reviewer. The PREPARER completes initial checks; the REVIEWER repeats and signs. Checklist items:
1. Debits = Credits and currency totals match.
2. Voucher ref assigned and matches filename.
3. All GL attributes completed per account rule (account, cost centre, project, SOF, DEA, analysis).
4. Narrative meets quality standard (see section 8).
5. All required attachments present and legible (originals where required).
6. Budget holder approval present where required; alternative approver if BH is claimant.
7. DoA approval levels validated; escalations recorded where applicable.
8. FX calculations included for multi-currency lines (source rate, rate date, conversion method, workbook).
9. Recurring journal parameters validated (owner, frequency, cap) if applicable.
10. Reviewer signs and dates voucher pack and logs any exceptions.
7. Workflow — End-to-End (expanded)
7.1 Preparation (Preparer)
• Use the standard journal template. Assign voucher ref and populate header. Enter each journal line with account and all required attributes.
• Build working paper: show calculation logic, assumptions, source documents references and any mapping to donor categories (SOF/DEA). Keep version control in file names (e.g., v1, v2).
• Attach supporting documents (PDF scans) and ensure originals are archived if required by donor/statute.
• Run validation checks: balancing, DV validation for GLACOS, attribute completeness. For GLACOS, run DV spreadsheet and fix errors before upload. Save validation logs.
7.2 Review
• Reviewer executes checklist, validates donor/award rules, notes any differences. If material disagreement, escalate to Controller.
• Document reviewer comments inline on voucher PDF and record sign-off (name, date).
7.3 Budget Holder Approval
• BH checks business rationale and confirms budget and donor compliance. Record approval as email or e-approval; attach to voucher.
7.4 Authorisation
• Approver checks DoA and reviewer sign-off. Use electronic workflow where available. For high-value or sensitive accounts, require dual approval per DoA.
7.5 Posting
• Poster posts to ledger and captures posting reference and batch ID. For GLACOS uploads, include DV validation evidence and upload checksum. For D365/Agresso, record posting ID. Attach posting confirmation to voucher. Verify posting balances and attributes in ledger. If posting error occurs, correct via reversal journal with new voucher and approvals.
7.6 Archive
• Save final voucher pack to central repository and update index. Mark retention expiry. Create backup copy.
7.7 Reconciliation & Reporting
• Ensure journals affecting control accounts are included in monthly reconciliation files. Report exceptions to Controller; record remediation actions and dates.
8. Narrative Quality Standard & Short Templates
Every journal narrative must include: voucher reference (first text); the why (purpose); the what (accounting impact); period the entry relates to; reversal month (if temporary); original invoice/ledger references; preparer/reviewer/authoriser names and dates; for inter-office recharges include origin CC and recipient analysis and attach acceptance evidence; for donor-funded items the SOF/DEA and award reference.
Narrative template (copy-ready):
<voucherref> — [Short Title] — [Business reason / contract clause / explanation]. Amount: [currency X]. Period: [YYYY-MM]. Reverse: [YYYY-MM if temporary]. Prep: [Name] ([date]); Review: [Name] ([date]); BH: [Name] ([date]); Approver: [Name] ([date]). Reference: [INV_XXX / PO_YYYY / BNK_REF]. SOF: [xxxxxx]; DEA: [xxxxxx]. Attachments: [list].
Short example: 20260331_JNL_Accrual_001 — Accrual electricity Mar2026 — Estimate based on 3-month average; reverse Apr2026 on invoice receipt. Prep: A.Name (2026-03-31); Review: B.Name (2026-04-01); BH: C.Name (2026-04-01). Ref: utility_readings_Q1_2026.xlsx.
9. Recurring Journals — Register & Controls
Register fields: Template ID, Voucher ref prefix, Owner, Frequency, Last run date, Next run date, Amount cap, Variance threshold, Rationale, Authoriser, Last review date, Attachments location, System job (if automated), Notes.
Controls: Quarterly owner review required; variance > cap triggers stop-and-investigate before posting; changes to template or owner require Controller re-approval; maintain historical runs for audit; automated alerts for missed runs.
10. Corrections, Reversals & Prior-period Adjustments
Minor correction: Preparer corrects coding and obtains reviewer sign-off. Document change on voucher and keep versioned PDF.
Material correction / Prior-period error: Requires accounting memo, impact analysis, Controller approval and possible auditor notification. Follow applicable accounting standards (IAS/IFRS/GAAP) for restatement vs disclosure. Where restatement required prepare comparatives and disclosure notes.
Voids: Mark original voucher VOID and create replacement voucher referencing the voided voucher. Never delete original ledger postings. Maintain audit trail.
11. Control Accounts & Reconciliations (detailed)
Accounts: AP (2000), AR (1100), Bank (1000), Payroll accruals, Prepayments, Accrued liabilities (2035), Intercompany control (2030/2031).
Reconciliation process: Monthly schedule, preparer posts reconciliation with samples, reviewer signs, exceptions logged and remedied within 30 days. Include journal listing affecting the control account and link to source vouchers. Aged items >90 days escalate to Controller.
12. Inter-office Recharges (2030/2031)
Origin posting rules: Post to 2030 with origin cost centre and recipient analysis. Narrative must include: brief description, person incurring cost, period, original posting ref, recipient SOF/DEA and name of recipient BH. Example narrative format: 2030 Recharge - Taxi - origin CC 17000; recipient 999 (centre); Taxi from Airport 03-02-2026; amount USD 2,000; origin ref BNK_20260307_221; recipient email 2026-03-08 attached.
Recipient clearing: Post Dr Expense / Cr 2031 using original currency and original posting date of origin. Attach acceptance email. Reconcile 2031 monthly.
Rejection: Post back via 2030 with prefix Rejection <orig_ref> and include reason and contact details. Do NOT use 2032 for rejects intended for centre visibility.
13. Payments & AP Interaction
Journals that create payables or record payments must comply with AP process: invoice authorisation form, PO and GRN where applicable, VAT/faktur if supplier PKP, payment approvals per DoA and remittance confirmation. For bank transfers recorded in GL ensure bank advice is attached and the voucher is stamped PAID. Where the office uses Agresso AP module, follow remittance proposal and confirmation steps. Maintain segregation between posting invoices and executing payments.
14. International Payments & Supplier Setup
International payments are made centrally when: invoice currency not local and > USD 100,000; country lacks required currency account; local regulation prevents local payment; or excessive bank costs require central treasury handling. Use Data Maintenance to create international supplier records and attach KYC, bank confirmation and tax status to supplier file. For centre-managed international payments include centre payment reference in voucher.
15. Disallowed Costs & Reallocation
When disallowed costs occur: prepare reallocation journal with full justification and BH approvals; for material amounts require Country Director approval and escalate to regional/centre finance as required. Record disallowed flag in reporting packs and include correspondence with donor if needed. Follow formal reallocation workflow (Dr Expense (new SOF) / Cr Expense (old SOF)) with signed approvals.
16. Exception Monitoring, KPIs & Audit Sampling
Exception reports (monthly): manual journals >threshold, missing attachments, recurring journals overdue review, new code usage without Data Maintenance evidence, posters without approval. KPIs examples: % of journals with complete voucher pack at initial review, average time from prep to posting, manual journal volume by preparer, time-to-clear 2031 accounts. Audit samples: Controller selects top manual journals by value, recurring journals, intercompany and unusual or one-off journals for audit readiness.
17. System Controls, Security & SoD
• Enforce role-based access: preparers cannot post while also authorising for same voucher. Separate create, approve and post roles.
• Implement automated blocks on posting when required attachments not present for sensitive journal types.
• Maintain user access review monthly and remove access upon role change or termination. Use dual approvals for bank, payroll and intercompany postings.
18. Training, Change Control & Version Management
• Provide induction training for all finance staff and annual refreshers covering Chart of Accounts, DEA/SOF coding, system posting rules (D365/Agresso/GLACOS), Data Maintenance and this Journal SOP.
• Maintain version history with: version number, effective date, author, approver, summary of changes. Controller approves minor amendments; major updates require Finance Director sign-off and re-training.

21. Implementation checklist & operational actions
1. Publish SOP and link to Chart of Accounts and Data Maintenance Manual.
2. Configure ledger workflows to enforce voucher ref and required attachments for set journal types.
3. Create recurring journal register and migrate existing recurring journals into the register.
4. Train finance staff on narrative standards, coding rules and pre-posting checks.
5. Deploy sample audit pack and schedule quarterly sample review by Controller.
22. Troubleshooting & common incidents
• Missing attachments after posting: Raise an exception; perform corrective journal if necessary and attach missing documents; record reason and remedial action.
• Journal posted without approval: Reverse (if appropriate) and re-post with required approvals; run an access and SoD review; log incident.
• Recurring journal failure: Stop subsequent runs until owner reviews variance; investigate cause and document corrective action.
• 2031 outstanding items: Contact originator; if no response after two business emails escalate to regional office; use Rejection via 2030 only after validation.
23. Governance & escalation
Escalation matrix (example):
• Day-to-day exceptions → Preparer/Reviewer.
• Unresolved exceptions >7 days → Controller.
• Material exceptions (>DoA Tier 2 or defined USD/IDR threshold) → Finance Director.
• Suspected fraud → Immediate escalation to Finance Director & Internal Audit; preserve evidence and suspend accounts as necessary.
Reporting: Monthly governance pack to senior management covering manual journal volumes, exceptions, aged control account items and remediation status.

25. FAQ (operational)
Q: Can I post without reviewer if reviewer is on leave? A: No — use delegation or Controller-approved temporary compensating control documented and time-limited.
Q: Are scanned documents acceptable? A: Yes if originals unavailable; annotate reason and retain originals where required by donor/statute.
Q: How long to retain vouchers? A: Minimum statutory/donor requirements (example >=7 years); follow donor-specific rules when longer retention required.
26. Final mandatory compliance note
This SOP is mandatory. Non-compliance (e.g., splitting payments to avoid DoA, posting without approvals, using unapproved codes) will be escalated and may result in remedial actions including reversal, audit adjustments and disciplinary procedures. Controller is responsible for monitoring adherence and reporting material breaches to senior management. For any uncertainty over coding, donor restrictions, multi-currency treatment or materiality, consult the Data Maintenance Manual, Awards/Grants team or Controller before posting.

31. PQ (Procurement / Payment Query) Examples — purpose & usage
Purpose: PQs are standardised queries used by finance or procurement teams to request clarifications, additional documents or corrections from internal requestors, vendors, banks or budget holders. PQs create an auditable trail and accelerate resolution. Use the PQ template matching the query type (Supplier PQ, Payment PQ, Bank PQ, Tax PQ, Inter-office PQ). Always attach PQ to the voucher pack and log PQ ID in the voucher narrative. Standard fields: PQ ID, raised by (name & role), date raised, related voucher ref/transaction id, recipient (vendor/BH/bank), query summary (short), detail (required evidence or correction), deadline (SLA), priority (Low/Normal/High/Critical), attachments requested, response (to be completed by recipient), date closed, resolver name.

42. PQ Handling & Logging Process (operational rules)
1. Logging: All PQs must be logged in the central PQ tracker (Excel/PowerApp) with a unique PQ ID, raised date, preparer, SLA and related voucher ref. Link PQ ID in voucher narrative and attach PQ and responses to voucher pack.
2. SLA & Priority: Standard SLAs: Critical = 24 hours, High = 48 hours, Normal = 5 business days, Low = 10 business days. Adjust per local operations and donor urgency. Document any SLA changes and approvals.
3. Escalation: If recipient does not respond within SLA, escalate to recipient manager, Procurement Manager, Controller, then Finance Director per escalation matrix. Document escalation steps in PQ log.
4. Resolution & Closure: When PQ response received, attach evidence to PQ and voucher, enter closure date and resolver name. For any PQ that causes payment hold, record hold status and release only after verification and required approvals.

44. PQ Best Practices & Controls
• Standardise PQ templates and required attachments to reduce back-and-forth. Use dropdowns for priority and issue type in the PQ tracker.
• For critical PQs (duplicate payments, suspected fraud), require immediate phone confirmation and copy to Internal Audit.
• Retain PQ history for audits; link PQs to voucher metadata and to AP/Bank reconciliation entries.
• Use PQ trend reporting monthly to identify recurring vendor or process issues and implement corrective actions (training, supplier deactivation, enhanced onboarding).
45. PQ Integration with Journal Workflow (how PQs appear in SOP steps)
• During Preparation: If missing evidence identified, preparer raises PQ before submitting voucher for review; voucher status = Pending PQ.
• During Review: Reviewer rejects or raises PQs if supporting documents incomplete or anomalies present; voucher remains in Pending PQ until resolved.
• Before Posting: All high/critical PQs must be resolved and evidence attached before posting. For low/normal PQs, Controller may allow posting with remediation plan and explicit note in voucher pack (only where policy permits).
• After Posting: If PQ reveals post-posting issue (e.g., bank credited different account), raise adjustment PQ and corrective journal with full approvals. Attach PQ resolution to reversal/replacement voucher.
46. PQ Audit Trail Requirements
• Each PQ must be archived with: PQ ID, initiating user, recipient, timestamps (raised & closed), attachments and final response. Maintain PQ log as an auditable register linked to voucher ref and to AP/bank transaction records. Use PQ analytics quarterly to reduce rework and vendor disputes.
