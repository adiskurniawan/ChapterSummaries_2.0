OUCRU Indonesia – End-of-Service Risk and Mitigation Summary
Short summary (thesis)
OUCRU Indonesia’s current practice — hiring staff on one-year fixed-term contracts through Alert Asia (AA), paying end-of-service compensation at contract end and recognising the expense on payment rather than accruing — reduces short-term cash outflows but creates legal, accounting, audit, and grant-compliance risks. Indonesian labour law and accounting standards support employer obligations for termination payments and treat some termination benefits as obligations that may require recognition earlier than cash payment. The arrangement can be defensible if carefully designed, documented, and governed. But it must be stress-tested against three vectors: (A) employment law (conversion/continuous employment and third-party employer risk), (B) accounting and audit standards (timing and disclosure), and (C) grant/donor compliance (allowability, budget control, and auditability). Below are the key analyses and mitigations.

A. Legal risk: employment status, fixed-term contracts and third-party arrangements
• Indonesian law requires termination compensation (uang pesangon, uang penghargaan masa kerja, uang penggantian hak) when employment ends under certain conditions. These rights exist for both indefinite and specified-term workers.
• PKWT (fixed-term) contracts are lawful for specific tasks but subject to strict formal rules. Multiple renewals or continuous renewals may cause reclassification to PKWTT (permanent), triggering back pay and protections.
• If OUCRU effectively controls work and supervision, authorities may treat OUCRU and AA as joint employers. Substantive control outweighs contractual form.
• The Job Creation Law (UU 11/2020) and Government Regulation No. 35/2021 revised benefit calculation rules. Outdated methods risk non-compliance.
Legal takeaway: the one-year PKWT through AA reduces exposure only if (a) the PKWT is objectively justified and not renewed to create permanence, (b) OUCRU’s control remains limited, and (c) AA acts as a genuine independent employer with HR capacity and liability.

B. Accounting and audit risk: timing of recognition and disclosure
• Indonesian PSAK mirrors IAS 19. Termination benefits are recognised when a demonstrable commitment or constructive obligation exists.
• Recognising payments only upon cash settlement is acceptable only when no present obligation exists before contract end. If benefits are routinely paid at each renewal, auditors may require accrual.
• Donor accounting requires costs to be recognised when incurred. Cash-only recognition can create timing mismatches and questioned costs.
Accounting takeaway: the practice is defensible only with written policy, legal and audit support confirming no present obligation. Otherwise auditors may demand accrual or disclosure.

C. Grant, compliance and practical controls
• Donor rules require costs to be reasonable, allocable, and consistent. Large termination payments at project end may be disallowed if unbudgeted.
• Maintain written PKWTs, evidence of AA’s independent HR role, OUCRU-AA agreements defining liability, and a register of renewals and justifications.
• Limit renewals; where continuity is essential, consider permanent employment with full statutory cost budgeting. Ensure AA has financial capacity and insurance for liabilities.

D. Specific recommended actions (high leverage)
1. Obtain legal opinion on structure, control, and exposure under GR 35/2021.
2. Obtain auditor or accounting-advisor opinion on PSAK/IAS 19 recognition requirements.
3. Strengthen PKWT drafting and ensure AA exercises HR control and maintains indemnity coverage.
4. Document accounting policy and, if using cash basis, include clear disclosure and contingent-liability register.
5. Pre-audit grant budgets, forecast termination costs, and document inter-project transfers for auditability.

E. Practical numeric checks
• Produce a renewal history table for each staff member (start date, end date, renewals, role changes).
• Compute statutory termination entitlement under current regulation (pesangon + penghargaan + penggantian hak) to estimate worst-case liability.
• Reconcile payroll, tax, and BPJS filings showing AA as employer. Keep proof of payments and filings.

Conclusion and decision points
Continue the current approach only if: (1) AA is a genuine independent employer; (2) PKWT use is legally justified; (3) legal and audit opinions support cash recognition; and (4) indemnities and reserves are robust.
If any condition fails, move to accrual recognition, strengthen indemnities, or convert to permanent employment with full statutory cost planning.
Immediate next steps: commission legal and accounting opinions and prepare the renewal/liability table for board review.

Model Contract Clauses — OUCRU ↔ AA Service Agreement and AA ↔ Employee PKWT (Refined & Expanded)
Overview (purpose & scope)
Model clauses to reduce reclassification and joint-employer risk while remaining compliant with Indonesian law. Templates only. Must be reviewed and adapted by Indonesian labour-law counsel. Use Bahasa Indonesia as primary language in final contracts. Include schedules: Schedule A (Staff list), Schedule B (Scope & Deliverables), Schedule C (Fees, Notice periods, Insurance & Indemnities), Schedule D (Record & Audit format).

SERVICE AGREEMENT — CORE CLAUSES (OUCRU ↔ AA)
1. Definitions & Parties
1.1 Define Employer, Client, Staff, Supply, Workplace, Project Manager, PKWT, Effective Date.
1.2 State: AA is the sole legal employer of Staff listed in Schedule A. OUCRU is Client engaging AA to supply Staff for specified project work in Schedule B.

2. Express Allocation of Employer Functions (non-exhaustive)
2.1 AA retains exclusive authority over recruitment, offer letters, salary setting, payroll, tax/BPJS filings, performance management decisions affecting employment status, disciplinary measures, hiring/termination decisions, and issuance of employment references.
2.2 OUCRU’s role is limited to specifying technical deliverables, workspace and health & safety conditions at OUCRU sites, and performance monitoring strictly against Schedule B metrics. OUCRU must not instruct or issue HR policies that would alter employment terms.

3. Control, Supervision and Day-to-Day Management (risk minimiser)
3.1 OUCRU may provide task-level instructions through the Project Manager for project deliverables only. OUCRU shall not set hours of employment, make salary adjustments, decide promotions, or issue disciplinary warnings that bind AA. Any such action must be requested in writing to AA for AA’s decision.
3.2 All performance concerns raised by OUCRU must be provided to AA in writing with a proposed remediation plan. AA retains sole authority to implement contractual discipline or termination in accordance with law.

4. AA Employer Warranties & Undertakings
4.1 AA warrants that it will: (a) hire Staff under written PKWTs compliant with GR 35/2021 and other regulations; (b) register PKWTs with the Ministry of Manpower within statutory deadlines; (c) pay wages, taxes and BPJS contributions when due; (d) maintain employment records; (e) obtain and maintain employer liability insurance covering statutory claims; (f) immediately notify OUCRU of any labour inspection or claim relating to Staff supplied to OUCRU.
4.2 AA shall supply evidence of payroll and BPJS payments for Staff on request for audit purposes as set out in Clause 9.

5. OUCRU Covenants & Restrictions
5.1 OUCRU covenants not to: (a) sign or issue employment contracts on behalf of AA; (b) set or change salary/benefit terms; (c) impose disciplinary sanctions framed as employer actions; (d) include Staff in OUCRU internal HR communications as direct employees. 5.2 OUCRU must keep records showing that supervision related only to project deliverables and not employment control.

6. Term, Renewals and Anti-Automatic-Renewal
6.1 Agreement term stated; renewals require execution of a new Service Agreement or signed written amendment specifying new scope and term. No tacit/automatic renewal. 6.2 If OUCRU requests continued supply beyond expiry, parties will document a new scope, funding source and term before continuation of services.

7. Staff Transfer / Direct Hiring Protocol
7.1 OUCRU may propose to employ a Staff directly. Parties must follow a documented transfer protocol: (a) OUCRU gives AA and Employee written notice of intent [Y] weeks in advance; (b) AA and Employee may agree commercial terms for transfer; (c) any transfer will be a new employment contract between OUCRU and Employee; (d) during transition AA remains employer of record and responsible for statutory obligations until transfer effective date. 7.2 Include a cooling-off or notice period to avoid back-to-back renewals being recharacterised as continuous employment (recommended 4–8 weeks).

8. Indemnities, Insurance & Liability Caps
8.1 AA indemnifies OUCRU for liabilities arising from AA’s breaches of employer obligations including unpaid wages, BPJS, taxes, and statutory termination claims. 8.2 OUCRU indemnifies AA to the extent any regulatory or court finding arises because OUCRU exercised employer control inconsistent with this Agreement. 8.3 Require AA to maintain employer liability insurance (amount and minimum cover specified in Schedule C). 8.4 Consider mutual liability caps but carve out gross negligence, willful misconduct and statutory labour liabilities from caps.

9. Records, Audit Rights & Reporting
9.1 AA to keep HR records for at least Z years (recommend 7 years). 9.2 OUCRU has audit rights on reasonable notice to inspect payroll, BPJS, tax filings and PKWT originals to satisfy grant/donor audits. 9.3 Define redaction rules to protect personal data and compliance with privacy law. 9.4 Require periodic reporting (quarterly payroll reconciliation for supplied Staff, proof of contributions, PKWT register).

10. Labour Inspections, Claims Handling & Co-operation
10.1 AA must notify OUCRU in writing within 3 business days of any labour inspection, claim or threatened litigation concerning supplied Staff. 10.2 Parties will cooperate on factual matters but AA retains conduct of defence. Costs allocation for legal defence to follow indemnity provisions.

11. Fees, Payment Mechanism & Withholding
11.1 Fee structure: AA bills OUCRU a supply charge covering gross wages, employer BPJS/tax, service fee and insurance. 11.2 OUCRU may withhold amounts only where AA fails to provide required evidence of statutory payments after written notice and cure period. 11.3 Include invoicing format to support grant audibility and cost allocations by project codes.

12. Termination of Agreement & Continuity
12.1 Either party may terminate with X weeks’ notice. 12.2 For cause termination for material breach, insolvency or unlawful conduct is immediate. 12.3 On termination, AA remains responsible for all employer liabilities for periods when AA was employer of record. Include transition obligations and handover plan for Staff and records.

13. Remedies & Specific Performance
13.1 Include injunctive relief if parties act contrary to employer allocation clauses. 13.2 Clarify that remedies are cumulative.

14. Miscellaneous
14.1 Governing law Indonesia; dispute resolution clause (mediation followed by Industrial Relations Court if labour dispute). 14.2 Entire agreement; severability; assignment restrictions.

FIXED-TERM EMPLOYMENT AGREEMENT (PKWT) — DETAILED CLAUSES (AA ↔ EMPLOYEE)
A. Preamble & Language
A.1 State PKWT is governed by Indonesian law and Bahasa Indonesia version controls. Provide bilingual only as supportive text.

B. Role, Objective & Finite Deliverable
B.1 Clear statement that employment is for a defined project/task with explicit deliverables in Schedule 1. B.2 State objective criteria constituting completion. Avoid vague or open-ended duties.

C. Term, Maximum Aggregate Duration & Extensions
C.1 Fixed start and end dates. C.2 Any extension requires a new written PKWT executed before expiry. C.3 For time-based PKWTs respect GR 35/2021 maximum aggregate duration (e.g., up to five years where law allows). If multiple renewals are used, document distinct objective justification each time.

D. Wages, Allowances & Statutory Benefits
D.1 Explicit monthly wage and pay date. D.2 AA to make statutory BPJS and tax contributions and provide proof on request. D.3 State benefits for termination at law and specify formula or reference statutory calculation method so auditors can reconcile.

E. No Probation Clause
E.1 Do not include a probationary clause that invokes rights inconsistent with a PKWT. If probation is required, counsel must confirm legality for PKWT in the role type.

F. Reporting, Supervision & Employer Control
F.1 Employee reports to OUCRU Project Manager for task supervision only. F.2 All matters regarding salary, performance appraisal affecting employment status, disciplinary action and termination are managed and decided by AA. F.3 Include mechanism for OUCRU to provide written performance feedback to AA. F.4 Specify working hours only in the employment contract if AA sets them; OUCRU may set site access hours but not contractual hours without AA consent.

G. Termination, Automatic Expiry & Compensation
G.1 State that employment terminates automatically at end date without need for termination letter. G.2 If law or contract mandates notice or payment, set out required notice and compensation. G.3 For early termination by AA for operational reasons include compliance with statutory severance rules. G.4 For resignation by Employee specify notice and any lawful recovery of costs if appropriate and lawful. G.5 Define formula for end-of-term payment or reference statutory rules and show sample calculation example in Schedule (for auditor clarity).

H. Confidentiality, IP & Return of Property
H.1 Standard confidentiality clause covering OUCRU and AA information. H.2 Ownership of IP created in project assigned per scope; specify licence back to OUCRU where required by grant terms.

I. Indemnity & Insurance
I.1 Employee indemnifies AA for willful misconduct or gross negligence causing loss. Limit indemnity consistent with labour law (avoid unenforceable penalties). I.2 AA to maintain statutory insurance and employer liability insurance per Service Agreement Schedule C.

J. Dispute Resolution
J.1 Internal grievance first, then mediation. J.2 If unresolved, Industrial Relations Court in Employee workplace jurisdiction.

K. DB & Audit Ready Provisions
K.1 Include a clause allowing AA to provide OUCRU, donors and auditors with necessary employment documents on a confidential basis to support audits. K.2 Include retention schedules and specified formats for payroll and time allocation records.

DRAFTING NOTES, RISK MITIGATIONS & PRACTICAL CONTROLS (IMPLEMENTATION CHECKLIST)
1. Separation of Powers. Implement written SOP describing what constitutes employment control. Train OUCRU managers to avoid actions that may be treated as employer actions (salary decisions, hiring, firing, formal warnings). Keep examples. Use an internal escalation path where HR matters are referred to AA HR.
2. Document Objective Justification. For each PKWT include a 1–2 paragraph commercial justification explaining why role is project/temporary and not core. Save contemporaneous evidence (project charters, funding period).
3. Registration Proof. Save screenshots/PDFs of Ministry PKWT registration and BPJS/tax payment receipts in a per-employee folder.
4. Renewal Protocol. When extension is needed, create a short written extension memo recording changed deliverables, funding source, start/end dates, and justification. Execute a new PKWT rather than amend silently.
5. Aggregate Term Tracking. Maintain a register showing start dates, prior renewals, total aggregate time against GR 35/2021 limits. Flag staff approaching thresholds.
6. Operational Separation Evidence. Keep copies of OUCRU’s written performance feedback memos and AA’s employment actions (warnings, salary adjustments) showing AA made HR decisions.
7. Insurance & Indemnity. Require AA to maintain employer liability insurance with minimum limits. If possible, get AA’s insurer to confirm coverage for claims arising from supply to OUCRU.
8. Accounting & Audit Preparedness. Insert a contractual obligation on AA to supply quarterly payroll reconciliations and end-of-term payment schedules formatted for donor audits. Agree on cost-allocation keys for multi-project staff.
9. Communication Templates. Prepare standard wording for job adverts, offer letters, performance memos and termination letters that emphasise AA as employer. Use AA letterhead for HR documents.
10. Empower HR Oversight. AA should run induction, payroll, tax/BPJS filings and disciplinary processes. AA must document decisions and outcomes in employee HR file.

ADDITIONAL CLAUSE SAMPLES (SHORT, ADAPTABLE WORDING)
Sample anti-joint-employer clause: “The Parties agree that nothing in this Agreement shall constitute or be deemed to create a partnership, joint venture, agency or employment relationship between OUCRU and any Staff. Each Party shall act within its allocated role and shall not purport to exercise the other Party’s employer functions.”

Sample audit cooperation clause: “AA shall, on reasonable notice and at OUCRU’s cost where required by donor terms, provide copies of payroll, BPJS and tax filings and originals of PKWTs for Staff supplied to OUCRU to enable donor audits. Sensitive personal data may be redacted but full financial records must be made available.”

RISK MATRIX (HIGH-LEVERAGE ITEMS & CONTROLS)
• Risk: Recharacterisation as PKWTT or joint employer. Control: Strict role separation, written proof AA made HR decisions, register of justifications, insurance and indemnities.
• Risk: Auditor requires accrual for termination benefits. Control: Obtain written external audit opinion, include sample statutory calculation in PKWT Schedule and maintain contingent liability register.
• Risk: Donor disallowance of end-of-term costs. Control: Pre-audit budgets, cost allocation templates, and timely documentation of staff transfers.

IMPLEMENTATION TIMETABLE (RECOMMENDED FIRST 90 DAYS)
Day 0–14: Legal review and Bahasa translation.
Day 7–30: Update master Service Agreement and PKWT templates. Train OUCRU managers on employer-control boundaries.
Day 14–45: Build PKWT register and import historic renewals.
Day 30–60: Obtain AA insurance certificate and indemnity confirmation.
Day 30–90: Commission legal opinion on reclassification risk and auditor opinion on accrual recognition under PSAK/IAS 19.

DOCUMENTATION & AUDIT PACKAGE (DELIVERABLES TO KEEP)
• Signed Service Agreement and Schedules.
• Employee PKWTs (originals).
• Ministry PKWT registration proofs.
• Payroll register, payslips, BPJS/tax receipts per employee.
• Renewal memos and justifications.
• Insurance certificate and indemnity clause evidence.
• Quarterly reconciliations and donor-ready audit packs.

FINAL REMARKS
These clauses and controls materially reduce legal, accounting and grant risks when correctly implemented together with operational discipline, insurance and external legal and auditor sign-off. Do not rely on form alone. Ensure Bahasa Indonesia primary contracts, contemporaneous project justifications, PKWT registration, robust recordkeeping, and enforceable indemnities and insurance.
