Key Terms & Cross-References
Tawḥīd (Oneness of Allah): core monotheism in three aspects — rubūbiyyah (Lordship), ulūhiyyah (worship), and asmāʾ wa ṣifāt (names & attributes). Tawḥīd is foundational in belief and practice.
Imān (Faith): conviction and affirmation of belief in Allah, angels, scriptures, prophets, the Day of Judgment, and divine decree; involves heart, tongue, and actions.
Iḥsān (Excellence / Spiritual excellence): inner perfection and sincerity in worship and conduct — “to worship Allah as if you see Him.”
Sunnah & Ḥadīth: the Prophet’s ﷺ practice and sayings as primary sources of guidance and law; preservation and authentication through transmission methodology (isnād, matn).
Fiqh (Jurisprudence): human understanding of Sharīʿah rulings derived from the Qur’ān and Sunnah, covering acts of worship, transactions, social, legal, and ethical matters.
Usūl al-Fiqh (Legal Theory): principles governing the derivation of rulings, including Qur’ān, Sunnah, ijmāʿ, qiyās, and ijtihād, with distinctions from taqlīd.
Aqīda (Creed / Theology): foundational beliefs concerning Allah, His attributes, angels, scriptures, prophets, the unseen, the Hereafter, and divine decree (qadar).
Taqwa (God-consciousness): continual awareness of Allah that motivates obedience and restraint from sin; central to moral and spiritual life.
Akhlaq (Ethics / Morality): character and conduct shaped by faith, including virtues such as ṣabr (patience), ṣidq (truthfulness), ʿadl (justice), shukr (gratitude), and humility.
Hikmah (Wisdom): sound judgment arising from correct knowledge and righteous action; applied in belief, teaching, and law.
Death & Afterlife (Barzakh, Qiyāmah, Hisāb): stages from death to resurrection, accountability, and final judgment; moral responsibility and consequences emphasized throughout revelation.
Paradise & Hell: final abodes of reward and punishment, described in terms of both physical and spiritual realities, linked to faith and deeds.
Angels & Jinn: unseen creations with defined roles; angels as obedient servants of Allah, jinn as morally responsible beings alongside humans.
Scriptures & Prophets: divine revelation sent through successive prophets, culminating in the Qur’ān and the finality of prophethood with Muhammad ﷺ.
Manhaj (Method / Way): coherent approach to understanding and practicing Islam, integrating creed, worship, ethics, and law.
Practical Cross-References: link ritual acts (ṣalāh, zakāh, ṣawm, ḥajj) with ethical, social, legal, and spiritual dimensions in the main thematic index.
Recommended Supporting Materials: tafsīr works, canonical ḥadīth collections, classical aqīda texts, and foundational works of uṣūl al-fiqh and akhlaq for structured deep study.
