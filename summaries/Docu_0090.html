1 – WHAT IS SEVERANCE PAY? — Precise definition, types, and legal character

Precise definition: A payment or package of payments and benefits provided to an employee when the employment relationship ends. It is consideration given for termination and sometimes for a release of claims. Severance can be mandatory (statutory), contractual (agreed in contract or policy), or discretionary (employer goodwill).

Types of severance:
- Statutory severance: Required by law for specific termination causes. Legally enforceable minimums often depend on tenure and reason for termination.
- Contractual severance: Terms set in employment contract or collective bargaining agreement. Can exceed statutory minima.
- Discretionary severance: One-off offers, retention bonuses at exit, or enhanced packages to key employees.

Legal character and enforceability: Severance is a debt if promised in contract or statute. Acceptance often conditions on employee signing a release. Releases must be supported by consideration and not obtained by fraud or undue pressure to be valid. Courts may set aside releases that are unconscionable or violate public policy.
2 – OBJECTIVES & RISK MANAGEMENT — Employer and employee perspectives

Employer objectives:
1) Financial buffer for departing employee. 2) Minimize litigation risk through a documented release. 3) Preserve employer reputation. 4) Manage workforce change efficiently. 5) Comply with laws and contracts.

Employee objectives: Cash for transition. Continuity of benefits. Clear documentation of entitlements. Opportunity to negotiate additional support (outplacement, references).

Risk management levers for employer:
- Condition payment on signed releases and non-disparagement.
- Require reasonable review period for employee.
- Keep calculations auditable and documented.
- Follow statutory consultation/notice obligations to avoid claims.
3 – TERMINATION CATEGORIES & TRIGGERS — How reason drives entitlement

Common termination categories:
- Redundancy / economic layoff — typically highest statutory entitlement.
- Employer-initiated without cause — contractual or statutory entitlements apply.
- Dismissal for cause (serious misconduct) — statutory entitlements often reduced or forfeited.
- Mutual separation / negotiated exit — entitlements defined by negotiation.
- Constructive dismissal — employee claims termination by employer breach; often treated as employer-initiated for severance calculation.

Triggering checks: Confirm termination date, documentation of reasons, prior warnings, consultation minutes, union notices. Eligibility depends on continuous service and the statutory definition of termination cause.
4 – COMPONENTS OF A FULL SEVERANCE PACKAGE — Cash and non-cash breakdown

Cash components:
- Primary severance (pesangon/termination pay): Core lump-sum tied to tenure.
- Service appreciation (long-service award): Additional months based on length of service.
- Accrued leave payout: Payment for unused annual leave or compensatory time.
- Pro rata bonus/commission: Portion of incentive earned but unpaid at termination.
- Reimbursement elements: Unpaid expenses, relocation return, repatriation for expatriates.

Non-cash components:
- Benefit continuation: Health coverage for X months.
- Salary continuation: Employer pays salary for a notice or transition period.
- Outplacement services / career coaching.
- Retention of stock/vesting acceleration: Acceleration of equity vesting or extended exercise windows.

Administrative items: Final payslip, tax documentation, signed release, e-signature records.
5 – DETAILED CALCULATION METHODOLOGIES — Formulas, bands, edge cases

Core formulas (generic):
- Severance_primary = Monthly_Base_Salary × Multiplier_by_years_of_service
- Service_award = Monthly_Base_Salary × Service_multiplier
- Unused_leave = Daily_rate × Unused_leave_days where Daily_rate = Monthly_Base_Salary ÷ Working_days_in_month
- Pro_rata_bonus = Annual_bonus × (Months_worked_in_year ÷ 12)

Typical multipliers and bands (industry practice ranges):
- 0–2 years: 0.5–1 month per year.
- 3–5 years: 1–3 months per year.
- 6–10 years: 3–6 months per year.
- 10+ years: escalating bands, or fixed caps.

Edge cases & rounding rules:
- Fractional years: use completed years or prorate by months; choose rule consistently and document.
- Partial-month termination: prorate monthly salary to daily basis.
- Variable pay: average previous 12 months for commissions and bonuses unless contract specifies otherwise.
- Expat pay in foreign currency: convert at spot rate on payment date and document FX method.
6 – INDONESIA DETAILED EXAMPLE (STATUTORY FRAMEWORK SUMMARY)

Note: Illustrative structure. Always verify current statutory tables and regulations.

Statutory components:
- Uang Pesangon — statutory severance portion.
- Uang Penghargaan Masa Kerja — service appreciation.
- Uang Penggantian Hak — compensation for accrued rights.

Calculation:
1) Identify termination reason.
2) Lookup statutory months by tenure.
3) Multiply months by base salary.
4) Sum components.
5) Document statute references.

Example: 7 years service; salary IDR 8,000,000; table: Pesangon 6; Penghargaan 4; Penggantian Hak 1 → Total 11 × 8,000,000 = IDR 88,000,000. Apply tax rules.

Employer levers: Release for moderate cash, disclosure limits, benefit caps, non-compete, staggered payments.

Tactics: Pre-calc offers, present rationale, secure release and payment together.
